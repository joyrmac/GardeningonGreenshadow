# ❓ What Is This Repository?

## The Short Answer

**GardeningonGreenshadow is a complete, detailed growing guide for one specific garden property in Fuquay-Varina, NC.**

It contains everything you need to grow food, flowers, and healing herbs on this land:
- Plans for 11 gardens with exact layouts and plant lists
- A calendar with 50+ planting and maintenance dates
- Inventory of 228 seeds and plants
- How-to guides for every gardening task
- Reference materials for troubleshooting and learning
- Historical archives of planning decisions

**Think of it as:** A gardening operating system for one piece of land. Living documentation that gets better every year.

## The Core Philosophy

> **This yard should get better and wilder every year with less work, not more.**

The gardens are designed to:
- **Self-seed and perpetuate** — Once established, half the plants come back on their own
- **Feed each other** — Pollinators from Wayward Meadow help everywhere; compost from all beds feeds next season
- **Be sustainable** — No synthetic chemicals, native plants, rainwater harvesting
- **Serve multiple purposes** — Beautiful AND productive; witchy AND functional; resilient AND abundant
- **Root in herbology** — Every plant heals, nourishes, or protects

## The Garden Philosophy in One Paragraph

**This garden is designed to produce food, flowers, and medicine while getting easier and more abundant every year. Every plant has a purpose. Native perennials feed pollinators that visit every bed. Herbs are medicines, not decoration. Pumpkins climb, beans climb, squash shades soil. Spent plants decompose in winter to build soil. Seed heads dry on plants so things reseed themselves. The frog pond catches rain for irrigation. Compost from all beds feeds next season. By year 3, half the garden plants itself. This isn't decorative gardening—it's a self-improving, self-seeding ecosystem that costs less work every year while producing more food, more beauty, and more healing.**
