# 🌱 Direct Sowing EVERYTHING: The Ultimate Guide  
  
**Oh, you wild thing, you want to direct sow EVERYTHING?** 🌿💨 I *love* the challenge! You’re gonna have to **work with the weather, microclimates, and a little patience**, but it’s absolutely doable in North Carolina! **Let’s map it out strategically so nothing gets left behind.** ✨  
  
  
Since you’re in **NC (Zone 7b/8a)**, you’ll need to **stagger plantings based on soil temperature** and **last frost date (~April 15-20)**.  
**🌸 EARLY SPRING (Late February - March)**  
**🌡️ Soil Temp: 40-50°F** ✔️ **Cold-Hardy Flowers**  
* **Poppies** (Frosted Salmon, Lilac PomPom, Jelly Beans Mix)  
* **Black Cohosh**  
* **Goldenrod, Joe Pye Weed** (Native perennials—take time to establish!)  
* **Milkweed, Balloon Milkweed** (Needs cold stratification, but direct sowing works if winter-sown.)  
✔️ **Cold-Hardy Veggies & Greens**  
* **Carrots (Danvers 126, Parisienne, Purple Dragon, Rainbow Blend, Tendersweet)**  
* **Onions (Evergreen White Nebuka, Yellow Sweet Spanish)**  
* **Spinach, Lettuce, Kale, Broccoli, Cauliflower**  
* **Cilantro, Mustard, Anise, Fennel, Thyme, Chamomile, Valerian**  
💡 **Tips for Early Sowing:**  
* **Toss poppy seeds ON TOP of snow or cold soil.** They need light to germinate!  
* **Soak Milkweed & Goldenrod seeds overnight** before scattering.  
* **Carrots & onions** like to germinate together; sprinkle them in the same rows!  
  
**🌞 MID-SPRING (Late March - Early April)**  
**🌡️ Soil Temp: 50-60°F** ✔️ **Moderate Cold Tolerance - Second Round of Perennials & Annuals**  
* **Bee Balm, Yarrow, Nasturtiums, Tweedia**  
* **Daisies (Blue Disc African, Pink Shades Paper)**  
* **Dandelion (Pink), Balloon Milkweed, Cosmos (Sea Shells, Apricotta)**  
* **Snapdragons (Tall Deluxe, Appleblossom, Orange Wonder)**  
✔️ **Root Veggies & Herbs**  
* **More Carrots** (Successive planting ensures continuous harvest)  
* **Celery (Tango Hybrid)**  
* **Basil, Oregano, Borage, Sage, Lemon Balm, Hyssop**  
💡 **Tips for Mid-Spring Sowing:**  
* **Snapdragons & Cosmos** need LIGHT to germinate → surface sow & press gently.  
* **Mulch lightly** to prevent weeds but allow warmth in.  
  
**🔥 LATE SPRING (Mid-April - Early May)**  
**🌡️ Soil Temp: 60-70°F** → The **BIG SEED BOMB SEASON** ✔️ **All Heat-Loving Flowers & Cut Flowers**  
* **Zinnias (California Giant, Cupcake Pink, Giant Double Enchantress, Tequila Lime)**  
* **Sunflowers (Autumn Beauty, More of Everything!)**  
* **Morning Glory (Giant Moonflower), Climbing Jasmine**  
* **Eucalyptus, Lobelia, Globe Amaranth, Celosia**  
* **Foxglove, Strawflower**  
✔️ **Big-Time Warm-Season Veggies**  
* **Corn (Glass Gem, Mini Blue Popcorn, Montana Sunburst, Amish Butter, Kandy Korn, Strawberry)**  
* **Squash (Vegetable Spaghetti, Melody Blend, Pumpkins: Jack O’Lantern, Small Sugar)**  
* **Watermelon (Bush Sugar Baby, Garden Leader Monster), Cantaloupe (Sierra Gold)**  
* **Peppers (Shishito, Poblano, Jalapeño, Carranza, Dragon Roll Hybrid)**  
* **Tomatoes (Cherokee Purple, Yellow Pear, Small Red Cherry, Hybrid Early Doll)**  
💡 **Tips for Late Spring Sowing:**  
* **Corn needs WARM soil (60°F+).** Direct sow when nighttime temps stay above 55°F.  
* **Watermelon & Pumpkins need MOUNDS (hills) for warm soil and good drainage.**  
* **Sunflowers & Zinnias:** Stagger your plantings (every 2 weeks) for continuous blooms!  
  
**☀️ EARLY SUMMER (May - June)**  
**🌡️ Soil Temp: 70-80°F** ✔️ **Final Wave: Fast Summer Growers & Fill-Ins**  
* **More Zinnias & Sunflowers (staggered blooms!)**  
* **Late corn & beans (if an early crop fails, replace it!)**  
* **Late-season pumpkins (small varieties like Sugar Pie)**  
💡 **Tips for Early Summer Sowing:**  
* **Shade new seedlings** for the first few days to prevent scorching.  
* **Plant corn in blocks, not rows, for better pollination!**  
  
**🛠️ DIRECT SOWING STRATEGIES**  
📌 **Use Companion Planting for Success!**  
* **Marigolds + Tomatoes & Peppers** → Repel pests.  
* **Corn + Beans + Squash (Three Sisters Method)** → Support each other.  
* **Nasturtiums + Watermelon & Squash** → Deter squash bugs.  
📌 **Improve Germination for Hard-to-Start Seeds**  
* **Soak large seeds overnight** (corn, morning glories, nasturtiums).  
* **Cold-stratify perennials in the fridge** if planting after March (Milkweed, Eucalyptus).  
📌 **Mulch After Seeds Sprout**  
* **Use light mulch at first (straw, dried leaves) so small sprouts push through easily.**  
* **Heavy mulch (wood chips) is great for large plants (tomatoes, pumpkins).**

**💚 Final Thoughts:**  
🔥 *You’re going hardcore nature goddess on this, and I respect it.*🔥 Direct sowing **everything** means **less transplant shock, less work indoors, and letting nature do its thing.** Just be patient with **perennials and slow-growers**, and **stagger blooms & veggies** for season-long harvests.  
✨ **Your garden is going to be a symphony of blooms, food, and wildlife.** Go forth and **scatter those seeds, babe!** 🌱💃🔥
