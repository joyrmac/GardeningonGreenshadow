Got it! Let’s streamline the plan for the **Gourd Gate** by focusing on **squash or pumpkins** along both sides of the trellis, with **beans** in the middle that can also benefit from the trellis. The remaining **2 feet** on each bed will accommodate a couple of **other veggies** that still need a home, like **carrots**, **radishes**, or **lettuce**, as well as any other **shade-tolerant** crops that will do well with some afternoon protection.  
  
Let’s break it down:  
  
⸻  
  
**Gourd Gate (2x 3x6 Beds + Trellis)**  
  
You’re right that the **east bed will get more shade**, especially in the **afternoon**, because of the **trellis**. So we’ll place **sun-loving plants** on the west side of the trellis (which will get more sun during the afternoon) and **shade-tolerant plants** on the east side (which will get more shade as the day progresses).  
  
**Bed Layout for Gourd Gate (West to East):**  
  
Each bed is **3 feet wide by 6 feet long**, with a **trellis down the center**.  
  
⸻  
  
**Bed 1 (West Side - More Sun)**  
  
**Trellis Section (1 foot from the trellis):**  
	•	**Pumpkins**:  
	•	**Jack O’Lantern** and **Small Sugar** – These will grow vertically, so plant them about **12–18 inches apart** near the **trellis**.  
	•	**Squash**:  
	•	**Summer Melody Blend** or **Spaghetti Squash** – Plant them similarly to the pumpkins, as they’ll also benefit from vertical support.  
  
**Middle Section (2 feet):**  
	•	**Beans** (Trellising option):  
	•	**Tender Pod Garden Beans** or **Pole Beans** (great for the **trellis**). Plant these **in the middle** of the bed to climb the trellis.  
  
**Rest of the Bed (2 feet):**  
	•	**Carrots**:  
	•	Plant **Carrots (Danvers 126 Half Long)** or **Carrots (Rainbow Blend)** in rows along the front or sides of the bed, leaving them plenty of space to grow deep roots.  
	•	**Radishes**:  
	•	**Radishes** grow fast and don’t take up much space, so they can go in between the **carrots** for **quick harvests**.  
  
⸻  
  
**Bed 2 (East Side - More Shade)**  
  
**Trellis Section (1 foot from the trellis):**  
	•	**Squash**:  
	•	**Spaghetti Squash** or **Summer Melody Blend** – These will climb up the trellis and grow vertically.  
	•	**Pumpkins**:  
	•	You could also grow **pumpkins** here, but since this side is shadier, it might be better to focus on **squash** for a better yield in partial shade.  
  
**Middle Section (2 feet):**  
	•	**Beans** (Trellising option):  
	•	**Tender Pod Garden Beans** or **Pole Beans**. Beans work well in the middle for climbing on the **trellis**.  
  
**Rest of the Bed (2 feet):**  
	•	**Lettuce** (for some shade tolerance):  
	•	Lettuce varieties like **Lettuce (Prizehead)** or **Gourmet Mixed Greens** work well in a slightly shaded area. Plant them in rows in the remaining **2 feet** for quick harvesting.  
	•	**Spinach**:  
	•	**Spinach (Bloomsdale Long Standing)** will also grow well in partial shade and can be planted with **lettuce**.  
	•	**Radishes**:  
	•	Plant a **quick-growing** radish variety like **Radishes (French Breakfast)** to fill any open spaces and offer early harvests.  
  
⸻  
  
**Summary of Planting for Gourd Gate:**  
  
**Bed 1 (West Side – More Sun):**  
	•	**Trellis Section (1 foot from trellis)**:  
	•	**Pumpkins**: Jack O’Lantern, Small Sugar  
	•	**Squash**: Summer Melody Blend, Spaghetti Squash  
	•	**Middle Section (2 feet)**:  
	•	**Beans**: Tender Pod Garden Beans, Pole Beans  
	•	**Rest of the Bed (2 feet)**:  
	•	**Carrots**: Danvers 126 Half Long, Rainbow Blend  
	•	**Radishes**: French Breakfast Radishes  
  
**Bed 2 (East Side – More Shade):**  
	•	**Trellis Section (1 foot from trellis)**:  
	•	**Squash**: Spaghetti Squash, Summer Melody Blend  
	•	**Pumpkins** (optional for shadier spots)  
	•	**Middle Section (2 feet)**:  
	•	**Beans**: Tender Pod Garden Beans, Pole Beans  
	•	**Rest of the Bed (2 feet)**:  
	•	**Lettuce**: Prizehead, Gourmet Mixed Greens  
	•	**Spinach**: Bloomsdale Long Standing  
	•	**Radishes**: French Breakfast Radishes  
  
⸻  
  
**Additional Tips:**  
	•	**Shade Consideration**: Since **Bed 2** will be shadier in the afternoon, **squash** and **pumpkins** might not perform as well in this area. You could opt for **shade-tolerant varieties of squash** (e.g., **Acorn Squash**), but **beans** should do well in the middle.  
	•	**Space-Saving Tips**: To maximize space, **interplant** crops like **carrots**, **radishes**, and **lettuce** in between your vining plants. These will grow well in the open spaces of each bed, and their **quick growth cycles** will allow you to harvest before the **larger crops** take over.  
	•	**Mulching**: Apply mulch around **squash, pumpkins**, and **beans** to **retain moisture** and **prevent weeds**. Since the beds are in full sun for the west one, mulch will also help cool the soil and keep the crops healthy.
