# 📷 “==A Year in the Garden==” – Monthly Photography Guide 🌿✨  
  
This list gives you **specific photo ideas for each month** + **situations to capture**, so you can fully document your garden’s transformation **throughout the seasons** with your Fujifilm camera.  
  
🌱** JANUARY: The Garden at Rest**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	Bare trees, frost-covered soil, and dormant plants.  
	•	Close-ups of **frozen water droplets** or **ice crystals on leaves**.  
	•	First **seed packets & gardening plans** (flat lays of your tools & journal).  
	•	Moody, overcast lighting for a **peaceful winter aesthetic**.  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
☀️ **Golden hour light** on frosted surfaces.  
🌫️ **Early morning fog** for an ethereal look.  
🖐️ **Your hands holding seeds or warming near a mug of tea** (gardener’s anticipation).  
  
🌿** FEBRUARY: Signs of Life Begin**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**First buds or bulbs pushing through the soil.**  
	•	Morning **dew on leaves** or frost on garden beds.  
	•	Seedlings growing indoors (place them near a window for soft light).  
	•	Bare branches against bright blue winter skies.  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
🌧️ **Raindrops on petals or windows**.  
☀️ **Contrast of sunlight hitting frozen or wet leaves**.  
🎨 **Monochrome challenge:** Try a few black-and-white shots for winter drama.  
  
🌷** MARCH: First Blooms & Prepping the Soil**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Crocuses, daffodils, or tulips starting to bloom**.  
	•	The **texture of soil being tilled & prepared**.  
	•	Bees & insects returning to the garden.  
	•	**Macro shots** of budding leaves & early flowers.  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
💦 **After rain:** Capture water droplets on petals.  
🌅 **Sunrise or sunset garden shadows**.  
✋ **Hands planting or watering the first crops**.  
  
🌸** APRIL: Spring Explosion**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Blossoms in full bloom** (cherry blossoms, azaleas, etc.).  
	•	**Vibrant green leaves unfurling**.  
	•	**Pollen-covered bees and butterflies** visiting flowers.  
	•	**Worms or insects in soil** (life beneath the surface!).  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
🌪️ **Wind blowing through flowers** (motion blur for drama).  
🌞 **Backlighting:** Sun shining **through flower petals**.  
🐝 **Action shots of bees landing on flowers**.  
  
☀️** MAY: Growth Everywhere**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Lush greenery & thickening stems.**  
	•	**Small veggies starting to form** (baby tomatoes, peppers, etc.).  
	•	Climbing vines wrapping around trellises.  
	•	**Dew or mist on large leaves in the morning.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
🕶️ **Harsh midday light challenge** – capturing contrast.  
👣 **Your footprints in damp soil after watering**.  
🌱 **Macro close-ups of tiny new growth.**  
  
🌞** JUNE: The Garden in Full Swing**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**First major harvests** – berries, herbs, early summer crops.  
	•	**Lush, tangled vines & wildflowers.**  
	•	**Insects in action** – butterflies, ladybugs, caterpillars.  
	•	**Shadows of plants cast against fences or walls.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
💨 **Movement:** Wind through tall grass or plants swaying.  
📦 **Hands holding freshly harvested vegetables.**  
🔥 **Late-evening golden hour glow over the garden.**  
  
🌻** JULY: Peak Summer Growth**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Tomatoes ripening on the vine**.  
	•	**Sunflowers at their tallest**.  
	•	**Wild overgrown areas of the garden** (organized chaos).  
	•	**Butterflies & hummingbirds in action**.  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
☀️ **High-contrast summer light & shadows.**  
🚿 **Sprinkler or watering can creating water droplets in the air.**  
🍉 **Food from the garden being prepped for dinner (storytelling shot).**  
  
🍂** AUGUST: The Garden Feeds You**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Baskets of fresh-picked produce.**  
	•	**Wilting or drying plants** (the cycle of life).  
	•	**Flowers starting to go to seed.**  
	•	**Garden visitors – pets, birds, or kids helping.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
🍽️ **Plated meals using garden ingredients.**  
🌅 **Cool, soft sunrise light.**  
🖐️ **Hands reaching for ripe veggies on the vine.**  
  
🍁** SEPTEMBER: Change in the Air**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**First fallen leaves** mixed with green plants.  
	•	**Late-season harvests** (squash, pumpkins, apples).  
	•	**Faded summer flowers next to fresh fall growth.**  
	•	**Cooler morning fog rolling through the garden.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
☕ **Cozy autumn aesthetic – hands holding a warm drink in the garden.**  
📏 **Before & after comparisons (garden at peak vs. now).**  
🌬️ **Wind sweeping dried leaves across the ground.**  
  
🎃** OCTOBER: Autumn Glory**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Pumpkins & gourds on the vine.**  
	•	**Bright autumn leaves framing the garden.**  
	•	**Sunflowers or plants fully dried & ready to be saved for seeds.**  
	•	**Halloween & seasonal decorations in the garden.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
🕸️ **Spiderwebs in morning dew.**  
🍂 **Leaves piled up around garden beds.**  
🔥 **Golden evening light catching autumn colors.**  
  
🍂** NOVEMBER: The Garden Slows Down**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Last flowers holding on before winter.**  
	•	**Bare trellises & emptied-out beds.**  
	•	**Frost-covered leaves & plants.**  
	•	**Reflections of bare trees in puddles after rain.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
📜 **Write a note about this year’s garden & photograph it with dried leaves.**  
🌬️ **Breath in cold air during an early morning shot.**  
🌨️ **First frost settling in on plants.**  
  
❄️** DECEMBER: The Sleeping Garden**  
  
📸 **What to Capture:**  
	•	**Snow-covered plants (if applicable).**  
	•	**Icicles hanging from trellises.**  
	•	**Birds or animals visiting the quiet garden.**  
	•	**Your boots in the snow or mud while checking on the garden.**  
  
⏳ **Situations to Try:**  
🔥 **Garden tools stored away for winter.**  
📸 **Take one last full wide shot of the whole garden to compare next year!**  
  
🎯** HOW TO USE THIS LIST**  
  
✔ **Choose 2-3 ideas per month.**  
✔ **Experiment with different angles & lighting.**  
✔ **Look for unexpected beauty, even in decay.**  
✔ **By the end of the year, you’ll have a full seasonal story of your garden.**  
  
💖 **Let me know if you want to refine anything or add another creative challenge!** 🌿✨
