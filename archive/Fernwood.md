* Lady Fern: Carefree lacy fronds up to 30" tall and 12–24" wide create a lush, fluffy form you’ll love. This fully hardy, long-lived fern makes a wonderful addition to shaded borders or woodland spaces, or plant them near a stream or pond for a professional looking landscape display.   
* Cinnamon Fern:  A big, bold, fully hardy fern that forms a large, lacy, circular mound. In the centre of the bright green fronds are tall stems covered in a velvety down of spores. Very dark green when they first emerge, these stems gradually mature to a rich cinnamon brown. Long-lived variety.  
* Maidenhair  Fern:  Ethereal and delicate, this lovely fern earns its namesake with feathery, circular fronds growing outward in flat fans that resemble the plaited coifs of the maid of olden days. These shade-loving plants add light and beauty to wooded gardens, borders or nearby water features.  
	•	Christmas Fern: Resilient and refined, this fern adds sophisticated structure to shaded spots with its symmetrical, fountaining form and deeply serrated, leathery fronds that hold their rich green colour through winter. It thrives in moist, well drained soil but adapts admirably to average garden conditions when given consistent moisture during the growing season.
