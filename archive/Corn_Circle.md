☀️** Concept: Sunburst Corn Field with Three Sisters**  
  
**Layout**: A central circle with radiating “spokes” like sun rays — each spike dedicated to a different **corn variety**. Between the spokes, we’ll plant **beans and squash**.  
  
⸻  
  
✍️** Layout Plan**  
  
**1. Central Hub (the sun’s core)**  
	•	Optional focal: flat stone, sunflower, or squash mound.  
  
**2. Corn Spokes (the sun rays)**  
	•	7 “spokes,” spaced out evenly around a circle.  
	•	Each spoke is a **row of 4–6 corn plants**, all of a single variety.  
	•	Each corn plant spaced about 1 ft apart.  
	•	Total = ~28–42 corn plants.  
  
**3. Beans**  
	•	At the **base of each corn stalk**, plant 1–2 **pole beans** (you’ve got Tender Pod Garden Beans — perfect).  
	•	They’ll climb the corn like lovers in moonlight.  
  
**4. Squash**  
	•	In the spaces **between the spokes**, plant your **Summer Melody Blend** or **Spaghetti Squash**.  
	•	Let them **trail outward like flames** — the squash leaves will suppress weeds and protect the soil.  
  
⸻  
  
🌱** Step-by-Step Planting Guide**  
	1.	**Mark Your Circle**: Use a stick + string to make a 5–7 ft wide circle.  
	2.	**Mark Your Spokes**: Divide into 7 rays (like slicing pizza).  
	3.	**Plant Corn First** (mid to late April): 4–6 kernels per spoke, 1 ft apart.  
	4.	**Once Corn is 6” Tall**, plant:  
	•	**Pole beans** at each corn base.  
	•	**Squash mounds** between each spoke, 2–3 seeds per mound.  
  
⸻  
  
✨** Add a Little Magic**  
	•	Name each spoke if you want: “Gem Ray,” “Butterbeam,” “Strawberry Sun,” etc.  
	•	Mulch heavily with straw or pine needles.  
	•	Scatter calendula or marigolds at the base to honor the sun and bring in pollinators.  
	•	You can even edge it with stones or herbs to complete the circle.
