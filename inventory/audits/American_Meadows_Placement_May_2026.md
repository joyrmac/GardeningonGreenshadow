## Cautions & Watch-Outs

- **Bee balm (#193, #214):** Aggressive runners. Do NOT add to Honeyglow (already overrun). Wayward = OK to naturalize. Front yard = only if you'll keep dividing.
- **Old Field Goldenrod (#190):** *Solidago nemoralis* — well-behaved compared to Canadian goldenrod, but still a spreader. Wayward is best.
- **Northern Sea Oats (#203):** Self-seeds heavily. Do not plant near Bloomveil or Wildwood beds — it'll show up everywhere.
- **Cardinal Flower (#204):** Will die in dry soil. Non-negotiable: wet site.
- **Giant BES (#208):** *Rudbeckia maxima* — flops in rich soil. Plant lean, plant tall, and don't fertilize.
- **Switchgrass cultivar 'Shenandoah Red' (#199, #201):** Shorter than straight species (~3–4 ft instead of 5–6 ft) and red-tipped. Treat as ornamental clumper, not pure prairie filler.
- **Spring planting heat risk:** It's May 1. We have ~3–6 weeks before real summer heat. Get these in the ground in the next 2 weeks if possible — or hold the rest in pots in shade and water daily until fall planting in September.
