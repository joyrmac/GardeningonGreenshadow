## Garden Philosophy

**This yard should get better and wilder every year with less work, not more.**

### Core Principles

**🌱 Let it reseed.** Don't deadhead everything. Poppies, cosmos, zinnias, borage, chamomile, black-eyed susans, coneflowers, bachelor buttons, and dill will all come back on their own if you let the seed heads dry. By year 3, half the garden plants itself.

**🇺🇸 Natives first.** Native perennials (coneflowers, milkweed, bee balm, goldenrod, Joe Pye weed, asters, switchgrass, bluestem) don't need fertilizer, don't need extra water once established, and feed the pollinators that actually live here.

**🚫 No synthetic chemicals.** Companion planting for pest control. Neem oil and insecticidal soap only when needed. Let the beneficial insects do their jobs.

**🧪 Build soil, don't buy it forever.** Compost, cover crops (buckwheat), mulch leaves in fall instead of bagging them, let spent plants decompose over winter.

**💧 Harvest rainwater.** Even one barrel off a downspout cuts watering needs. The frog pond in the herb spiral doubles as irrigation reserve.

**🌿 Herbology is the heart.** Every plant in this yard has a use beyond looking pretty — learn it, label it, harvest it. This isn't decorative gardening. It's a living apothecary.

**🎨 Fun and kitschy.** Giant sunflowers. A gourd gate. Glass gem popcorn. A cat garden. Pumpkin patches. Don't make it precious — make it alive.

### How the Beds Feed Each Other

The property isn't 15 separate gardens — it's one system:

- **Wayward Meadow** raises the pollinators
- **Wildwood** grows the food (benefiting from meadow pollinators)
- **Bloomveil** feeds the soul and attracts more bees
- **Herb Spiral** heals you and controls pests naturally
- **Fernwood** recycles what falls from overhead trees
- **Compost from all beds** feeds the next season
- **Nothing is wasted.**

## Subscribing to the Calendar

To sync your garden schedule to your phone or calendar app:

**Option 1: Direct iCal Subscription**
- Copy this URL: `https://raw.githubusercontent.com/joyrmac/GardeningonGreenshadow/main/gardening/Joy_Garden_2026.ics`
- In Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, or Outlook:
  - **Mac Calendar:** File → New Calendar Subscription → paste URL
  - **Google Calendar:** Add by URL in "Other calendars" settings
  - **Outlook:** File → Open & Export → Import Calendar → paste URL

**Option 2: Download & Import**
- Download the [Joy_Garden_2026.ics](gardening/Joy_Garden_2026.ics) file directly
- Import it into your preferred calendar app
