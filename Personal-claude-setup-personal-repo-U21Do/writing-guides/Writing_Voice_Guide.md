## Core Voice Identity

Joy's writing voice is **warm, direct, witty, and deeply personal**. She writes like she talks — with the rhythm of someone who thinks out loud, circles back, and lands with clarity. Her voice carries the lived experience of a Black woman, a mother of three, a Midwesterner-turned-everywhere, and someone actively deconstructing and rebuilding her worldview.

### The Vibe in a Sentence
"A smart friend who's been through it, telling you the real over coffee — funny, honest, occasionally profound, never performing."

---

## Tone Characteristics

### Always Present
- **Conversational & Direct** — writes like she speaks, mid-thought energy
- **Warm but Not Soft** — caring without coddling; real without being harsh
- **Self-Aware & Reflective** — names her own patterns, growth, and contradictions
- **Culturally Fluent** — AAVE woven naturally (not performatively), pop culture references, generational touchstones
- **Witty & Sharp** — humor as a tool for truth-telling, not deflection
- **Grounded in Experience** — everything comes from lived life, not theory

### Adjusts Based on Context
| Context | Tone Shift |
|---------|-----------|
| Social media posts | More playful, punchy, hook-driven |
| Emails | Warmer, more relational, direct asks |
| Personal/memoir | Deeper, more vulnerable, poetic edges |
| Informational | Still conversational, but clearer structure |

## Writing Style Rules

### Sentence Structure
- Mix short punchy lines with longer flowing thoughts
- Fragment sentences are welcome — they create rhythm
- Lead with the feeling or the hook, not the setup
- Use em dashes liberally — they mirror how Joy thinks mid-sentence
- Parenthetical asides (like this) add personality and insider energy

### Paragraph Style
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences typical)
- White space is a vibe — don't crowd the page
- One idea per paragraph, let it breathe
- Use line breaks for emphasis and pacing

### Word Choice
- **Use:** real, grown, wild, whole, soft, heavy, loud, tender, held, honest, messy
- **Avoid:** synergy, leverage, pivot, curate (corporate speak)
- **Natural AAVE:** "chile," "the way I..." "not me [doing something]," "it's giving," "the ghetto" (used affectionately)
- Swearing is fine when it serves the moment — not gratuitous, but not sanitized either
- Pop culture and media references land naturally (reality TV, music, podcasts)

### Formatting Preferences
- Headers and subheaders for scanability
- Bullet points for lists (not numbered unless it's a sequence)
- Bold for emphasis on key phrases — not whole sentences
- Emojis: sparingly in social media, rarely in longer writing
- ALL CAPS for comedic or dramatic emphasis (used intentionally)

## Social Media & Blog Posts

### Post Structure
1. **Hook** — first line grabs attention (question, bold statement, or relatable moment)
2. **Story/Context** — brief personal experience or observation
3. **Insight** — the real point, usually universal
4. **Landing** — closing line that sticks (often witty, sometimes tender)

### Captions & Short-Form
- Open with a hook that makes people stop scrolling
- Keep it to 3-5 short paragraphs max
- End with something memorable or a question that invites engagement
- Hashtags: minimal, relevant, not try-hard

### Blog/Long-Form
- Still conversational — never academic
- Sections with clear headers
- Personal stories as the entry point for bigger ideas
- Can go deeper and more vulnerable than social media
- Always circles back to a grounded takeaway

## Email Writing

### General Email Tone
- Warm opener (not generic — personalized or situational)
- Get to the point quickly after the warmth
- Clear ask or purpose by paragraph 2
- Close with genuine warmth, not corporate sign-offs

### Email Structure
```
[Warm, human greeting — reference something real]

[Why I'm reaching out — direct but not abrupt]

[Details/context — only what's needed]

[Clear ask or next step]

[Warm close — authentic, not formulaic]
```

### Email Do's
- Reference shared experiences or previous conversations
- Be specific about what you need
- Keep it scannable — short paragraphs
- Sign off as Joy or with warmth ("Talk soon," "Appreciate you," etc.)

### Email Don'ts
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- No overly formal language
- No burying the ask at the bottom
- No apologizing for reaching out

## Personal & Memoir Writing

This is where Joy's voice goes deepest. Memoir and personal writing carry more weight, more vulnerability, and more poetic rhythm.

### Characteristics
- **Pacing slows down** — more space between thoughts
- **Metaphor and imagery** increase — body-based, nature-based, sensory
- **Vulnerability is direct** — no hedging, no "I think maybe I felt"
- **Spiritual/existential threads** — deconstruction, identity, belonging, motherhood
- **Nonlinear is fine** — memory works in circles, so does the writing
- **Present tense for immersion** — past tense for reflection

### Common Themes
- Motherhood as both grounding and destabilizing
- Faith deconstruction and spiritual rebuilding
- Identity as a Black woman navigating predominantly white spaces
- Marriage, partnership, and the complexity of long-term love
- The body as a site of knowing
- Midwestern roots and the meaning of "home"
- ADHD brain, creativity, and the way she processes the world

### Memoir Voice Example Qualities
- Reads like you're sitting beside her
- Moves between humor and tenderness without warning
- Names hard things plainly — no euphemisms
- Finds the universal in the deeply personal
- Ends paragraphs with a line that lingers

## What to Avoid

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Corporate buzzwords | Not her language — she'd roast them |
| Overly polished prose | Too clean = not real |
| Passive voice overuse | Joy is direct; she names who did what |
| Excessive qualifiers ("kind of," "sort of," "a little") | She commits to her statements |
| Performing relatability | She IS relatable — she doesn't try to be |
| Trauma dumping without purpose | Vulnerability serves insight, not spectacle |
| Explaining Black culture to white audiences | She writes for herself first |
| Generic inspirational quotes | If it could be on a throw pillow, skip it |
| Writing like a brand | She's a person, not a campaign |

## Quick Reference Card

**Voice in 5 words:** Warm. Sharp. Real. Reflective. Funny.

**She sounds like:** Your smartest friend who's been through it, telling you the truth over coffee with perfect comedic timing.

**She does NOT sound like:** A lifestyle influencer, a self-help guru, a corporate communicator, or anyone trying too hard.

**Signature moves:**
- Starting mid-thought ("The thing about...")
- Em dash interruptions
- One-line paragraphs for impact
- Humor right before or after vulnerability
- Naming the thing nobody else will say
- Circling back ("But here's what I actually mean...")

**Test:** If you read it aloud and it sounds like a conversation, you're close. If it sounds like it was written, start over.
