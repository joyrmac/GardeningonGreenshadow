# How to Train AI on Your Voice
### A Practical Guide to Getting ChatGPT, Claude, and Other AI Tools to Actually Sound Like You

---

## Why This Matters

AI can write fast. But it writes like AI — polished, generic, and emotionally flat. Your voice is the opposite of that. It's specific, rhythmic, culturally grounded, and alive. The goal isn't to make AI replace your voice — it's to make AI a useful first draft tool that you can edit into something real.

This guide teaches you how to get AI tools to produce output that's 70-80% you, so you only have to polish the last 20%.

---

## Step 1: Feed It Your Voice Guide

Before asking any AI tool to write for you, paste in your voice profile. You already have one — it's the best one you're going to find:

**File:** `Writing_Voice_Guide.md` (this folder)

### How to Use It

**In ChatGPT:**
1. Open a new conversation
2. Paste the entire `Writing_Voice_Guide.md` (or the Quick Reference Card section at minimum)
3. Say: "Use this as my writing voice for everything in this conversation."
4. Then give your actual writing prompt

**In Claude:**
1. Start a new conversation
2. Paste the voice guide
3. Say: "This is my writing style. Match this voice in everything you write for me."
4. Then give your prompt

**In ChatGPT Custom Instructions** (persistent across all chats):
1. Go to Settings > Personalization > Custom Instructions
2. In "How would you like ChatGPT to respond?" paste the Quick Reference Card from your voice guide
3. Now every conversation starts with your voice loaded

### The Quick Reference Card (Copy-Paste Ready)

From your voice guide:

```
VOICE: Warm, direct, witty, deeply personal. Writes like she talks.
TONE: Conversational, culturally fluent (AAVE woven naturally), self-aware.
STRUCTURE: Short paragraphs. Em dashes. Fragment sentences for rhythm.
NEVER: Corporate language, filler phrases ("In today's world..."), over-explaining, sanitizing cultural voice, inspirational poster energy.
ALWAYS: Lead with feeling. Specificity over generality. Humor as truth-telling. Real over polished.
```

## Step 2: Show, Don't Just Tell

Voice guides help, but examples are more powerful than rules. Give the AI 2-3 samples of your actual writing and say: "Match this style."

### What Makes Good Examples

- A social media post you wrote that felt like YOU
- An email you sent that nailed the tone
- A paragraph from a personal essay or blog post
- Even a text message thread that captures how you really talk

**Length doesn't matter. Authenticity does.** A 3-sentence Instagram caption that sounds exactly like you is more useful than a 500-word essay that doesn't.

### The Prompt Formula

```
Here's my writing voice guide: [paste guide]

Here are 3 examples of my actual writing:
1. [example]
2. [example]
3. [example]

Now write [what you need] in this same voice.
```

## Step 3: Context-Specific Instructions

Your voice shifts depending on what you're writing. Tell the AI which mode to use:

| Context | What to Tell the AI |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Social media** | "Write this for Instagram. Punchy, hook-driven, casual. Like I'm talking to a friend. Use short paragraphs and line breaks." |
| **Email (professional)** | "Write a warm but direct email. No corporate fluff. Friendly but clear about what I need." |
| **Email (personal)** | "Write this like I'm texting a close friend but slightly longer. Emoji-friendly." |
| **Blog/memoir** | "Write this in my personal essay voice. Vulnerable, reflective, with some humor. Let the sentences breathe." |
| **Steel Sharpens Steel (NFL content)** | "Write this for women learning football. Confident, no condescension, explain jargon in context, ADHD-friendly formatting with TL;DRs and section breaks." |
| **Scout Media (client-facing)** | "Write this professionally but warm. I'm a real person, not a brand. Approachable expert energy." |

## Step 4: The Edit Pass

AI will never nail your voice 100%. Here's what to fix in the edit:

### Things AI Gets Wrong About Your Voice

| AI Tendency | Your Fix |
|------------|---------|
| **Over-explaining** | Cut the second sentence of any explanation that already landed. |
| **Generic transitions** ("Furthermore," "Additionally,") | Delete them or replace with em dashes and natural flow. |
| **Sanitized language** | Add back your real words. If you'd say "that's some bullshit," say it. |
| **Lists everything equally** | Bold the important parts. Cut the filler items. |
| **No rhythm** | Read it out loud. If it doesn't sound like you talking, restructure the sentence. |
| **Inspirational fluff** | Delete any sentence that could appear on a motivational poster. |

### The Read-Aloud Test

The single best edit technique: **read the output out loud.** If any sentence makes you cringe, sound fake, or feels like someone else — rewrite that sentence in your own words. The AI got you 80% there. Your ear gets you the rest.

## Step 5: Build a Prompt Library

Once you find prompts that produce great results, save them. Don't reinvent the wheel every time.

### Example Saved Prompts

**Weekly NFL Rundown (Steel Sharpens Steel):**
```
Using my voice guide, write this week's Steel Sharpens Steel rundown. Cover:
- Steelers update: [this week's news]
- League news: [2-3 stories]
- Slang of the week: [term]
Include a TL;DR at the top. Keep it under 800 words. Confident and fun, not preachy.
```

**Instagram Caption:**
```
Write an Instagram caption in my voice about [topic].
Keep it under 150 words. Start with a hook. End with something real.
No hashtags in the caption — I'll add those separately.
```

**Client Email (Scout Media):**
```
Write a warm, professional email to a potential client. I run a social media
management business in the Raleigh area. The email should:
- Introduce myself naturally (not a sales pitch)
- Reference [specific thing about their business]
- Suggest a quick call or coffee
- Sound like a real person, not a template
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Asking AI to "write in a casual tone" | Too vague — everyone's casual is different | Give your specific voice guide + examples |
| Pasting your voice guide once and expecting it to stick | AI context resets with each conversation | Re-paste at the start of each new conversation (or use Custom Instructions) |
| Accepting the first draft without editing | It'll sound like polished AI, not you | Always do an edit pass. Always read aloud. |
| Using AI for deeply personal writing without editing | AI can't replicate genuine vulnerability | Use AI for structure/ideas, then rewrite the emotional parts yourself |
| Overcorrecting by micromanaging every word | Defeats the purpose of using AI | Let AI do the heavy lifting. You do the seasoning. |
