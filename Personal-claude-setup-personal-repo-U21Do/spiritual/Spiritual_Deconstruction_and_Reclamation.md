# Spiritual Deconstruction & Reclamation
### A Self-Guided Syllabus for Dismantling What Was Weaponized and Rebuilding What's Real

---

## Who This Is For

This syllabus is for people raised in Christianity — particularly evangelicalism, fundamentalism, or high-control religious environments — who feel that key truths were hidden, distorted, or weaponized, and who are ready to rebuild something authentic. It's structured but flexible. It's rigorous but gentle. It honors your pace, your body, and your emerging inner authority.

---

## How This Works

### Structure
- **4 Phases, 16 Weeks** (plus a preparation week and 4 bonus modules)
- **Phase 1:** Stabilization & Awareness (Weeks 1-4) — ground yourself, name the harm
- **Phase 2:** Deepening Understanding (Weeks 5-8) — examine power, reclaim the feminine
- **Phase 3:** Mystical Awakening (Weeks 9-12) — discover contemplative tradition, expand your God-image
- **Phase 4:** Reconstruction & Integration (Weeks 13-16) — build your personal theology, design your practice
- **Bonus Modules** (self-paced, do anytime after Phase 2): Psychedelics, Earth as Sacred, Reclaiming Ritual, Navigating Relationships

### Pacing
- **Suggested:** 1 phase every 4 weeks (16 weeks total)
- **Reality:** Go slower. Repeat weeks. Take breaks. This is not a race.
- **Daily commitment:** 30-60 minutes (reading + practice + journaling)
- **Weekly Sabbath:** One day per week where you do NO deconstructive reading or processing. Just rest, create, enjoy.

### What You Need
- A private journal (physical or digital) dedicated to this journey
- A quiet, safe space for reading and reflection
- 2-3 trusted people who can support you (friends, therapist, spiritual director)
- A simple daily grounding practice (see Phase 0)

## Phase 0: Foundation (Before You Begin)

### Create Your Sacred Container

Before you start pulling things apart, build a container strong enough to hold what comes up.

**Physical space:**
- Designate a spot for this work — a chair, a corner, a room
- Set up a simple altar or meaningful space (see [How to Create a Ritual](guides/How_to_Create_a_Ritual.md) for altar setup)
- Keep your journal, a candle, and a glass of water there

**Emotional support:**
- Identify 2-3 people you trust to hold space (not fix, not judge — hold)
- Consider finding a therapist experienced with religious trauma (Psychology Today's directory lets you filter for this)
- If you don't have a therapist, at minimum have the crisis resources at the bottom of this document saved in your phone

**Protective boundaries:**
- Decide who you WILL and WON'T discuss this journey with
- Prepare simple responses for intrusive questions:
  - *"I'm exploring some spiritual questions right now."*
  - *"I'm taking time to reflect on my faith."*
  - *"I appreciate your concern, but I need to work through this on my own terms."*
- Set limits on religious content consumption (social media, family group chats, church invitations)
- Give yourself permission to skip religious holidays, decline invitations, or leave conversations that feel unsafe

**Establish a daily grounding practice (start this NOW, before Week 1):**
- Morning: 5 minutes of conscious breathing (inhale for 4, hold for 4, exhale for 6)
- Evening: Body scan (lie down, notice tension from head to toes, breathe into what you find)
- Use the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique when overwhelmed (see [Grimoire — Grounding Techniques](Grimoire_Book_of_Shadows.md))

---

> **Before you begin:** This work can surface intense emotions, memories, and body responses. That's expected — but you should never go through it alone. Read the [Warning Signs & Self-Care Protocols](#warning-signs--self-care-protocols) section now and save the [Crisis Resources](#crisis-resources) in your phone before starting Week 1.

**Daily Practice:**

| Time | Practice |
|------|----------|
| Morning | 10-15 minutes of **Centering Prayer**: Choose a sacred word (Light, Mercy, Here, Love, Peace). Sit in silence. When thoughts arise, gently return to your word. That's it. |
| Throughout day | **Sacred pauses** — 3 times per day, stop for 30 seconds. Feel your feet. Take one breath. Notice where you are. |
| Evening | Journal on experiences of presence, peace, or connection — even fleeting ones |

**How to Practice Centering Prayer:**
1. Choose a sacred word as the symbol of your intention to consent to God's/the Divine's/Love's presence and action within. (Word suggestions: God, Love, Light, Mercy, Here, Peace, Abba, Stillness)
2. Sitting comfortably with eyes closed, introduce the sacred word silently.
3. When you notice you're engaged with thoughts, return ever so gently to the sacred word.
4. At the end of 20 minutes (start with 10), remain in silence with eyes closed for a couple of minutes.

*(Full method guide: [Contemplative Outreach](https://www.contemplativeoutreach.org/centering-prayer-method/))*

**How to Practice Lectio Divina (Sacred Reading):**
1. **Lectio (Read):** Read a short passage slowly. Any sacred or meaningful text — a poem, a psalm, a paragraph from your current book.
2. **Meditatio (Reflect):** Read it again. What word or phrase stands out? Sit with it.
3. **Oratio (Respond):** What rises in you? Speak, write, or feel your response.
4. **Contemplatio (Rest):** Let go of words entirely. Just be.

**Journal Prompts:**

- What happens when I stop talking to God and just listen?
- How does silent prayer differ from the prayer I learned growing up?
- Stillness meditation with the mantra "I am not my thoughts" — what came up?

**Weekly Check-In:**
- [ ] Did I practice centering prayer daily? What happened when I sat in silence?

**Exercise: Write Your Personal Creed**

This is not a statement of beliefs. It's a statement of what you've experienced, what you value, and what you're choosing. Write it in your own voice. Revise it as often as you want. It doesn't need to be finished — ever.

Template to start:
```
I believe _________________________________ because I have experienced it.
I no longer believe ________________________ because it did not give life.
I value ___________________________________.
I am learning to ___________________________.
I trust ___________________________________.
I don't know about _________________________, and that's okay.
The divine feels like _______________________ to me.
My spiritual practice looks like _______________.
I am becoming _____________________________.
```

## Daily Structure & Rhythms (Reference)

### Morning Ritual (10-15 minutes)
1. **Ground:** 5 minutes of breathwork or body awareness
2. **Intention:** Read your current personal creed, values statement, or a passage from your current book
3. **Practice:** Your chosen spiritual practice — centering prayer, meditation, movement, journaling, or card pull

### Evening Reflection (15-20 minutes)
1. **Integrate:** Journal on the day's reading, insights, or emotional shifts
2. **Body check:** Where am I holding tension? What am I carrying that isn't mine?
3. **Gratitude:** Name 2-3 things you're grateful for in your journey — including hard things that taught you something

### Weekly Sabbath (2-3 hours, one day per week)
- Take a COMPLETE break from deconstructive reading, processing, or thinking
- Engage in pure joy: nature, creativity, friendship, cooking, music, rest
- Let your soul breathe without analyzing
- This is not optional. Deconstruction without rest becomes self-destruction.

## Warning Signs & Self-Care Protocols

### Red Flags — Pause and Seek Professional Support

If you experience any of the following, STOP the syllabus and reach out for help:

- Persistent insomnia or nightmares
- Panic attacks or severe anxiety
- Complete loss of meaning or hope
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm impulses
- Inability to function in daily life (work, eating, hygiene)
- Excessive isolation or withdrawal from everyone
- Increased substance use to cope

**This work opens deep wounds. That is expected. What is NOT expected is drowning in them alone.**

### When Overwhelmed (The Reset Protocol)

1. **Stop reading.** Take a 2-3 day break from all syllabus material.
2. **Return to your body.** Bath, walk, gentle movement, warm food, sleep.
3. **Connect with support.** Call a trusted friend, your therapist, or a helpline.
4. **Practice grounding.** 5-4-3-2-1 technique: 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
5. **Remind yourself:** *"This is temporary. I am safe. I am not alone. I am not broken — I am healing."*

## Remember Always

This journey is not linear. You will have breakthrough days and setback days. Both are part of the process.

**Your pace is perfect.** If you need to slow down, repeat a week, or take breaks, honor that wisdom. There is no deadline for healing.

**You are not alone.** Millions of people have walked this path. Your story matters and your healing is possible.

**Trust your inner knowing.** You have everything you need within you to discern truth from manipulation, love from control, and authentic spirituality from performative religion.

**Your body is wise.** When something feels heavy, constraining, or fear-based, listen. When something feels expansive, peaceful, or life-giving, lean in.

**You are already whole.** This journey is not about becoming something new — it's about remembering who you've always been beneath the layers of others' expectations and fears.

*"The cave you fear to enter holds the treasure you seek."* — Joseph Campbell

Begin when you feel ready. Trust the process. Honor your pace. You've got this.
