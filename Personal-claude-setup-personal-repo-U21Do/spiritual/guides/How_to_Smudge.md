# How to Smudge (Smoke Cleanse)
### A Practical Guide to Clearing Energy with Smoke

---

## A Note on Language and Respect

"Smudging" is a sacred ceremony practiced by Indigenous peoples across North America. If you are non-Indigenous, the more respectful term for your practice is **smoke cleansing** — the general practice of burning herbs for purification, which exists across many world cultures (European, African, Asian, and more). This guide uses both terms so you can find the language that fits your practice and heritage.

Traditional smudging was illegal in the US until the American Indian Religious Freedom Act of 1978. White sage in particular has been over-harvested due to commercial demand. Consider growing your own herbs or sourcing from Indigenous growers directly if you choose to use white sage.

---

## Why Smoke Cleanse?

People have burned herbs and resins to clear energy for thousands of years across every continent. Common reasons include:

- **Clearing stagnant or heavy energy** from a room, home, or object
- **After conflict, illness, or stressful events** to reset a space
- **Before meditation, ritual, or spiritual practice** to prepare sacred space
- **When moving into a new home** or starting a new chapter
- **Seasonal clearing** — many people cleanse at the change of each season
- **After having guests or visitors** to reset your home's energy
- **When something just feels "off"** — trust your intuition

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Dried herb bundle or loose herbs** | The smoke source (see herb guide below) |
| **Fireproof dish** | To catch ash and hold the bundle — abalone shell, ceramic bowl, soapstone, or clay dish |
| **Matches or lighter** | A candle works well for relighting |

| **Cup of water or sand** | Safety — for extinguishing |

## Step-by-Step: How to Smoke Cleanse

### Preparation

1. **Set your intention.** Before you light anything, get clear on WHY you're doing this. What are you clearing? What are you inviting in? Your intention is the most important part of this practice. Say it out loud or hold it silently in your heart.

2. **Open windows and doors slightly.** The smoke (and the energy it carries) needs somewhere to go. Even cracking a window in each room helps.

3. **Tidy your space first.** Physical clutter holds stagnant energy. You don't need to deep clean, but clear surfaces and pick up what you can.

4. **Gather your materials.** Have your herb bundle, fireproof dish, lighter, and water or sand ready.

### Lighting

5. **Hold your herb bundle at a 45-degree angle** and light the tip with a match or candle.

6. **Let it burn for 15-20 seconds** until you see a steady flame.

7. **Gently blow out the flame.** You want glowing embers and smoke — not a fire. If the bundle won't stay lit, it may need to be drier, or you may need to gently blow on the embers to encourage them.

### Cleansing Yourself First

8. **Start with yourself.** Hold the bundle over your fireproof dish and use your free hand (or a feather) to waft smoke over your body:
   - Start at your feet and move upward
   - Move the smoke around your head
   - Down your arms and over your hands
   - Down your back if you can reach (or turn slowly)
   - Breathe naturally — don't inhale the smoke directly

9. **State your intention** as you cleanse yourself. Something like: *"I release what no longer serves me. I welcome clarity, peace, and protection."*

### Cleansing Your Space

10. **Start at your front door.** This is where energy enters your home.

11. **Move clockwise through each room.** Walking clockwise (to the left when you enter a room) is traditional for inviting positive energy. Some traditions go counterclockwise for banishing — use what feels right to you.

12. **Pay special attention to:**
    - **Corners** — energy collects and stagnates in corners
    - **Closets and behind doors** — hidden spaces hold hidden energy
    - **Mirrors** — they absorb and reflect energy
    - **Windows and doorways** — entry points for energy
    - **Anywhere that feels heavy or "stuck"**

13. **Waft smoke from floor to ceiling** in each area. Use your hand or a feather to guide the smoke.

14. **Speak your intention** as you move through each room. You can repeat the same phrase or speak specifically to each space: *"This kitchen is filled with nourishment and love"* or *"This bedroom holds only rest and peace."*

15. **End where you started** — back at the front door.

### Closing

16. **Extinguish your bundle safely.** Press the burning end firmly into sand, soil, or your fireproof dish until the embers are fully out. Let it cool completely. **Do not use water** — it damages the bundle and makes it harder to relight next time.

17. **Take a moment of gratitude.** Thank the herbs, thank your space, thank yourself for taking the time.

18. **Leave windows open for 15-20 minutes** after cleansing to let the smoke fully clear.

## How Often to Smoke Cleanse

There are no hard rules. Here are some guidelines:

| Frequency | Good For |
|-----------|----------|
| **Daily** | If you're an empath, healer, or doing intensive spiritual work |
| **Weekly** | General maintenance and keeping energy fresh |
| **Monthly** | Aligned with moon cycles — New Moon (fresh start) or Full Moon (release) |
| **Seasonally** | At the change of each season or solstice/equinox |
| **As needed** | After arguments, illness, stressful events, visitors, or whenever it feels right |

## Safety

- **Never leave a burning bundle unattended.**
- **Keep away from flammable materials** — curtains, paper, fabric.
- **Use a fireproof dish** at all times. Carry it under the bundle to catch falling embers.
- **Avoid smoke around birds, small pets, infants, and anyone with respiratory conditions.** Open windows well or cleanse those areas with sound or spray instead.
- **Keep water or sand nearby** for quick extinguishing.
- **Ensure the bundle is fully extinguished** before storing it. Embers can reignite.

## Alternatives to Smoke

If smoke doesn't work for your living situation (apartments, pets, sensitivities), you can cleanse energy with:

- **Sound** — Bells, singing bowls, clapping, drumming, or playing music
- **Salt** — Place bowls of salt in corners to absorb energy, then discard after 24 hours
- **Sprays** — Make an herbal spray with water, witch hazel, and essential oils (lavender, rosemary, sage)
- **Visualization** — Imagine white or golden light moving through your space, clearing everything heavy
- **Fresh air and sunlight** — Open all windows and let nature do the work
