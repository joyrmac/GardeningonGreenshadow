# How to Make Smudge Bundles (Herbal Smoke Bundles)
### A Complete Guide to Crafting Your Own Herb Bundles for Smoke Cleansing

---

## Why Make Your Own?

Making your own herb bundles is more sustainable, more affordable, and more personal than buying them. When you grow, harvest, dry, and wrap your own herbs, you build a relationship with the plants and infuse the bundle with your own energy and intention from the very beginning.

---

## What You'll Need

### Materials

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Fresh herbs** | 8-12 stems, each 6-10 inches long |
| **Natural cotton twine or hemp cord** | NEVER use synthetic string — it releases toxic fumes when burned |
| **Scissors or garden shears** | For harvesting and trimming |
| **Optional: dried flowers** | Lavender, roses, calendula — for beauty and added properties |
| **Optional: essential oils** | A few drops on the finished, dried bundle |

## Step 1: Harvest

### When to Harvest
- **Morning** after the dew has dried but before the heat of the day — this is when essential oils are strongest
- **Before the plant flowers** for maximum potency (though flowering herbs are still wonderful)
- **During a waxing or full moon** if you want to align with lunar energy

### How to Harvest
- Cut stems **6-10 inches long** — long enough to create a handle
- **Never take more than 1/3** of any single plant — leave enough for the plant to recover and for pollinators
- Use clean, sharp scissors or garden shears
- **Only harvest plants you can positively identify** — never burn anything you're unsure about
- Avoid plants that have been treated with pesticides, herbicides, or chemical fertilizers
- **Thank the plant.** Whether you pray, speak, or just pause — acknowledge what you're taking

---

## Step 2: Prep and Dry (Partially)

1. **Gently rinse** your herbs under cool water to remove dirt and insects
2. **Shake off excess water** and lay on a clean towel
3. **Let herbs wilt for 1-2 days** in a cool, dry spot — you want them **flexible but not crispy.** If they're too fresh, they'll mold inside the bundle. If they're too dry, they'll crumble when you try to wrap them.
4. **Remove any dead or brown leaves** from the lower stems (this becomes your handle area)
5. **Sort by size** — longer, sturdier stems in the center; shorter, leafier pieces on the outside

---

## Step 3: Arrange Your Bundle

1. **Create your base layer.** Start with 3-4 of your longest, sturdiest stems (sage, rosemary, or cedar work well as a spine).

2. **Layer in your secondary herbs** around the base. Fan them out slightly so the bundle has a nice, full shape.

3. **Tuck in dried flowers or smaller herbs** on the outside where they'll be visible.

4. **Keep the stem ends (bottom) even** — this is your handle. The top can be uneven and natural-looking.

5. **Aim for a bundle about 1-1.5 inches in diameter** — not too thick (won't dry properly) and not too thin (burns too fast).

## Step 4: Wrap Your Bundle

### What You Need
- About **4 feet of natural cotton twine or hemp cord** (roughly 4x the length of your bundle)

### Wrapping Technique

1. **Start at the base (stem end).** Leave a 4-inch tail of string hanging.

2. **Wrap tightly around the base 3-4 times** and tie a firm knot. The base needs to be the tightest part — this is what holds everything together.

3. **Begin spiraling upward.** Wrap the twine in a spiral pattern, moving up toward the top of the bundle. Keep even spacing — about 1/2 inch between wraps.

4. **Crisscross as you go.** When you reach the top, don't tie off — instead, wrap back down in the opposite direction, creating an X pattern. This locks everything in place.

5. **Tuck in any loose leaves or flowers** as you wrap back down. Adjust the shape as you go.

6. **Finish at the base.** Tie the working end to the tail you left at the beginning. Make it secure — a square knot works well.

7. **Trim the stem ends** so they're even and tidy.

### Wrapping Tips
- **Wrap tighter than you think you need to.** Herbs shrink significantly as they dry, and a loose wrap will fall apart.
- **If the bundle feels loose after a few days of drying,** you can re-wrap it tighter.
- **For a decorative finish,** tie a small loop at the base for hanging.

---

## Step 5: Dry Your Bundles

### Hanging Method (Preferred)
1. **Hang bundles upside down** from a hook, clothesline, or nail
2. Use your string loop or tie a separate piece of twine to hang them
3. Choose a location that is:
   - **Cool** (not hot — heat destroys essential oils)
   - **Dark** (sunlight fades color and reduces potency)
   - **Well-ventilated** (air circulation prevents mold)
   - **Dry** (humidity causes mold — avoid bathrooms and kitchens)
4. A closet, attic, covered porch, or spare room works great

### Drying Time
- **Minimum: 2 weeks**
- **Ideal: 3-4 weeks**
- **Test for doneness:** The bundle should feel crisp and dry throughout. Squeeze the center — if it gives or feels damp at all, it needs more time. A bundle that isn't fully dry will smolder poorly and may mold.

### Signs of Problems
| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Mold (white/gray fuzz) | Too humid, not enough airflow | Discard — don't burn moldy herbs |
| Brown/black spots | Herbs were too wet when bundled | Unwrap, re-dry herbs, re-bundle |
| Falling apart | Wrapped too loosely | Re-wrap tighter while herbs are still slightly flexible |
| No scent | Dried in sunlight or heat | Next time, dry in a cool, dark place |

---

## Step 6: Store Your Bundles

- Store in a **cool, dark, dry place**
- A linen bag, paper bag, or open basket works well — **avoid plastic** (traps moisture)
- If you want to add essential oil, place 2-3 drops on the outside of the dried bundle and let it absorb for a day before using
- **Label your bundles** if you make different blends — you'll forget which is which
- Well-dried bundles can last **1-2 years** stored properly

---

## Using Your Bundles

1. Light the tip at a 45-degree angle
2. Let it flame for 15-20 seconds
3. Blow out the flame — you want smoke, not fire
4. Hold over a fireproof dish at all times
5. Use your hand or a feather to direct the smoke
6. Extinguish by pressing firmly into sand or the dish when done
7. See the full **How to Smudge** guide for detailed smoke cleansing instructions
