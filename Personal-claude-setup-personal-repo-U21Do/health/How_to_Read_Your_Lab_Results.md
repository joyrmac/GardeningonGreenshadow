## The Basics: How Lab Results Work

### Reference Ranges
Every lab value comes with a **reference range** — the "normal" window for that test. Example:

```
LDL Cholesterol: 137.8 mg/dL    Reference Range: <100 mg/dL (optimal)
```

- **Within range** = your body is handling that function normally
- **Slightly out of range** = worth monitoring, usually not urgent
- **Significantly out of range** = your provider should discuss this with you

**Important:** Reference ranges are population averages. Your personal normal might be slightly different. What matters more than a single number is the **trend over time**.

### Flagged Results
Labs use flags:
- **H** = High (above reference range)
- **L** = Low (below reference range)
- **A** = Abnormal
- No flag = within normal range

Don't panic at a single flag. One slightly elevated number is usually noise. A pattern over multiple tests is signal.

## Your Key Lab Panels Explained

### Complete Metabolic Panel (CMP)
This is the big one — measures how your organs are functioning.

| Test | What It Measures | Why You Care |
|------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Glucose (fasting)** | Blood sugar | Screens for diabetes/prediabetes. Your HbA1c at 5.3% means your glucose control is solid. |
| **BUN / Creatinine** | Kidney function | If these are normal, your kidneys are filtering properly. |
| **Sodium / Potassium** | Electrolyte balance | Affects heart, muscles, nerves. Usually normal unless you're dehydrated or on certain meds. |
| **Calcium** | Bone/nerve health | Rarely a problem unless thyroid is involved. |
| **ALT / AST** | Liver enzymes | Elevated = liver is working harder than it should. Causes: alcohol, medications, fatty liver. |
| **Albumin** | Protein levels | Reflects nutrition status. Low = possible malnutrition or chronic inflammation. |

### Lipid Panel (Cholesterol)
This is your heart health snapshot.

| Test | What It Measures | Your Numbers (Dec 2025) | What It Means |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|---------------|
| **Total Cholesterol** | Overall cholesterol | — | Less important than the breakdown below |
| **LDL ("bad")** | Cholesterol that clogs arteries | 137.8 | Slightly elevated. Goal is <100 (optimal) or <130 (acceptable). Diet, exercise, and genetics all play roles. |
| **HDL ("good")** | Cholesterol that cleans arteries | 37.4 | Low. Goal is >40 (men) or >50 (women). Exercise raises this. Genetics are a factor too. |
| **Triglycerides** | Fat in your blood | 155 | Slightly elevated. Goal is <150. Affected by sugar, refined carbs, alcohol, and activity level. |

**The ratio matters more than individual numbers.** High LDL + Low HDL = your arteries are getting more deposits than cleanups. The fix is usually: more movement, less processed food, more healthy fats (avocado, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish).

### HbA1c (Hemoglobin A1c)
This is your **3-month blood sugar average** — more reliable than a single fasting glucose.

| Range | What It Means |
|-------|--------------|
| Below 5.7% | Normal |
| 5.7% – 6.4% | Prediabetic |
| 6.5%+ | Diabetic range |

**Your number: 5.3%** — solidly normal. No concerns here.

### Thyroid Panel (TSH)
- **TSH** (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) is the primary screening test
- Normal range: ~0.5–4.5 mIU/L
- High TSH = underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism) — fatigue, weight gain, brain fog
- Low TSH = overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism) — anxiety, weight loss, rapid heartbeat
- **Yours: Normal** — thyroid is functioning properly

### CBC (Complete Blood Count)
Measures red cells, white cells, and platelets.

| Component | What It Tells You |
|-----------|------------------|
| **WBC (White Blood Cells)** | Immune function. High = possible infection or inflammation. Low = immune suppression. |
| **RBC / Hemoglobin / Hematocrit** | Oxygen-carrying capacity. Low = anemia (fatigue, shortness of breath). |
| **Platelets** | Clotting ability. Low = bruising/bleeding risk. High = clotting risk. |

## How to Track Your Labs Over Time

The single most useful thing you can do with lab results is **compare them to your previous results**. One number means little. A trend means everything.

### What to Watch
1. **LDL trending up or down?** — Are lifestyle changes working?
2. **HDL improving?** — Exercise and diet should push this up over time
3. **HbA1c stable?** — As long as it stays below 5.7%, you're good
4. **Anything newly flagged?** — New flags deserve a conversation with your provider

### Questions to Ask Your Provider
- "How does this compare to my last results?"
- "Is this something we need to act on, or just monitor?"
- "Does my CYP2C19 status affect any medication you're considering?"
- "What specific changes would move this number?"
