# Symptom & Medication Journal
### A Template for Tracking What You Take, How You Feel, and What Patterns Emerge

---

## Why Track This

You have three conditions (ADHD, GAD, PTSD) that overlap in symptoms, plus you're a CYP2C19 intermediate metabolizer — meaning your body processes certain medications differently than average. Tracking helps you:

1. **Spot patterns** — Is the anxiety worse at certain times? Does energy crash after meds? Do symptoms cycle with your period, sleep, or stress?
2. **Talk to your provider with data** — Instead of "I've been feeling off," you can say "I've had increased anxiety 4 out of the last 7 days, mostly in the afternoon, and it correlates with poor sleep."
3. **Catch medication issues early** — Side effects, tolerance changes, or interactions show up as patterns before they become problems.
4. **Separate what's what** — Is that brain fog ADHD, anxiety, poor sleep, or a medication side effect? Tracking over time reveals the answer.

## Patterns to Watch For

### ADHD vs. Anxiety vs. Medication — How to Tell the Difference

| Symptom | If It's ADHD | If It's Anxiety | If It's Medication |
|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Can't focus** | Scattered across everything; better with novelty/interest | Focused on worrying; can't focus because mind is racing about threats | Started or worsened after dose change |
| **Fatigue** | Mental exhaustion from forcing focus; crashes after hyperfocus | Physical fatigue from constant nervous system activation | Consistent drowsiness, worse at same time of day relative to medication timing |
| **Irritability** | Frustration with tasks, transitions, interruptions | Snapping because everything feels like a threat | New or worsened after starting/changing medication |
| **Restlessness** | Need to move, fidget, switch tasks | Can't sit still because of internal dread | Akathisia (medication-induced restlessness — distinct from ADHD) |
| **Brain fog** | Normal for you, fluctuates with interest level | Worse when anxiety is high | New or notably worse after medication change |

### Red Flags to Report to Your Provider Immediately

- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm urges (especially in first weeks of SSRI changes)
- Severe mood swings (very high then very low within days)
- Chest pain, severe headaches, or fainting
- New or severe allergic reactions
- Feeling "worse than before" consistently for 2+ weeks after a medication change

### Your CYP2C19 Warning Signs

Because you metabolize Escitalopram more slowly, watch for signs of accumulation:
- Unusual drowsiness or sedation
- Increased nausea
- Sexual side effects worsening
- Feeling "flat" emotionally (over-medicated vs. therapeutic)

If these appear, it may mean even 5mg is too much — talk to your provider.

## How to Bring This to Your Provider

Don't dump the whole journal on them. Summarize:

**Script:** "I've been tracking my symptoms daily. Over the last [month], my biggest patterns are [top 2-3 symptoms]. They seem to correlate with [trigger]. I'm wondering if [specific question]."

**What they need from you:**
1. Trend direction (better, worse, same)
2. Top 2-3 symptoms
3. Any medication concerns
4. One specific question

See `How_to_Advocate_at_VA_Appointments.md` for more on making appointments productive.

## Tracking Tools (Pick ONE)

| Tool | Best For | ADHD-Friendliness |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| **Phone notes app** | Quick daily entries | High — always with you |
| **Daylio app** | Mood + activity tracking with minimal writing | Very high — just tap icons |
| **Bearable app** | Detailed symptom tracking with correlations | Medium — more setup, but powerful insights |
| **Paper notebook** | Tactile, no screen | Medium — satisfying but easy to forget |
| **Spreadsheet** | Data nerds who want to chart trends | Low ADHD-friendliness, high power |

**The best tracker is the one you'll actually use.** If a full daily log is too much, just track: mood (1-5), sleep hours, and one sentence. That's enough to spot patterns.
