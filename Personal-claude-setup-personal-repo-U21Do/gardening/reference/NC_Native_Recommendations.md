🌻** The Sunflower Garden (Golden Hour Patch)**  
*A bright, full-sun area. Great for prairie flowers, companion plants, and deep roots.*  
	•	**Coreopsis (Lanceleaf or Plains Coreopsis)** – sunny yellow, native, low-maintenance, and blooms long.  
	•	**Blazing Star (Liatris spicata)** – beautiful vertical spires of purple; pollinator heaven.  
	•	**Mountain Mint (Pycnanthemum tenuifolium)** – native, doesn’t spread aggressively, excellent for pollinators.  
  
⸻  
  
🍉** Melon Moon Patch**  
*Viney and warm, with lots of room for spreading.*  
	•	**Partridge Pea (Chamaecrista fasciculata)** – native, nitrogen-fixer, pairs well with melons.  
	•	**Rattlesnake Master (Eryngium yuccifolium)** – unusual and stunning, adds textural variety and tolerates heat.  
  
⸻  
  
🦋** The Honeyglow Patch (Pollinator Garden)**  
*Already a stunner, but can always make room for more nectar-rich options!*  
	•	**Aromatic Aster (Symphyotrichum oblongifolium)** – beautiful fall bloomers, essential for late-season pollinators.  
	•	**Ironweed (Vernonia noveboracensis)** – deep purple flowers that add dramatic height.  
	•	**Spotted Beebalm (Monarda punctata)** – whorled blossoms, native, and beloved by native bees.  
  
⸻  
  
🌸** Bloomveil (Cut Flower Garden)**  
*We’re being selective here—nothing too wild, but you can sneak in a few structured, clumping natives:*  
	•	**Wild Bergamot (Monarda fistulosa)** – not as aggressive as other Monardas, and so lovely in bouquets.  
	•	**Blue Mistflower (Conoclinium coelestinum)** – dreamy color, manageable spread if pinched back.  
	•	**Stoke’s Aster (Stokesia laevis)** – tidy, beautiful, and long-blooming.  
  
⸻  
  
🌿** The Alchemist’s Spiral (Herb Garden)**  
*You’re already set here, but maybe tuck in one or two more native herbs:*  
	•	**Virginia Mountain Mint (Pycnanthemum virginianum)** – narrow habit, aromatic, pollinator favorite.  
	•	**Golden Alexander (Zizia aurea)** – early bloomer and host plant for black swallowtails.  
  
⸻  
  
🧚‍♀️** Everhollow (Shady Fairy Garden)**  
*Time stands still here. Think magical woodland understory plants:*  
	•	**Heuchera americana (Alumroot)** – colorful leaves and sweet blooms for shade.  
	•	**Golden Ragwort (Packera aurea)** – dreamy early bloom and spreads slowly in shade.  
	•	**Solomon’s Seal (Polygonatum biflorum)** – graceful, arching native for dappled shade.  
  
⸻  
  
🦜** Edgeworth Corner (Bird & Butterfly Bed)**  
*Already lush but these add height and movement:*  
	•	**New York Ironweed** – great for layering and drawing in butterflies.  
	•	**Purple Coneflower (you’ve got plenty, but consider letting a few naturalize here too)**
