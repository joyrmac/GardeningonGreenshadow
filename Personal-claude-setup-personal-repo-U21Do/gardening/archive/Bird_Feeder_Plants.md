**ZONE LAYOUT**  
  
**ZONE 1 — Feeder Drop Zone**  
  
**Center of tree | 4–5 ft diameter | NO plants**  
  
**Purpose:**  
Contain bird seed so it can’t turn into weeds.  
  
**Treatment:**  
	•	Cardboard (overlapping)  
	•	2–3” compost  
	•	3–4” pine fines or hardwood mulch  
  
This area stays **mulch-only forever**.  
  
⸻  
  
**ZONE 2 — Native Groundcover Matrix**  
  
**Surrounds drop zone | Weed suppression layer**  
  
These plants are **tough, shallow-rooted, and forgiving of seed disturbance**.  
	•	**Carex pensylvanica** *(Pennsylvania sedge)* — 20–25  
→ Backbone plant, grass-like, excellent weed suppression  
	•	**Chrysogonum virginianum** *(Green-and-gold)* — 10–15  
→ Low, spreading, bright spring flowers  
	•	**Fragaria virginiana** *(Wild strawberry)* — 10  
→ Living mulch, birds enjoy fruit, fills gaps quickly  
  
**Spacing:** 12–15”  
**Goal:** Dense coverage within 1–2 seasons  
  
⸻  
  
**ZONE 3 — Native Accent & Structure Layer**  
  
**Outer edge of 10×15 footprint**  
  
Adds height variation, pollinators, and seasonal interest without fuss.  
	•	**Heuchera americana** *(American coral bells)* — 3–5  
→ Evergreen-ish foliage, handles shade + sun  
	•	**Aquilegia canadensis** *(Eastern red columbine)* — 3–5  
→ Hummingbird favorite, airy spring blooms  
	•	**Packera aurea** *(Golden ragwort)* — 5–7  
→ Early blooms, spreads politely, stabilizes soil  
	•	**Schizachyrium scoparium** *(Little bluestem)* — 1–2  
→ Structural anchor, winter interest, glowing fall color  
  
⸻  
  
**INSTALL TIMELINE (ZONE 7b, NC)**  
  
**JANUARY–FEBRUARY**  
	•	Switch all feeders to **no-mess seed**  
	•	Finalize feeder placement  
	•	Order plants early  
  
⸻  
  
**LATE FEBRUARY – EARLY MARCH**  
  
**Weed reset**  
	1.	Cut weeds to ground (do not pull)  
	2.	Lay cardboard over entire 10×15 area  
	3.	Add compost  
	4.	Mulch heavily  
  
⸻  
  
**MID-MARCH – EARLY APRIL**  
  
**Planting**  
	•	Cut planting holes through cardboard  
	•	Leave Zone 1 empty (mulch only)  
	•	Install Zones 2 & 3  
	•	Water deeply for 2–3 weeks  
  
⸻  
  
**MAY–JUNE**  
	•	Light spot-weeding only if needed  
	•	Let maple leaves stay  
  
⸻  
  
**NOVEMBER**  
	•	1–2” mulch refresh  
	•	Walk away

**MASTER SHOPPING LIST (FOOD ONLY)**  
  
Buy **these five** and skip everything else:  
	1.	**Hulled sunflower hearts / chips**  
	2.	**Shelled peanuts (no skins, unsalted)**  
	3.	**Quality suet cakes** (plain or peanut / nut & fruit — no millet)  
	4.	**Nyjer (thistle) seed**  
	5.	**Dried mealworms** (optional but great for variety)  
  
⸻  
  
**WHAT GOES IN WHICH FEEDER**  
  
**Camera Feeder (Netvue Birdfy) — “Variety Star”**  
  
**Use:**  
	•	Hulled sunflower hearts (base)  
	•	Shelled peanuts (mixed in)  
	•	Small amount of nyjer  
	•	Occasional pinch of mealworms  
  
**Purpose:**  
Maximum species diversity with clean footage and low mess.  
  
⸻  
  
**Tube Feeders (Perky-Pet Squirrel-Be-Gone, metal tube styles)**  
  
**Use one of these (or rotate):**  
	•	Hulled sunflower hearts  
	•	Shelled peanuts  
  
**Purpose:**  
Cardinals, chickadees, titmice, nuthatches, woodpeckers  
Low drop, low sprout.  
  
⸻  
  
**Fly-Through Feeders (Perky-Pet open square, Birdream style)**  
  
**Use ONLY:**  
	•	Hulled sunflower hearts  
  
**Purpose:**  
Birds eat inside → almost zero waste.  
  
⸻  
  
**Mesh / Cage Feeders (Kingsyard, squirrel-proof mesh)**  
  
**Best use:**  
	•	Shelled peanuts  
  
**Alternate:**  
	•	Hulled sunflower hearts  
  
**Purpose:**  
Woodpeckers, jays, nuthatches — very little seed hits the ground.  
  
⸻  
  
**Suet Block Holders**  
  
**Use:**  
	•	Plain beef suet  
	•	Peanut suet  
	•	Nut & fruit suet (no filler seed)  
  
**Season note (Zone 7b):**  
	•	Fall–Spring: regular suet  
	•	Summer: **no-melt suet only**  
  
**Purpose:**  
Zero weeds, high-value calories, brings in different species.  
  
⸻  
  
**Tray / Hanging Basket Feeders**  
  
**Use sparingly:**  
	•	Dried mealworms  
	•	Small amounts of hulled sunflower  
	•	Seasonal fruit (oranges, berries)  
  
**Avoid:**  
Mixed seed (this is where weeds explode).  
  
⸻  
  
**WHAT YOU SHOULD NOT BUY (EVER AGAIN)**  
  
These cause almost all feeder weeds:  
	•	Millet (any kind)  
	•	Milo  
	•	Cracked corn  
	•	“Wild bird mixes”  
	•	Cheap economy blends  
  
⸻  
  
**TL;DR — IF YOU WANT IT ULTRA SIMPLE**  
	•	**Sunflower hearts everywhere**  
	•	**Peanuts in mesh + some tubes**  
	•	**Suet always**  
	•	**Nyjer only for the camera feeder**  
	•	**Mealworms as a bonus**
