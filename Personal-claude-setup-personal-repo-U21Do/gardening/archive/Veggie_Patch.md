**Here’s What You’ll Do for Your Salad + Root Chaos Bed:**  
  
**1. Choose a 2–4 ft wide patch (anywhere — end of Tanglebloom, or a dedicated shady-ish zone nearby).**  
  
**2. Prep the bed:**  
	•	Top with compost or Soil3 (which you already have).  
	•	Break up the soil well for carrot roots.  
  
**3. Mix seeds in this order:**  
	•	**Fast growers**: Arugula, mustard, spinach, lettuce  
	•	**Mid growers**: Kale, parsley, fennel  
	•	**Slow roots**: Carrots (short varieties work best here)  
	•	**Aromatic/beneficials**: Chives, calendula, marigolds  
  
**4. Scatter & Rake In:**  
	•	Toss the seed blend evenly like you’re seasoning magic.  
	•	Lightly rake them in, don’t bury too deep.  
	•	Water gently but deeply.  
  
**5. Optional verticals:**  
	•	Add a few **peas**, **bush beans**, or a short cucumber variety with sticks/branches for them to climb.  
  
**6. Maintenance magic:**  
	•	**Harvest early and often** to make space for slower growers.  
	•	Don’t thin like a maniac — just snip greens as needed.  
	•	Let it get tangled, then tame, then tangled again.
