**🌞 Full Sun vs. Partial Shade: Which Wildflowers Go Where?**  
✔️ **Most wildflowers prefer full sun (6+ hours a day),** but some will **tolerate shade.** Here’s how to **divide them** for the best success:  
**🌞 Full Sun (6+ Hours Daily) – Open, Bright Areas**  
(Plant these in the **sunniest** parts of your yard) ✅ **Eden Brothers Wildflower Mixes**  
* **Burst of Bloom Annual & Perennial Mix**  
* **Flowery Fields Forever Wildflower Mix**  
✅ **Mountain Valley Wildflower Mixes**  
* **Save the Monarchs Mix** (*Milkweed thrives in full sun!*)  
* **Save the Bees Mix** (*Bees love sun-loving flowers like poppies & cosmos.*)  
* **Hummingbird & Butterfly Mix** (*Trumpet-shaped flowers like full sun.*)  
✅ **Sweet Yards Seed Co.**  
* **Hummingbird & Butterfly Mix**  
* **Cracker Jack Mix African Marigold** (*Marigolds LOVE sun!*)  
* **Zinnias (all varieties)**  
* **Cosmos (all varieties)**  
* **Calendula**  
* **Strawflower, Celosia, Globe Amaranth**  
🌟 **Best full-sun planting method:**  
* **Use a seed spreader or scatter by hand** in the **brightest, most open** part of the yard.  
* **Mix with sand or compost** to help distribute evenly.  
* **Lightly rake & press seeds** into the soil (**don’t bury too deep!**).  
* **Water gently** after planting.  
  
**⛅ Partial Shade (4-6 Hours Daily) – Edges, Near Trees, or Shaded Fences**  
(Plant these where they get **morning sun & afternoon shade** or dappled sunlight.)

✅ **Other Shade-Tolerant Flowers & Herbs**  
* **Foxglove** (*Thrives in partial shade, start indoors first!*)  
* **Bells of Ireland** (*Likes cooler, partial shade spots.*)  
* **Cornflower (Bachelor’s Button)** (*Can handle some shade but prefers sun.*)  
* **Milkweed (Rose & Common)** (*Will tolerate shade but blooms better in sun.*)  
* **Lavender (Munstead, Hidcote, Vera, Spike)** (*Lavender does best in sun but tolerates partial shade.*)  
🌟 **Best partial-shade planting method:**  
* Scatter **lightly** in areas where sun shifts throughout the day.  
* **Skip raking too deep!** Just press into the soil so they’re not covered too much.  
* **Water gently, but don’t overdo it!**  
  
**🌿 Final Wildflower Plan for Your Back Yard**  
* **🌞 Full Sun Areas (Center, Open Spaces):** Bright, heat-loving flowers (Zinnias, Cosmos, Calendula, Marigolds, Monarch & Bee Mixes).  
* **⛅ Partial Shade (Edges, Near Trees, Shaded Fences):** Shade-tolerant varieties (Sweet Peas, Foxglove, Bells of Ireland, Bird & Butterfly Mix).  
* **🌾 Natural Blending:** Let some flowers overlap **so the transition looks seamless!**
