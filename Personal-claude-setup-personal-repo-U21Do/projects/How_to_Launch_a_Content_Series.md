# How to Launch a Content Series
### A Step-by-Step Playbook for Taking an Idea From Concept to Consistent Output

---

## Who This Is For

You have two active projects right now:
1. **Steel Sharpens Steel** — NFL content for women
2. **Scout Media** — social media management business

Both require consistent content. This guide covers how to take any content idea — a blog series, a social media series, a newsletter, a YouTube concept — from "I have an idea" to "this is a real thing that exists in the world."

---

## Phase 1: Define It (Before You Create Anything)

Answer these five questions. Don't skip them — they prevent the most common failure mode, which is starting strong and losing direction.

### The 5 Questions

| Question | Why It Matters | Your Answer (Steel Sharpens Steel Example) |
|----------|---------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Who is this for?** | Content without a specific audience is just journaling | Women learning football — specifically Black women, ADHD-friendly, no condescension |
| **What problem does it solve?** | People don't consume content for fun — they consume it because it fills a gap | "I want to understand football but every resource talks down to me or assumes I already know things" |
| **What format?** | Format determines workflow, tools, and time commitment | Weekly written rundown + occasional deep-dive posts |
| **What's the cadence?** | Consistency matters more than frequency | Weekly (Tuesdays during season, biweekly off-season) |
| **What's the voice?** | See `../writing-guides/Writing_Voice_Guide.md` | Confident, straight-talking, explains jargon in context |

### The One-Sentence Pitch

Can you describe the series in one sentence? If not, it's not clear enough yet.

**Steel Sharpens Steel:** "A weekly NFL guide for women who want to understand football without being talked down to."

**Template:** "A [frequency] [format] for [audience] who want to [outcome] without [pain point]."

## Phase 2: Build the Content Engine

### Create a Template

Every piece of content in a series should follow a consistent structure. This:
- Reduces decision fatigue (ADHD-friendly)
- Sets audience expectations
- Makes creating each piece faster

**Steel Sharpens Steel Template:**
```
1. TL;DR (3-4 bullet points)
2. Steelers Update
3. Around the League (1-2 other stories)
4. Player/Coach Spotlight OR Slang of the Week
5. What to Watch This Week
```

**Your turn:** Whatever series you're building, map out the recurring sections. They don't all have to appear every time — but the skeleton stays consistent.

### Batch Create

ADHD brains do better with dedicated creation time than scattered daily creation. Batch it:

| Instead of... | Try... |
|--------------|--------|
| Writing 1 post per day, 5 days a week | Writing 5 posts in one sitting on Thursday (Creative Day) |
| Filming content whenever inspiration hits | Filming 4 videos in one session, editing them later |
| Researching and writing simultaneously | Researching on one day, writing from notes on another |

**The ideal content day:**
1. 30 min: Research/gather information
2. 60 min: Write/create the content
3. 30 min: Edit, format, schedule
4. Done. Walk away.

### Use AI as a First Draft Tool

See `../writing-guides/How_to_Train_AI_on_Your_Voice.md` for the full process, but the short version:

1. Paste your voice guide into ChatGPT/Claude
2. Give it your template structure + this week's topics
3. Let it produce a draft
4. Edit with your voice, your takes, your personality
5. Publish

AI does the scaffolding. You do the soul.

## Phase 3: Set Up Distribution

### Choose Your Platforms (Start With One)

| Platform | Best For | Time Commitment |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Instagram** | Visual content, reels, carousels, community building | High (daily engagement expected) |
| **Substack/Newsletter** | Long-form writing, loyal audience, owned list | Medium (weekly drops, minimal algorithm) |
| **TikTok** | Short-form video, discovery, viral potential | High (frequent posting, trending audio) |
| **Twitter/X** | Hot takes, real-time commentary, threads | Medium (bursty during events, otherwise light) |
| **Blog/Website** | SEO, portfolio, long-form, evergreen content | Low-Medium (weekly or biweekly) |

**The rule: Start with ONE platform. Master it. Then expand.**

For Steel Sharpens Steel, a Substack or newsletter is the natural fit — weekly, written, no algorithm to fight.

For Scout Media clients, you're managing their multi-platform presence — but your own Scout Media presence should showcase your best work on 1-2 platforms.

### Schedule in Advance

Use tools to schedule content so you're not posting live every time:
- **Later, Buffer, or Hootsuite** — schedule social media posts
- **Substack** — schedule newsletter sends
- **Blaze.ai** — you already use this for Scout Media planning

## Phase 4: Launch It

### The Soft Launch

You don't need a grand announcement. Post the first one. That's the launch.

**What a soft launch looks like:**
1. Publish your first piece
2. Share it on your personal channels with a simple intro: "I'm starting something. Here's the first one."
3. Ask 3-5 people you trust to read it and give honest feedback
4. Publish the second one. Now it's a series.

### The Harder Part: Consistency

The first 3 posts are fueled by excitement. Posts 4-15 are where most series die. Here's how to survive the dip:

| The Dip | The Fix |
|---------|--------|
| "Nobody's reading this" | They are — they're just not commenting yet. Small audiences are still audiences. |
| "I ran out of ideas" | Keep a running idea list (phone notes). Capture ideas when they hit, create from the list when it's time. |
| "It's not good enough" | Published beats perfect. You can always edit after posting. Most people won't notice. |
| "I missed a week" | Skip it. Don't apologize. Just post the next one. Consistency means long-term, not perfect attendance. |
| "I lost motivation" | Reread the 5 Questions from Phase 1. Reconnect with WHY you started. |

---

## Phase 5: Grow It

### Month 1-3: Foundation
- Post consistently (whatever cadence you set)
- Build the template habit
- Gather feedback from real readers
- Adjust format based on what resonates

### Month 4-6: Optimization
- Which posts got the most engagement? Do more of that.
- Which sections do readers love? Expand those.
- Which parts feel like a chore? Simplify or cut them.
- Start cross-promoting on a second platform.

### Month 7-12: Expansion
- Consider guest features, collaborations, or interviews
- Build an email list if you haven't already (owned audience > algorithm)
- Explore monetization if it makes sense (sponsorships, paid tier, products)
- Repurpose content across platforms (a blog post becomes 3 Instagram carousels)

---

## The Content Creator's Non-Negotiables

1. **Protect your creative energy.** Don't doom-scroll before creating. Create first, consume later.
2. **Your voice is the product.** Don't mimic what's popular. What makes your content different IS you.
3. **Finished beats perfect.** Every single time.
4. **Rest is part of the process.** Burnout kills more content series than bad ideas do.
