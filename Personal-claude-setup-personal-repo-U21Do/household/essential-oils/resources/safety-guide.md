# 🛡️ Essential Oil Safety Guide
*Complete Safety Reference for the McNamara Family*

---

## Golden Rules of Essential Oil Safety

1. **Always Dilute** for topical use (except in emergencies)
2. **Never Ingest** without professional guidance
3. **Keep Away** from children's unsupervised reach
4. **Patch Test** new oils on small area of skin first
5. **Never Exceed** recommended drop counts
6. **Research First** before using on pregnancy/special conditions
7. **Quality Matters** - use reputable brands (your Plant Therapy, etc.)
8. **Storage** in cool, dark, glass containers

## Safety by Oil — Your Collection

### **Safest Oils for All Ages (with proper dilution)**

✅ **Lavender:** Safe for all ages; very low toxicity; can use neat in emergency
✅ **Frankincense:** Safe for all ages; beneficial for children; no major restrictions
✅ **Sweet Orange:** Safe for all ages; gentle, uplifting; slightly photosensitive
✅ **Cedarwood:** Safe for all ages; calming, grounding

### **Oils Requiring Caution with Children**

⚠️ **Peppermint:**
- Safe for kids 5+ (diluted)
- Can stimulate; not before bed
- Don't use on children under 5
- May irritate sensitive skin

⚠️ **Eucalyptus:**
- Safe for kids 6+ (diluted, diffusion only)
- Never apply to face of young children
- Can irritate mucous membranes
- Keep away from mouth

⚠️ **Rosemary:**
- Safe for kids 6+ (diffusion, diluted topical)
- Can be stimulating
- Not for evening use
- Generally safe but age-appropriate monitoring

⚠️ **Tea Tree:**
- Safe for kids 2+ (well diluted)
- Keep away from mouth
- Can irritate skin if concentrated
- Not for ingestion

### **Use with Extreme Caution or Avoid**

❌ **Cinnamon Cassia:**
- Skin irritant (can cause redness/sensitivity)
- Use at 0.5% max for topical
- Okay for diffusion/cleaning
- Not for children's skin without supervision
- Always patch test

❌ **Bergamot:**
- **Photosensitizing** - Do NOT use on exposed skin before sun
- Safe for diffusion
- Can be used in products not exposed to sun (evening lotions)
- Okay for cleaning, diffusion
- Safe when sun-protected

### **Not Appropriate for Children Under 12**

- High-concentration medicinal blends
- Direct inhalation without diffusion
- Undiluted applications
- Internal use (without professional guidance)

## Common Safety Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ **Mistake: Using Undiluted Essential Oil on Children's Skin**
✅ **Fix:** Always dilute (0.5-1% for children, 2-5% for adults)
✅ **Remember:** Skin is more permeable in children; oils absorb faster

### ❌ **Mistake: Mixing with Bleach or Ammonia**
✅ **Fix:** NEVER combine essential oils with bleach or ammonia - creates toxic gas
✅ **Remember:** Clean surfaces with water first, then use EO cleaners alone

### ❌ **Mistake: Using Same Blend Continuously Without Breaks**
✅ **Fix:** Take breaks (1 week on, 1 week off) to avoid desensitization
✅ **Remember:** Rotate blends; your body adapts to constant exposure

### ❌ **Mistake: Assuming "Natural = Safe for Everyone"**
✅ **Fix:** Pregnant people, nursing mothers, young children have different needs
✅ **Remember:** Research individual circumstances; ask healthcare provider

### ❌ **Mistake: Applying Hot Oils to Children's Skin**
✅ **Fix:** Test temperature; dilute in carrier; use on small areas only
✅ **Remember:** Eucalyptus, Peppermint, Cinnamon can feel hot on skin

### ❌ **Mistake: Ignoring Patch Test Results**
✅ **Fix:** Always patch test new oils behind ear or inside wrist
✅ **Remember:** Individual reactions vary; itching/redness = reduce concentration

### ❌ **Mistake: Overusing in Diffusion**
✅ **Fix:** Diffuse 20-30 minutes, break for 30 minutes, then diffuse again
✅ **Remember:** Continuous exposure can cause headaches/desensitization

- Lavender (calming, safe)

- Cedarwood (safe)
- Sweet Orange (safe)
- Lemon (safe)

### **If Nursing**

⚠️ **Oils to Avoid:**
- Very high concentrations (systemic absorption affects milk)
- Peppermint (can decrease milk supply in excess)

✅ **Safe Approach:**
- Diffusion is safer than topical
- Dilute well for topical use
- Keep topical applications on arms/hands (infant won't directly contact)
- Avoid applying to breasts/chest area

## Storage & Preservation

### **Proper Storage = Longer Shelf Life**

**Best Storage Conditions:**
- **Container:** Dark glass bottles (amber or cobalt)
- **Location:** Cool, dark cupboard (NOT under sink where heat/moisture)
- **Temperature:** Below 70°F ideal; consistent temperature
- **Light:** Away from direct sunlight
- **Humidity:** Low humidity area

**Shelf Life of Your Oils:**
- Plant Therapy oils: 1-2 years
- P&J fragrance oils: 2-3 years
- MitFlor blends: 1-2 years
- Folkulture blends: 1-2 years

### **Signs Oil Has Degraded**

❌ **Oxidation (most common):**
- Color change (darkening)
- Rancid smell (vinegary, off)
- Separation of components
- Thickened consistency

❌ **What to Do:**
- Stop using
- Dispose properly (don't pour down drain - mix with baking soda first)
- Replace with fresh oil

## Essential Oil Toxicity by Ingestion

### **If Accidental Ingestion Occurs**

**For Small Amounts (1-2 drops in child):**
1. Don't panic
2. Offer milk or carrier oil (helps dilute)
3. Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
4. Monitor for symptoms (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
5. Seek medical help if symptoms develop

**For Larger Amounts or High-Toxicity Oils:**
1. Call Poison Control immediately
2. Do NOT try to induce vomiting
3. Follow Poison Control instructions
4. Seek emergency care

**PREVENTION IS BEST:**
- Store ALL oils out of children's reach
- Use clearly labeled bottles
- Never tell children oils are "safe to eat"
- Supervise diffusion with young children

## First Aid for Common Issues

### **Skin Irritation from Oil**

**If Rash/Redness Appears:**
1. Immediately wash area with mild soap and water
2. Apply cool compress
3. Apply unscented lotion or coconut oil (dilutes remaining oil)
4. Do NOT apply more oils
5. Stop using that oil
6. Wait 24-48 hours before patch testing again

**Prevention:**
- Always patch test new oils
- Start with lower dilutions (0.5%)
- Monitor first 24 hours after application

### **Eye Contact**

**If Oil Gets in Eyes:**
1. Do NOT rub
2. Flush with cool water for 15+ minutes
3. Blink frequently
4. Remove contacts if present
5. Seek medical care if irritation continues

### **Inhalation Overexposure**

**If Someone Feels Dizzy/Headachy from Diffusion:**
1. Stop diffuser immediately
2. Open windows/doors for ventilation
3. Move to fresh air
4. Drink water
5. Rest
6. Use much smaller amount next time

### **Allergic Reaction (Rare)**

**Signs:**
- Persistent itching/hives
- Swelling of face/lips
- Difficulty breathing

**Action:**
- Stop using immediately
- Wash off thoroughly
- Call emergency services if difficulty breathing
- Seek medical care

## Testing New Oils Safely

### **"Patch Test" Protocol**

1. **Select Oil to Test:** Choose one new oil
2. **Dilute:** Mix 1-2 drops in small amount carrier oil
3. **Apply:** Place small amount behind ear (or inner wrist)
4. **Wait:** Observe for 24 hours
5. **Monitor:** Watch for redness, itching, burning, rash
6. **Result:** If no reaction, oil is likely safe for you

**For Children:**
- Use even more dilute patch (0.25-0.5%)
- Observe for 24-48 hours
- Monitor sleep/behavior that night

## When to Consult Healthcare Provider

### **Before Using Essential Oils, Ask Doctor If You:**

- Are pregnant or nursing
- Have been diagnosed with cancer
- Take blood thinners
- Have serious allergies
- Have liver/kidney disease
- Are taking medications
- Have skin conditions (eczema, psoriasis, etc.)
- Are giving to young children (under 3 years)

### **Seek Help If:**

- Persistent irritation develops
- Allergic reaction suspected
- Ingestion occurred
- Child experiences unusual symptoms
- Oil interacts with medications (unusual)
