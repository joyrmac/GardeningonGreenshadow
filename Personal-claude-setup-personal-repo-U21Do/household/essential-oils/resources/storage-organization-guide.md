# 🏠 Storage & Organization Guide
*How to Store & Organize Your 70+ Oil Collection*

---

## Golden Rules of Oil Storage

✅ **Dark glass containers** - Protect oils from light degradation
✅ **Cool location** - Below 70°F ideal; away from heat sources
✅ **Dark place** - Away from direct sunlight
✅ **Consistent temperature** - Avoid fluctuations
✅ **Tight caps** - Prevent oxidation
✅ **Organized system** - Easy to find what you need
✅ **Labeled clearly** - No guessing!

---

## ❌ Where NOT to Store Oils

❌ **Under sink** - Heat + humidity + temperature fluctuations
❌ **Above stove** - Direct heat = degradation
❌ **Windowsills** - Sunlight = oxidation
❌ **Bathrooms** (unless cool, dark shelf) - Humidity shortens shelf life
❌ **Plastic containers** - Oils degrade plastic over time
❌ **Unorganized** - Wasted time searching

---

## ✅ Best Storage Locations

**Option 1: Kitchen Cabinet**
- Cool, dark cabinet (not above stove)
- Away from direct heat
- Easy access during cooking/cleaning projects
- Example: Lower cabinet away from stovetop

**Option 2: Bedroom Closet**
- Cool, dark, undisturbed
- Easy access for evening/bedtime blends
- Good for personal oils
- Example: Shelf in Joy's bedroom closet

**Option 3: Bathroom Cabinet**
- If cool & dark (not above heater)
- Good for personal care oils
- Keep door closed to prevent humidity
- Example: Under-sink cabinet (if not over heating)

**Option 4: Dedicated "Oil Cabinet" or Shelf**
- Create special cabinet/shelf for all oils
- Organized, visible system
- Makes blending easier
- Example: Small bookshelf converted to oil storage

**Option 5: Small Cooler or Box**
- For summer heat (if room temp rises above 75°F)
- Portable for blending projects
- Optional, depending on climate
- Example: Small insulated box in cool closet

## 🏷️ Labeling System

### **What to Label:**

**On Each Bottle:**
1. Oil name (clearly!)
2. Brand
3. Date purchased or "best by" date
4. Any special info (KidSafe, photosensitive, etc.)

**Example Label:**
```
LAVENDER
Plant Therapy
Purchased: March 2026
✓ All Ages Safe
```

### **Label Creation Options:**

**Option 1: Printed Labels**
- Print label template
- Cut & apply with waterproof tape
- Professional looking
- Easy to update

**Option 2: Handwritten Labels**
- Permanent marker on tape
- Personal touch
- Quick to do
- Easy to update

**Option 3: Colored Tape System**
- Different colors = different categories
- Red = grounding, Blue = calming, etc.
- Quick visual reference
- No writing needed

### **Label Location:**
- Front of bottle (easy to read)
- OR back of bottle (if diffuser bottles look nice)
- Use waterproof labels (humidity-resistant)

## 🧴 Physical Cabinet Setup

### **Cabinet Style 1: Shelving Unit**

**Best For:** Large collection, family access, visual reference

**Setup:**
1. Small 3-4 shelf bookcase or cabinet
2. Each shelf = one category
3. Bottles arranged front-to-back (tallest back)
4. Label each shelf
5. Keep inventory sheet on top shelf

**Pros:**
- All oils visible
- Easy to find what you need
- Good for kids to learn
- Easy to take inventory

**Cons:**
- Takes up space
- Not ideal if you have dust/heat issues

---

### **Cabinet Style 2: Storage Box System**

**Best For:** Space-conscious, hidden storage, organized compartments

**Setup:**
1. Use divided storage boxes (craft organizers work great)
2. Each compartment = one category
3. Label each compartment
4. Store entire box in cool cabinet/closet
5. Keep inventory list with box

**Pros:**
- Compact
- Protected from light/dust
- Easy to move
- Organized into categories

**Cons:**
- Have to open box to see oils
- Harder for kids to browse

---

### **Cabinet Style 3: Drawer Organizer**

**Best For:** Compact collection, hidden storage, drawer space

**Setup:**
1. Use utensil/jewelry drawer organizers
2. Fit bottles into compartments
3. Use dividers to separate categories
4. Store in cool, dark drawer
5. Keep inventory on drawer exterior

**Pros:**
- Hidden from view
- Uses existing drawer space
- Compact & organized
- Dust protection

**Cons:**
- Takes up drawer space
- Hard to see all at once
- Not good for kid access

---

### **Cabinet Style 4: Wall-Mounted Shelf**

**Best For:** Visual display, easy access, creates aesthetic

**Setup:**
1. Mount floating shelf in cool, dark location
2. Arrange oils by color or category
3. Pair with decorative labels
4. Use shelf risers if desired
5. Keep inventory card nearby

**Pros:**
- Beautiful display
- Easy access
- Space-efficient
- Decorative element

**Cons:**
- Exposed to dust
- Needs dark location
- Not ideal for humid areas
- Kids can easily grab

## 🧴 Carrier Oil Storage

### **Where to Store Carrier Oils:**
- Cool, dark cabinet (same as essential oils)
- Can use same cabinet system
- Keep bottles tightly sealed
- Check for oxidation/rancid smell before use

### **Carrier Oil Shelf Life:**
- Sweet Almond Oil: 1 year
- Jojoba Oil: 1-2 years (longest)
- Coconut Oil: 2 years (solid at room temp, ok)
- Shea Butter: 1-2 years
- Grapeseed Oil: 1 year

## 💡 Pro Tips

✅ **Keep frequently used oils accessible** - Lavender, Frankincense at eye level
✅ **Store by season** - Keep seasonal blends together for easy rotation
✅ **Use small trays** - Group related oils on small tray for easy blending
✅ **Label clearly** - Worth the 5 minutes to make professional labels
✅ **Inventory matters** - Know what you have before reordering
✅ **Temperature stable** - Avoid cabinets that get warm/cool seasonally
✅ **Keep sealed** - Tight caps = longer shelf life
✅ **Rotate stock** - Older bottles in front, newer in back (FIFO method)
