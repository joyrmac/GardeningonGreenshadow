# ADHD-Friendly Weekly System
### How to Run Your Week Without Burning Out or Losing Track of Everything

---

## Why Traditional Planners Don't Work for You

Most productivity systems assume a brain that can:
- Hold multiple tasks in working memory
- Transition smoothly between activities
- Maintain consistent motivation over time
- Follow a plan without dopamine incentives

ADHD brains do none of these things reliably. So instead of fighting your wiring, this system works WITH it: short sprints, external scaffolding, minimal decisions, built-in recovery.

---

## The Weekly Structure

### The 3-Block Day

Instead of scheduling every hour, divide each day into three blocks. You only need to know: what's the focus of this block?

| Block | Time (Approximate) | Energy Level | Best For |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|---------|
| **Morning Block** | Wake – 12pm | Highest focus (usually) | Deep work, appointments, hard tasks |
| **Afternoon Block** | 12pm – 4pm | Dipping energy | Admin, errands, lighter tasks, kids' activities |
| **Evening Block** | 4pm – bedtime | Recovery mode | Family time, routines, winding down |

**Rule:** Assign ONE anchor task per block. Not five. One. If you finish it and have energy, great — bonus tasks are bonus. If you only do the anchor, you still won.

### The Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Theme | Why |
|-----|-------|-----|
| **Monday** | Reset & Plan | Set the week. Review calendar. Identify the 3 biggest things this week. |
| **Tuesday** | Deep Work | Your freshest day after reset. Tackle the hardest creative or business task. |
| **Wednesday** | Catch-Up | Whatever fell through Monday/Tuesday. Appointments. Admin. Emails. |
| **Thursday** | Creative Day | Content creation, writing, garden planning, spiritual practice — whatever feeds you. |
| **Friday** | Loose Ends | Light tasks. Finish what's open. Plan the weekend. Give yourself permission to coast. |
| **Weekend** | Family + Recovery | No guilt. Rest IS productive. One fun family thing. One thing just for you. |

**This is a template, not a prison.** If Wednesday turns into a creative day because that's where the energy is, follow the energy. The themes are guardrails, not walls.

## The Daily Minimum (Non-Negotiable 5)

When everything feels like too much, these five things keep the wheels on:

1. **Take your meds** (Escitalopram, Omeprazole)
2. **Move your body** (even 10 minutes — walk, stretch, dance in the kitchen)
3. **Feed yourself real food** (not just coffee and snacks)
4. **Do one task that moves something forward** (any task, any size)
5. **Set tomorrow's anchor tasks before bed** (3 blocks, 1 task each)

If you do these five things, the day counts. Everything else is extra credit.

## Task Management for ADHD Brains

### The Capture Habit

Your brain generates ideas and to-dos constantly — and forgets them just as fast. You need ONE place to capture everything. Not three apps. Not sticky notes. One.

**Options:**
- A single running note in your phone (Notes app, Google Keep, whatever you'll actually use)
- A physical notebook that lives in one spot
- A Monday.com board (since you already use it for Scout Media)

**The rule:** When something pops into your head, capture it immediately. Don't judge it, organize it, or decide if it's important. Just write it down. Organize it later during your Monday reset.

### The 2-Minute Rule

If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it now. Don't add it to the list. Just do it.

Examples: replying to a text, putting dishes in the dishwasher, sending a quick email, filing a document.

### The "Pick 3" Method

Every day, look at your capture list and pick **3 tasks** that would make the day feel productive. Write them on a sticky note or at the top of your phone notes. That's the day's list.

**Why 3?** It's ambitious enough to create momentum but small enough to actually finish. Finishing feels good. Feeling good creates more energy. More energy means you'll do more than 3 anyway.

### Body Doubling

ADHD brains often can't start tasks alone but work fine with someone else nearby. Options:
- Work alongside Sean (even on different tasks)
- Virtual body doubling (YouTube "study with me" streams, Focusmate)
- Take your laptop to a coffee shop
- Have a kid sit near you doing homework while you work

## The Monday Reset (30 Minutes)

This is the single most important habit in this entire system.

### The Checklist

1. **Review last week** (2 min)
   - What got done? Celebrate it.
   - What didn't? Move it forward or delete it.

2. **Check the calendar** (3 min)
   - What's scheduled this week? Appointments, school events, deadlines?
   - Block travel time for appointments (ADHD time blindness is real).

3. **Pick the Big 3** (5 min)
   - What are the 3 most important things to accomplish this week?
   - Write them somewhere visible. These are your north stars.

4. **Process the capture list** (10 min)
   - Go through everything you captured last week.
   - For each item: Do it (if < 2 min), schedule it, delegate it, or delete it.

5. **Set Monday's anchor tasks** (5 min)
   - What's the anchor for each of today's 3 blocks?

6. **Pick one reward** (5 min)
   - What's something you're looking forward to this week? Schedule it. Put it on the calendar. Protect it.

## Dealing With the Hard Days

### When You Can't Start Anything

This happens. It's not laziness — it's executive dysfunction. The start signal isn't firing.

**Tricks that help:**
- **5-minute timer:** Tell yourself you only have to do 5 minutes. Often, starting is the hardest part and momentum carries you.
- **Change your environment:** Move to a different room, go outside, work from a coffee shop.
- **Start with the body:** Take a walk, do 10 jumping jacks, take a shower. Physical movement can unstick the brain.
- **Lower the bar:** Instead of "clean the kitchen," try "put 5 dishes away." Instead of "write the blog post," try "open the document."
- **Pair it with dopamine:** Music, a podcast, a fun drink, a candle. Make the task more sensory.

### When You're Overwhelmed by the List

1. **Brain dump everything** onto one page — don't organize, just dump.
2. **Circle the 3 most urgent.** Urgent = someone is waiting or there's a deadline.
3. **Cross off anything that doesn't actually matter** (be ruthless).
4. **Pick one** from your circled three. Do that one.

### When You've Had a Bad Week

Bad weeks happen. They don't undo good weeks. They don't mean the system is broken. They mean you're human.

**The reset:**
- Don't review what went wrong (that's a shame spiral, not a strategy)
- Just do the Monday Reset as if last week didn't happen
- Pick your Big 3 for the fresh week
- Start again

## Household Task Distribution

You've got three kids with age-appropriate chores (see `Here's a comprehensive list of chores...pdf`). The key ADHD-friendly principles:

- **Visual chore charts** work better than verbal reminders (for you AND the kids)
- **Same time every day** for recurring tasks — routine reduces decision fatigue
- **Batch similar tasks** — all laundry on one day, all meal prep on one day
- **The "closing shift"** — a 15-minute family tidy before bedtime, every night, no exceptions
