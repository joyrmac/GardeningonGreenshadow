# ADHD-Friendly Budget System
### How to Manage Your Money Without Losing Your Mind

---

## Why Traditional Budgeting Fails ADHD Brains

Most budget systems assume you'll:
- Track every transaction daily
- Stick to 15 different categories
- Review spreadsheets weekly without losing interest
- Not impulse-buy when dopamine is low

ADHD brains do none of this consistently. So instead of fighting your wiring, this system uses automation, simplification, and visual cues to keep money on track with minimal daily effort.

---

## The 3-Account System

The simplest structure that actually works. Three accounts, three purposes.

| Account | Purpose | How It Works |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Bills Account** | Fixed monthly expenses | Paychecks deposit here. Rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance, subscriptions auto-pay from this account. This money is spoken for — don't touch it. |
| **Spending Account** | Daily/weekly variable spending | Transfer a set amount weekly (or biweekly on payday). Groceries, gas, fun money, kids' stuff. When it's empty, it's empty. |
| **Savings Account** | Emergency fund + goals | Auto-transfer a set amount on payday. Even $25/paycheck adds up. Out of sight, out of mind. |

### How to Set It Up

1. **List every fixed monthly bill** (see template below)
2. **Total them up** — that's what stays in the Bills Account
3. **Decide your weekly spending budget** — what's left after bills and savings
4. **Set up auto-transfers** on payday: bills stay, spending transfers, savings transfers
5. **Only look at the Spending Account day-to-day** — that's your real-time budget

The point: **you never have to wonder if you can afford something.** If it's in the Spending Account, you can spend it. If the Spending Account is low, you wait.

## Scout Media Business Finances

Running a business means separating personal and business money. Even if it's small right now, start clean.

### The Basics

1. **Separate bank account** for Scout Media (even a free checking account)
2. **All client payments go into the business account**
3. **Business expenses come out of the business account** (Blaze.ai, tools, ads, supplies)
4. **Pay yourself a set amount** monthly from business → personal

### Tax Rule of Thumb

Set aside **25-30% of business income** for taxes. Put it in a separate savings account and don't touch it. Self-employment tax is higher than you expect.

**Quarterly estimated taxes** are due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15. Set calendar reminders.

## The Financial Monday Reset (5 Minutes)

Add this to your weekly Monday Reset (see `../household/ADHD_Friendly_Weekly_System.md`):

1. **Check Spending Account balance** — How much is left this week?
2. **Check if any bills are coming up** in the next 7 days
3. **Log any Scout Media income received** this week
4. **Check savings account** — is auto-transfer working? (Don't obsess, just verify.)
5. **One sentence:** "This week, money is [tight/fine/comfortable]."

That's it. 5 minutes. Don't spiral into spreadsheets.

## Debt Strategy (If Applicable)

Two proven methods. Pick the one that matches your brain:

### The Snowball Method (ADHD-Recommended)
Pay minimum on everything, throw extra money at the **smallest balance** first. When it's paid off, roll that payment into the next smallest.

**Why it works for ADHD:** Quick wins. Dopamine from checking things off. Momentum builds.

### The Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)
Pay minimum on everything, throw extra money at the **highest interest rate** first.

**Why it's harder for ADHD:** The highest-interest debt might be the biggest balance, so it takes longer to see progress. Less dopamine.

**Recommendation:** Use the Snowball. The math difference is usually small. The motivation difference is huge.

## ADHD Money Traps (And How to Dodge Them)

| Trap | Why It Happens | The Fix |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **Impulse buying** | Low dopamine seeks a quick hit | 24-hour rule: add to cart, wait 24 hours, buy only if you still want it |
| **Subscription creep** | Signed up and forgot | Quarterly subscription audit — cancel what you haven't used in 30 days |
| **Avoiding the numbers** | Anxiety about money = avoidance | The 5-minute Monday check. Don't look at everything. Just the Spending Account. |
| **Hyperfocus spending** | New hobby = $300 in supplies overnight | Budget a "hobby fund" — $X/month for new interests. When it's gone, it's gone. |
| **Forgetting bills** | Out of sight, out of mind | Auto-pay EVERYTHING possible. Set phone reminders for manual ones. |
| **Lending money you don't have** | People-pleasing + poor boundary-setting | "I can't right now, but I hope things work out." You're not a bank. |
| **Emotional spending** | Retail therapy after a bad day | Name it: "I want to buy this because I feel ___." Then do one thing from your "What Helped" list (from symptom journal) instead. |

## Tools That Play Well With ADHD

| Tool | What It Does | ADHD Rating |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Greenlight** | Kids' allowance + chore tracking (you already use this) | High |
| **Copilot or Monarch Money** | Auto-categorizes spending, visual dashboards | High — set and forget |
| **YNAB (You Need A Budget)** | Zero-based budgeting with great app | Medium — powerful but requires engagement |
| **Simple spreadsheet** | Custom tracking | Low ADHD-friendliness, high control |
| **Cash envelopes** | Physical cash for categories | Medium — very visual and tangible, but inconvenient |

**The best system is the one you'll actually check.** If a full budgeting app feels like too much, just use the 3-Account System + the 5-minute Monday check. That's enough.
