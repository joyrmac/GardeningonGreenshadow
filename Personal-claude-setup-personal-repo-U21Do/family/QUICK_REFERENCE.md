## 🆘 CRISIS QUICK REFERENCE

### **Murphy Has a Fever (100.4°F or higher)**
1. Give acetaminophen or ibuprofen (age-appropriate dose)
2. Hydrate — water, electrolyte drinks, popsicles
3. Cool compress on forehead
4. Monitor 24 hours
5. **Call pediatrician if:** Fever >103°F, lasts >3 days, difficulty breathing, seems really sick

### **Murphy Is Having a Meltdown**
1. **Stay calm.** Don't match her energy.
2. **Validate:** "I hear you. You're really upset. I'm here."
3. **Physical outlet:** Punch pillow, run, stomp (Aries Moon needs release)
4. **Don't reason** with her during the meltdown
5. **After:** Let her recover, then briefly process
6. **See full guide:** `reference/Emergency_Quick_Reference.md`

### **Anxiety Spiral**
1. **5-4-3-2-1 grounding:** Name 5 things you see, 4 you touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste
2. **Box breathing:** In for 4, hold 4, out for 4, hold 4 (repeat 5x)
3. **Physical grounding:** Jump, cold water on face, heavy blanket
4. **What to say:** "You're safe. Your body is protecting you but you're okay."
5. **See full guide:** `reference/Emergency_Quick_Reference.md`

### **When a Kid is Sick**
1. One parent stays home
2. Skip chores (no allowance reduction)
3. No activities while contagious
4. Rest, quiet, light meals
5. Your expectations drop for everything that week
6. **See full guide:** `reference/Backup_Plans.md`

### **You're Overwhelmed**
1. Tell Sean: "I need a break. Can you handle dinner/bedtime?"
2. Protect Sunday rest time (sacred)
3. Simplify that week: easy meals, chores on hold
4. Ask for help (friend, family, order in)
5. **See full guide:** `reference/Backup_Plans.md`

## 💡 KEY PRINCIPLES

✅ **You don't have to be perfect.** You have to show up and try.

✅ **When things get hard, simplify.** Drop chores, order in, let screens happen.

✅ **Sunday rest is non-negotiable.** It's how the week works.

✅ **Each kid is different.** Treat them accordingly (different expectations, different approaches).

✅ **Kids need structure AND grace.** Rules matter. Flexibility matters too.

✅ **When in doubt, ask your pediatrician.** That's what they're there for.

✅ **You're the adult.** Modeling calm, vulnerability, problem-solving teaches them more than perfection.
