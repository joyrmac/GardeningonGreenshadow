## The Foundation

### **1. Daily Routines**
- **Morning:** 7:30-9:30 AM (wake, get ready, breakfast, out door)
- **After school:** 4:10-5:30 PM (pickup, snack, homework, prep)
- **Bedtime:** Murphy 8-9:30 PM, Birdie ~9 PM (consistent sequence, no negotiation)

### **2. Weekly Structure**
- **Mon/Sat:** Birdie's ballet (you get solo time)
- **Tues/Thurs:** Your yoga time (20-30 min)
- **Friday:** Lighter evening (week recovery)
- **Sunday:** House prep (9-10:30), your rest (1-3 PM), family sync (15 min after dinner)

### **3. Chore System**
- **Daily chores** = allowance (kids do them for living in house)
- **Paid jobs** = extra money (optional, kids choose)
- **Allowance:** Chad $50, Birdie $15, Murphy $10 per week
- **Paid jobs:** Chad $10-25, Birdie $5-15, Murphy $2-8 per job
- **Payment:** Friday or Sunday morning, cash, on time

### **4. Parenting Approach**
- **Chad:** Autonomy, minimal reminding, trust him to figure it out
- **Birdie:** Fairness, clarity, detailed expectations, acknowledge effort
- **Murphy:** Structure, celebration of wins, reassurance, outlet for big emotions

### **5. Crisis Response**
- **Medical:** Follow pediatrician protocols, trust your gut
- **Emotional:** Contain (don't suppress), validate, offer outlet, repair after
- **Schedule changes:** Simplify everything else that week
- **You're overwhelmed:** Tell Sean, protect Sunday rest, ask for help

## The Money System

### **How It Works**
1. Kids do daily chores → earn basic allowance
2. Kids have option to do extra paid jobs → earn more money
3. Payment on Friday or Sunday (cash)
4. Allowance can reduce if chores slip consistently (multiple times/week)

### **Allowance (Weekly)**
- Chad: $50 (room, own dishes, bathroom respect)
- Birdie: $15 (room, own dishes, table setup/cleanup, help Murphy)
- Murphy: $10 (room, own dishes, toy sorting, dust low surfaces)

### **Paid Jobs (Examples)**
- **Chad:** Yard work ($15-20), bathroom deep clean ($10), car wash ($15), vacuum ($8), laundry help ($5-10)
- **Birdie:** Bathroom clean ($8), decorative duty ($5), dust ($5), organize space ($8-10), her choice ($5)
- **Murphy:** Laundry help ($5), bathroom help ($3-5), organize drawer ($5), help Birdie ($3), special project ($5-8)

### **Seasonal Work**
- Yard cleanup (spring/fall) — Chad leads, others help
- Holiday decorating — all together ($10-20 each)
- Deep clean rooms — self ($15 each) — 2x/year

### **Tracking**
- Print chore chart tracking sheet each week
- Note who completed what
- Pay on schedule (consistency matters)

## The Behavior Approach

### **What We're Watching**
- **Chad:** Homework completion, follow-through, handling stress, autonomy
- **Birdie:** Perfectionism, fairness, self-doubt, confidence
- **Murphy:** Emotional regulation, meltdowns, perfectionism, joy

### **How We Respond**
- **Celebrate wins** — "I noticed you..."
- **Address struggles** — "What's getting in the way?"
- **Protect rest** — "You're overwhelmed. Let's simplify."
- **Repair relationships** — "I'm sorry. Let's figure this out together."

### **When to Get Help**
- Anxiety 3+ times per week
- Major behavioral change
- Talking about self-harm
- Can't function at school/home
- Extreme fear/worry

## The Support System

### **When Things Go Wrong**
1. **You're sick** → Sean takes full duty (or call in support)
2. **Kid is sick** → That parent stays home, chores skip, no activities
3. **Schedule changes** → Simplify everything else that week
4. **Meltdown** → Contain, validate, offer outlet, repair after
5. **You're overwhelmed** → Tell Sean, protect Sunday rest, ask for help

### **Your Resources**
- **Pediatrician:** Fever, behavior, health questions
- **School counselor:** School performance, social issues
- **Therapist:** If professional support needed
- **Partner (Sean):** Backup, support, co-parenting decisions

## Success Metrics

### **The System is Working When...**

✅ Kids know what's expected (they can tell you)
✅ Bedtime happens consistently (mostly)
✅ Chores get done without constant reminding
✅ Kids feel understood (they say "you get me")
✅ You have Sunday rest (protected, non-negotiable)
✅ Family connection happens (Sunday sync, shared activities)
✅ You're not yelling every day
✅ Kids handle failure with resilience
✅ Problems get resolved (not swept under rug)
✅ You can laugh together

### **Signs You Need to Adjust**

⚠️ Kids are confused about expectations
⚠️ Bedtime is a nightly battle
⚠️ You're yelling constantly
⚠️ Someone feels resentful
⚠️ You're not getting rest
⚠️ Problems escalate instead of resolve
⚠️ Kids are withdrawn or anxious
⚠️ You feel totally alone

**If you see these:** Simplify, reassess, adjust. This is normal.
