# Weekly Meal Planning Template

**Sunday 15-minute planning → Monday-Sunday execution.**

Organized, simple, manageable. No overthinking.

---

## How This Works

**Every Sunday (during your house prep time):**
1. Plan 5-6 dinners (skip 1-2 nights for leftovers or eating out)
2. List ingredients needed
3. Note any prep that can happen Sunday
4. Post on fridge
5. Done

**Each night:** Refer to plan, use Sunday prep, cook

## Sunday Meal Prep (15-30 Min)

**During your 9:00-10:30 AM house prep block:**

### **Protein Prep (if doing Monday taco night)**
- Cook ground beef for tacos (season, drain, cool, store)
- → Ready to reheat Monday 5:00 PM

### **Vegetable Prep (if doing roasted veggies/stir-fry)**
- Chop vegetables for the week (store in containers)
- Options: broccoli, carrots, peppers, onions, zucchini
- → Ready to use Tuesday-Thursday

### **Grain Prep (optional)**
- Cook rice for the week
- Cook pasta if using multiple nights
- → Reheat or serve cold

### **Slow Cooker Setup (if doing Sunday dinner OR Monday slow cooker meal)**
- Assemble slow cooker with protein, broth, veggies
- Let it cook all day
- → Ready for Sunday dinner or Monday lunch

### **Lunch Prep (optional)**
- Make sandwich fillings
- Prep salad components
- Hard boil eggs
- → Ready for school lunches

### **Snack Prep**
- Cut fruit
- Portion nuts/pretzels
- Make trail mix
- Cut veggies for dips

## Easy Weeknight Meal Ideas (30 Min or Less)

### **Sheet Pan Dinners (One pan, minimal cleanup)**
- Chicken + vegetables roasted (20 min)
- Sausage + peppers + potatoes (30 min)
- Fish + asparagus + lemon (20 min)
- Ground meat + veggies (mix everything, bake)

### **Pasta Nights (Boil pasta, use sauce)**
- Jarred marinara + ground meat
- Jarred pesto + chicken
- Olive oil + garlic + veggies + pasta
- Butter + parmesan + vegetables (carbonara-style)

### **Rice/Grain Bowls (Build-your-own)**
- Cook rice in advance
- Protein on side: chicken, tofu, beans, eggs
- Toppings: veggies, sauce, cheese
- Everyone assembles their own bowl

### **Taco/Burrito Night**
- Pre-cooked meat (Sunday prep)
- Warm tortillas
- Set out toppings: cheese, salsa, sour cream, lettuce, tomato
- Everyone makes their own

### **Slow Cooker (Set & Forget)**
- Pulled pork + rolls
- Chili
- Beef stew
- Chicken salsa
- Set in morning, cook all day

### **Breakfast for Dinner**
- Scrambled eggs + toast
- Pancakes or waffles
- Breakfast burritos
- Omelets with veggies

## Shopping Strategy

**Best for ADHD Brain:**
- **Order online:** Instacart, grocery pickup, Amazon Fresh
  - Less time in store = less sensory overload
  - Less impulse buying
  - Use template to list items
- **OR**: Send kids with list (Chad can handle this at 16)
- **OR**: Same store, same route, same time weekly (routine helps)

**Time:** Sunday morning during meal planning (30 min)
**Where:** Online or store (your choice)
**Pickup/Delivery:** Next day or same day

## Kitchen Setup for Easy Cooking

**Make Monday-Friday feasible:**

### **Pantry Must-Haves**
- Olive oil, butter, salt, pepper
- Garlic powder, Italian seasoning, cumin
- Pasta sauce, canned tomatoes
- Broth (chicken, veggie)
- Pasta, rice

### **Fridge Staples**
- Eggs
- Milk
- Cheese
- Fresh garlic/onion
- Butter
- Plain yogurt

### **Tools That Make It Faster**
- Sheet pans (at least 2)
- Large skillet (cast iron or non-stick)
- Cutting board & sharp knife
- Colander
- Large pot (pasta/soup)
- Slow cooker (set it Sunday AM)
- Instant read thermometer (chicken done in 165°F)

### **Time-Savers to Buy**
- Pre-cooked rotisserie chicken (quick protein)
- Pre-cut vegetables (if budget allows)
- Jarred sauces (marinara, pesto, salsa)
- Frozen vegetables (just as healthy, no waste)
- Pre-shredded cheese (easier, slightly more expensive)

## Fallback Plan (For Bad Weeks)

**When you're overwhelmed, behind, or burned out:**

- **Monday:** Delivery pizza

- **Wednesday:** Rotisserie chicken + rice + veggies
- **Thursday:** Spaghetti & jarred sauce
- **Friday:** Takeout
- **Saturday:** Breakfast all day
- **Sunday:** Whatever

**This is NOT failure.** Sometimes survival is the goal. Adapt.

## Using This Template

1. **Print the blank sheet** (see below)
2. **Sunday morning:** Pick 5-6 dinners from the ideas list
3. **Write them in:** Include any prep notes
4. **List ingredients:** Add to shopping list
5. **Post on fridge:** Family can see the plan
6. **Shop:** Sunday or use delivery
7. **Sunday prep:** Do any pre-cooking (meat, veggies)
8. **Week executes:** Follow the plan
