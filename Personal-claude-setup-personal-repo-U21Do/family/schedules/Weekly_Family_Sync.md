# Weekly Family Sync

**15-minute Sunday meeting to review the week, address issues, celebrate wins.**

---

## When & Where

- **Time:** Sunday dinner or after dinner (pick a consistent time)
- **Duration:** 15 minutes (set a timer)
- **Location:** Around the table, after food, everyone present
- **Optional:** Treats, candles, make it feel special

---

## The Structure (15 minutes)

### **1. Week Review (3 minutes)**
"How was your week? What was good?"

Go around: Chad, Birdie, Murphy, You, Sean (optional but nice)
- One sentence per person
- Just listening, no judgment
- Celebrate something each kid did

**Examples:**
- "I finished my project and it turned out good"
- "I helped Birdie and felt useful"
- "I did my chores all week without reminding"

### **2. What Didn't Work? (3 minutes)**
"What was hard this week?"

Ask if something caused stress/problems:
- Chore not getting done?
- Bedtime chaos?
- School stress?
- Family conflict?

**Rule:** This is about *understanding*, not punishing.
- "I see you had trouble with X. What got in the way?"
- Listen. Don't fix it yet.

### **3. This Week Ahead (3 minutes)**
"What's happening this week?"

Look at the calendar together:
- Activities
- School events
- Anything changing
- Big test/project?

**Set expectations:**
- "Monday's different because..."
- "We need to remember that..."

### **4. Adjustments & Logistics (3 minutes)**
"What do we need to do differently?"

Address:
- Chore shifts if needed (someone struggling?)
- Activity changes
- If someone's overwhelmed, ease up
- Celebrate: "You all did great with [thing]"

---

## Sample Sync (What It Sounds Like)

**You:** "Let's do our Sunday check-in. Chad, how was your week?"

**Chad:** "Okay. I got the yard done Saturday."

**You:** "Nice. That was responsible. Birdie?"

**Birdie:** "I did my chores and helped Murphy. But I was worried about my test."

**You:** "I noticed you studying hard. How'd it go?"

**Birdie:** "Okay, I think."

**You:** "Good effort. Murphy?"

**Murphy:** "I did my stuff! And I didn't cry at bedtime!"

**You:** "That's huge, Murphy. I noticed! [High five] That took real courage."

**Sean:** "Week was solid here. You all did your thing."

**You:** "Okay, anything that was hard? What didn't work?"

**Birdie:** "I had trouble with table cleanup because I was tired Wednesday."

**You:** "What was going on Wednesday?"

**Birdie:** "I had school stuff and ballet that day and felt overloaded."

**You:** "Okay, I get it. Wednesday's packed. Maybe we skip the table cleanup Wednesday for a bit, or I help you. How does that feel?"

**Birdie:** "Better."

**You:** "This week: Tuesday is a normal day, but Wednesday we have Murphy's friend over, and Saturday we have Birdie's morning ballet. Everyone okay with that?"

**All:** "Yeah."

**You:** "Cool. Awesome week, everyone. You all handled it. Keep going."

---

## Questions to Ask (if conversation stalls)

- "What was your favorite part of the week?"
- "What made you proud this week?"
- "What was frustrating?"
- "Did anyone notice something your sibling did well?"
- "Is there anything you need from me or the family?"
- "What are you looking forward to this week?"

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Don't make it a lecture
❌ Don't use it to scold or rehash problems
❌ Don't compare kids ("Why can't you be like Chad?")
❌ Don't let it go over 15 minutes
❌ Don't skip it because "nothing happened"

---

## Benefits

✅ Kids feel **heard** and seen
✅ Small issues get addressed before they blow up
✅ Family stays on the same page
✅ Kids learn to think about their own behavior/week
✅ You catch stress early (test anxiety, social issues, overwhelm)
✅ Celebration of wins (they need this)
