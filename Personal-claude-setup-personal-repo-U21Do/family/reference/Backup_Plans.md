# Backup Plans — When Things Don't Go as Planned

**Quick responses for common disruptions. Print this.**

---

## When a Kid is Sick

### **Who stays home?**
- **You stay home** (or Sean if you're unavailable)
- Call school/activities to notify

### **Chores during sick week?**
- **Skip them.** No allowance reduction.
- Let them rest and recover
- Resume next week

### **Activities/sports?**
- **Sit out** until fever is gone OR 24 hours without medication
- Don't return to activities if contagious
- Email coach/teacher with update

### **Schoolwork from home?**
- For longer absences, contact teacher for homework
- Don't stress about keeping up during acute illness
- Focus on getting better

### **Your workload during sick kid week:**
- Reduce expectations on EVERYTHING (meals, chores, activities)
- Simple meals, minimal prep
- Movies, rest, let it slide

---

## When YOU Are Sick

### **Kids still need care. Here's the framework:**

**If mildly sick:**
- Simplified meals (cereal, sandwiches, leftovers)
- Chores reduced but light ones still happen
- Kids watch TV / quiet activities
- Sean helps with pickups if possible
- You rest as much as you can

**If seriously sick:**
- Call in support: family, friend, or Sean takes full duty
- Kids understand "Mom's really sick, we're helping extra this week"
- Bare minimum routine only

## When Schedule Changes Unexpectedly

### **School closed (snow, emergency)?**
- Keep the kids' routine as close to normal as possible
- They still have meals, bedtime, quiet time
- Add one special activity (outside, movie, baking)
- Your work? Let your employer know ASAP

### **Activity gets cancelled (weather, conflict)?**
- Tell kids in advance: "Ballet's cancelled today"
- Use the freed-up time for something else (park, walk, family time)
- Don't fill it with extra screens without notice

### **Someone needs to be picked up early?**
- One of you shifts schedule to get them
- Don't spiral about disruption — life happens

---

## When You Need to Adjust Bedtime

### **Late evening event or travel?**
- Bedtime can shift 30 mins later for special events
- But return to normal routine the next night
- Don't let one late night become permanent

### **Daylight Saving Time?**
- Give kids 3-4 days to adjust
- Wake them at normal time, let bedtime shift gradually
- Expect some grumpiness during the adjustment

## When Someone Has a Major Meltdown

### **See:** `/parenting/Emergency_Quick_Reference.md` for detailed response

**Quick version:**
1. Stay calm
2. Contain the situation (safe space, remove audience)
3. Don't negotiate during the meltdown
4. Once calm, debrief
5. Move on (don't rehash)

## When You're Overwhelmed

### **Signs you need a break:**
- Yelling more than usual
- Everything feels impossible
- You can't remember what's on the schedule
- You want to hide

### **What to do:**
1. **Tell Sean:** "I need a break. Can you handle dinner/bedtime tonight?"
2. **Sunday rest is sacred:** Protect it fiercely
3. **Simplify:** One week of easy meals, chores on hold, minimal extras
4. **Ask for help:** Friend to take a kid for an afternoon, order in food, skip an activity

---

## When Kids Are Fighting

### **Minor squabbles:**
- "Work it out, but no yelling"
- Don't referee every conflict
- Natural consequences if someone gets hurt

### **Major fights:**
- Separate if needed
- Once calm, debrief separately first
- Then together if needed: "What happened? What do you each need?"

### **During an activity (ballet, school)?**
- Handle it after you get home
- Don't let it derail your day
- Process when everyone's calm

---

## When Money Gets Tight

### **Chores/allowance stays the same**
- Kids still have responsibilities
- But you might pause extra "paid jobs" temporarily
- Explain: "Things are tight right now. We're being careful with money"

### **Activities you can reduce:**
- Paid extracurriculars (temporarily)
- Meals out
- New clothes/toys
- Keep basics: school, necessities

---

## When You Miss a Payment (Allowance/Chores)

### **It happens. Here's the fix:**
1. **Pay as soon as you remember** (even if late)
2. **Explain:** "I messed up. Here's your payment. I'm sorry"
3. **Don't make excuses.** Kids need to know you follow through.
4. **Set a reminder** for next time (phone alarm, calendar)

---

## When a Kid Refuses to Do a Chore

### **First time:**
- "This is part of living in this house. When can you do it?"
- Give them autonomy on the timing

### **Second time:**
- "I noticed the dishes didn't happen. What's going on?"
- Listen for the real issue (overwhelmed? forgot? can't do it?)

### **Third time:**
- Reduce allowance that week (not drastically, just 25%)
- "When chores get done, allowance comes back next week"
- Don't rescue them; let them experience the consequence

## When Technology/WiFi Goes Down

### **Your schedule doesn't change**
- Chores still happen
- Meals still happen
- Bedtime still happens
- Kids go outside, read, play

### **Work disruption?**
- If you work from home, head to a coffee shop or library
- Tell kids it's temporary
- They figure out alternate entertainment

---

## When You Need to Cancel Plans

### **Tell everyone as soon as you know**
- "We can't do [activity] because [reason]"
- Don't over-apologize
- Offer a simple alternative if appropriate
- Move on
