# McNamara Family Chore Chart

**Who does what, when, and what "done" looks like.**

This system uses **daily allowance** (basic living chores) + **weekly/monthly paid jobs** (earning money). Consistency over perfection.

---

## The System Explained

### **Daily Chores (Allowance)**
These are part of living in the house. Not optional. Earn basic allowance for doing them consistently.

**Chad (Age 16):** $50/week allowance if daily chores stay consistent
**Birdie (Age 10):** $15/week allowance if daily chores stay consistent
**Murphy (Age 8):** $10/week allowance if daily chores stay consistent

### **Weekly/Monthly Paid Jobs (Extra Money)**
Optional jobs kids can take to earn additional money. Post on the fridge.

**Chad:** $10-25 per job depending on task
**Birdie:** $5-15 per job
**Murphy:** $2-8 per job

### **How It Works**
1. Kids do daily chores to keep allowance
2. If chores slip, allowance reduces (not all at once, but you track it)
3. Extra paid jobs are available — they choose to do them or not
4. Payment happens weekly (Fridays or Sundays)

## CHAD'S CHORES (Age 16)

### **Daily (Allowance: $50/week)**

| Chore | When | What "Done" Looks Like | Notes |
|-------|------|------------------------|-------|
| **Room maintenance** | Ongoing | Clothes in hamper (not floor), clear floor space, bed made, trash in bin | Weekly deep clean acceptable |
| **Own dishes** | After eating | Plate/cup/utensil washed and in drying rack (or dishwasher) | Not left in room |
| **Bathroom usage respect** | Daily | Leave it as you found it (rinse sink, wipe counter if you spill, close door quietly) | Shared space = shared responsibility |

### **Weekly Paid Jobs**

| Job | Pay | When | Frequency | What "Done" Means |
|-----|-----|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Yard work** | $15-20 | Flexible | 1-2x/week as needed | Mow lawn, edge, rake leaves, whatever yard needs |
| **Bathroom deep clean** | $10 | Weekend | 1-2x/month | Scrub toilet, clean mirror, wipe down shower, mop floor |
| **Car wash** | $15 | Flexible | 1x/month or as needed | Exterior wash, interior vacuum, windows clean |
| **Vacuum living areas** | $8 | Flexible | 1-2x/week as needed | Living room, hallway, upstairs thoroughly |
| **Laundry (help)** | $5-10 | Folding day | 1x/week | Fold and deliver to rooms |

### **Chad's Mindset Notes**
- **Autonomy matters.** Tell him what needs doing and when. Let him figure out HOW.
- **Don't micromanage.** If the yard gets mowed, you win. Doesn't matter what day.
- **One warning, then follow through.** If he agrees to a job, pay him. Builds trust.
- **He processes slowly.** Remind him about jobs in advance, don't surprise him.

| **Toy sorting** | 1-2x/week | Sort toys into correct bins (you define what goes where) | She's detail-oriented; this satisfies that |
| **Dust (low surfaces)** | 1x/week | Dust low shelves, TV stand (she can reach) | Give her a duster and show her |

### **Weekly Paid Jobs**

| Job | Pay | When | Frequency | What "Done" Means |
|-----|-----|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Help with laundry** | $5 | Folding day | 1x/week | Fold her own clothes + help with towels (you finish) |
| **Bathroom help** | $3-5 | Weekend | 1-2x/month | Wipe sink, trash (supervised), simple tasks |
| **Organize drawer/shelf** | $5 | Flexible | 1-2x/month | You assign a specific small space; she organizes it |
| **Help Birdie** | $3 | Flexible | As needed | Assist with a chore (doubles her pride, reduces Birdie's load) |
| **Murphy's special project** | $5-8 | Flexible | Monthly | Something she invents or that needs doing — pay for initiative |

### **Murphy's Mindset Notes**
- **She needs to feel SEEN and USEFUL.** Chores meet both needs.
- **Perfection paralysis is real.** "Good enough is done" language helps.
- **Physical tasks work best.** She needs to MOVE and have visible results.
- **Don't redo her work in front of her.** Stings her Leo. Fix it later if needed.
- **Praise the attempt, not just the result.** "You tried something new and finished it."

## Tracking & Payment

### **Allowance Payment Schedule**
- **Day:** Friday evening or Sunday morning
- **Method:** Cash (kids choose to spend or save)
- **Dock if:** Chores consistently not done
  - One slip? Don't dock (everyone has bad days)
  - Multiple slips in a week? Reduce by 25% that week
  - Back on track? Restore full amount next week

### **Paid Job Payment**
- **When:** Same day as allowance, or immediately after job completion if she wants immediate payment
- **How:** Cash or add to allowance total
- **Approval:** You inspect the job. Is it done? Pay. Is it half-done? "I see you started this. Let's finish together and I'll pay you half"

## Making It Work (Tips)

### **Post Visual Reminders**
- Laminate chore cards with pictures (especially for Murphy)
- Check marks or stickers for completed tasks
- Post the tracking sheet on fridge

### **Don't Nag**
- Remind once: "Hey, remember your dishes?"
- After that, natural consequences take over
- Dishes left = can't eat off clean dishes later
- Clothes on floor = can't find clean clothes

### **Be Flexible With School/Activities**
- Big test week? Reduce expectations temporarily
- Sick? No chores that week
- But don't let it become permanent exception

### **Connect It to Real Life**
- "Everyone in the house helps keep the house running"
- "You live here, you contribute"
- "This isn't punishment, it's how we live together"

### **Acknowledge Good Work**
- "I noticed you got all your chores done this week AND did two extra jobs. That's responsibility"
- "Your room is organized perfectly. You should see how good it looks"
- "You helped me a lot this week. That made things easier"

### **If Someone Struggles**
- **Chad:** Give more autonomy, less reminding. He'll figure it out.
- **Birdie:** Make it fair and let her help design the system
- **Murphy:** Make it short, give clear steps, celebrate completion

## What NOT to Do

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Use chores as punishment** | Teaches that chores = bad, not that chores = responsibility |
| **Reduce allowance without warning** | Breaks trust. Warn first: "If this continues, allowance will reduce" |
| **Redo their work in front of them** | Hurts them and teaches their work isn't good enough |
| **Compare kids' chores** | "Why isn't your room like Birdie's?" causes resentment |
| **Add chores as random punishment** | Makes them resent chores; use natural consequences instead |
| **Forget to pay** | You lose credibility immediately. Pay on time, every time. |
| **Assign chores they can't physically do** | Set them up for success, not failure |
