Print, laminate if possible, keep on fridge and in your phone notes.

## 🌡️ Murphy Has a Fever

**Fever = 100.4°F (38°C) or higher**

### Immediate Actions
1. **Give acetaminophen or ibuprofen** (check dosing for her age/weight)
   - Acetaminophen (Tylenol): Every 4-6 hours, max 5 doses/day
   - Ibuprofen (Advil): Every 6-8 hours, max 4 doses/day
2. **Hydrate** — Water, electrolyte drinks, popsicles, soup broth
3. **Cool compress** on forehead if feverish (NOT ice baths)
4. **Monitor for 24 hours**

### When to Call Pediatrician
- Fever **above 103°F (39.4°C)**
- Fever **lasts more than 3 days**
- **Difficulty breathing** or persistent cough
- **Severe headache** or stiff neck
- **Rash** that doesn't blanch (turn white) when you press it
- **Lethargy** or unresponsiveness
- **She seems really sick** (trust your gut)

### Comfort Care
- Keep her home (no school/activities)
- Quiet, cool environment
- Light pajamas, light blanket
- Don't overdress her
- Movies, rest, minimal demands

### Your Mindset
Fevers are your body's way of fighting infection. Fever itself is not dangerous (unless extremely high). You're managing comfort, not the fever. Breathe.

## 😭 Murphy Is Having a Major Meltdown

**Signs: Yelling, crying, throwing things, complete dysregulation, seems out of control**

### In the Moment (DO THIS FIRST)

**1. Contain, don't suppress**
- Stay calm (or fake it)
- "I hear you. You're really upset. I'm right here."
- Don't say "calm down" — it invalidates
- Don't match her energy

**2. Physical safety first**
- If throwing things: "You're not allowed to throw. Let's find another way."
- Remove dangerous objects
- Don't escalate to power struggle

**3. Don't engage in logic**
- "I know this doesn't make sense right now"
- Don't try to reason with her
- Don't lecture

**4. Offer outlet**
- "Want to punch this pillow?"
- "Want to run really fast?"
- "Want to stomp?"
- Physical release helps Aries Moon

**5. Physical comfort (if she wants it)**
- "Want a hug?" (if she'll accept)
- Hand on back
- Just presence if she won't be touched

### After the Peak (10-30 min later)

**6. Let her recover**
- She may collapse, cry, fall asleep
- This is normal. The release is GOOD.
- Don't ask her to explain it yet

**7. LATER (not immediately), process briefly**
- "That was a big one. What happened?"
- "When did you start feeling that way?"
- "What do you need right now?"

**8. Connect**
- She'll want to reconnect after. Let her.
- Even if you're not fully recovered, let her repair
- Quick hug, validation, move forward

### What NOT to Do
- ❌ Yell back
- ❌ Isolate her ("go to your room")
- ❌ Shame her ("what's wrong with you?")
- ❌ Withdraw affection
- ❌ Compare to siblings ("why can't you be like Birdie?")

### Your Stress Level After
- **You're depleted.** Meltdowns are exhausting for you.
- Take 5 minutes alone after. Breathe.
- This is NOT failure. This is her processing.
- You're not doing anything wrong.

## 😰 Murphy (or Any Kid) Is Having an Anxiety Spiral

**Signs: Racing thoughts, can't slow down, catastrophizing, "what if" loops, panic, physical symptoms (stomach ache, chest tightness)**

### Immediate Grounding (Pick One)

**5-4-3-2-1 Technique**
- "Name 5 things you see"
- "Name 4 things you can touch"
- "Name 3 things you hear"
- "Name 2 things you smell"
- "Name 1 thing you taste"
This interrupts the anxiety loop.

**Breathing (Box Breathing)**
- Breathe in for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Breathe out for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Repeat 5 times
(Show her, do it together)

**Physical Grounding**
- Cold water on face
- Jump up and down
- Press feet hard into ground
- Hold ice cube (brief)
- Heavy blanket (weighted blanket if you have one)

**Moving Her Body**
- Walk around the block
- Do jumping jacks
- Run in place
- Dance to music
- Physical movement burns off nervous energy

### What to Say
- "Your body is trying to protect you, but you're safe right now"
- "The worry is lying. Here's what's actually true: ___"
- "Let's slow this down. Here's what we can do: ___"
- "I'm here. You're not alone in this"
- "This feeling will pass. It always does"

### After She's Regulated
- Talk about what triggered it
- Problem-solve if there's something real to solve
- If it's catastrophizing, gently reality-test: "What's the actual likelihood of ___?"
- Don't dismiss the worry, but help her reality-check

### When to Get Professional Help
- **Anxiety happens 3+ times per week consistently**
- **It's interfering with school, friends, sleep**
- **Physical symptoms are severe** (chest pain, difficulty breathing that seems medical)
- **You can't calm her down** and it's escalating

**Talk to pediatrician about:** Evaluation, possible therapy, possible medication if it's severe

### Your Role
You're not her therapist. You're her anchor. Calm presence, validation, grounding tools. That's enough.

## 🤬 Murphy Is Being Defiant/Disrespectful

**Signs: Talking back, refusing to listen, being rude, rolling eyes, arguing**

### Immediate Response

**1. Stay calm (critical)**
- Her defiance triggers your defensiveness
- Take one breath before responding
- You're the adult. Act like it.

**2. Name what's happening**
- "I see you're upset with me"
- "I hear that you disagree"
- Don't shame the emotion

**3. One clear boundary**
- "I'm not going to be spoken to that way"
- "We can talk about this, but not like that"
- "I need you to speak respectfully"

**4. Give her a choice (when possible)**
- "You can _____ or _____. Your choice"
- Gives her agency (helps Aries Moon)
- Makes it not about power struggle

**5. Disengage if it escalates**
- "I've said what I need to say. We can talk about this when we're both calm"
- Walk away
- Don't keep arguing

### After She Settles

**6. Repair conversation**
- "I got frustrated too. I'm sorry for how I handled that"
- Acknowledge her side: "I hear that you felt ___"
- Problem-solve together: "What should we do differently next time?"

### What NOT to Do
- ❌ Match her defiance with yelling
- ❌ "Because I said so"
- ❌ Taking it personally (she's testing boundaries, not rejecting you)
- ❌ Bringing up past incidents ("You always ___")
- ❌ Comparing to siblings

### Remember
Defiance in kids is normal. She's testing where the boundaries are. Hold the boundary, stay kind, repair after. That's the whole dance.

## 😴 Murphy Won't Sleep / Bedtime Is a Battle

**See: `/schedules/Murphy_Bedtime_6Week_Plan.md`** for the full gradual approach

### Emergency Bedtime (Tonight, She Won't Sleep)

**1. Drop expectations**
- You're not "winning" bedtime tonight
- Goal: Get her to bed safely, you keep your sanity
- Tomorrow you reset

**2. Shortened routine**
- Pajamas → teeth → quick hug → bed
- No book, no long conversation
- 10 minutes max

**3. Offer tools**
- Weighted blanket
- Audiobook or calm podcast
- Night light she controls
- A "worry box" (she puts worries in it, picks them up tomorrow)

**4. You leave**
- "I know you're not sleepy. That's okay. Stay in bed. I'm leaving now."
- Don't stay with her
- One check-in in 10 min if you hear noise

**5. Take care of YOU**
- You're stressed. That's valid.
- This is temporary
- You're not failing

### Next Day: Start the Plan
Follow `/schedules/Murphy_Bedtime_6Week_Plan.md` — systematic approach over 6 weeks to independence

### Long-Term: Talk to Pediatrician If
- Sleep issues persist despite consistent routine
- She's having nightmares or night terrors
- You suspect ADHD, anxiety, or OCD
- She's waking multiple times/hour

## 🤒 One of the Older Kids (Chad/Birdie) Is Sick

### General Protocol
1. **Fever/illness:** Same as Murphy (fever dosing may differ by age/weight)
2. **Keep them home** from school if:
   - Fever without improvement after 24 hours
   - Vomiting
   - Persistent cough
   - Generally unwell
3. **Call pediatrician** if symptoms worsen or don't improve in 3 days
4. **Comfort care:** Rest, hydration, minimal demands

### For Chad (16)
- He'll likely just want to be left alone
- Check in: "Do you need anything?" — then respect his answer
- Bring water/food to his room
- No pressure to be social
- Let him sleep lots

### For Birdie (10)
- She'll want connection and reassurance
- Sit with her, bring comfort items
- Light activities (audiobooks, puzzles, movies)
- Reassure about fairness: "You'll feel better soon"
- Don't make her feel like a burden

## 🚨 Actual Medical Emergency

**Call 911 if:**
- Difficulty breathing or gasping
- Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness
- Choking
- Severe injury or bleeding
- Chest pain
- Poisoning or overdose
- Severe allergic reaction
- Seizure
- Severe burns
- Head injury with any neurological symptoms

**Don't wait. Call 911. Let them assess.**

## 💊 Medication Dosing Quick Ref

**For Murphy (Age 8, ~60-70 lbs):**
- **Acetaminophen (Tylenol):** 15 mg/kg per dose = ~450-500 mg (check bottle for exact dosing)
- **Ibuprofen (Advil):** 10 mg/kg per dose = ~300 mg (check bottle)

**Check the bottle for YOUR CHILD'S weight.** Dosing varies.

**For older kids:** Follow package or pediatrician guidance

## When to Call Pediatrician (Not 911)

**Same day or next morning:**
- Fever above 103°F or lasting 3+ days
- Persistent vomiting/diarrhea
- Ear pain, throat pain, rash
- Persistent cough
- Anything that feels "off"

**Within a few days:**
- Minor illness stuff that's not improving
- Questions about medication
- Behavioral concerns

**Trust your gut.** If you think she needs to be seen, call. Better to be safe.

## Your Crisis Mindset

- **You don't have to have all the answers.** Call the pediatrician.
- **You're not failing if she's sick.** Illness happens.
- **You're not failing if there's a meltdown.** Emotions happen.
- **Breathe. Most things pass.** Fevers go down, meltdowns end, anxiety spirals settle.
- **You know your kid. Trust yourself.**

**Pediatrician's office is closed? After-hours line exists. Use it.**

**You're overwhelmed? Call Sean. Ask for backup.**

**You don't know what to do? That's what pediatricians are for. Call.**
