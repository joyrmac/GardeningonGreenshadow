## Celebrating Growth (Important!)

### **Ways to acknowledge wins:**
- **Notice it out loud:** "I saw you handle that frustration without melting down. That's growth."
- **Tell others:** "Chad got his project done early. Responsibility."
- **Small rewards:** Extra screen time, choice of dinner, later bedtime on Friday
- **Weekly callout:** During Sunday sync, specifically celebrate one win per kid

---

## When to Adjust Your Approach

**If a kid is struggling more:**
- Reduce expectations temporarily
- Check: Are they sick? Anxious? Overwhelmed?
- Talk to them: "I notice things feel hard right now. What's going on?"
- Adjust chores/schedule if needed
- Consider if professional support is needed (therapist, tutor, etc.)

**If something's working really well:**
- Keep doing it
- Don't change what's not broken
- Acknowledge it's working

## Red Flags to Take Seriously

- Sudden major change in behavior (positive or negative)
- Talking about hurting themselves or others
- Withdrawal from activities they loved
- Extreme anxiety or depression symptoms
- Physical symptoms (stomachaches, headaches without cause)

**If you see these: Talk to a professional. Don't wait.**
