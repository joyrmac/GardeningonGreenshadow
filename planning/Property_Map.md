## Orientation

- **Property:** Almost a full acre, Fuquay-Varina NC, Zone 7b borderline 8a
- **House:** In the middle of the property — yard wraps around all four sides
- **Back door faces east** — when you stand at the back door looking out, north is to your left, south is to your right

## Boundaries

- **North** (left when facing east): wooden fence
- **South** (right when facing east): wooden fence
- **East** (back, far from house): brick wall — the most distant boundary from the house
- **West** (front): street/sidewalk

## Zones in Detail

### 1. Front Yard (West of House)
- **Boundaries:** West wall of the house to the street/sidewalk
- **Existing features:** Twin Beds (right bed = Bird Feeder Maple Bed with Japanese maple + Autumn Joy + Creeping Jenny, planted May 1, 2026)
- **Future:** **'Winter King' Hawthorn** (Celtic threshold tree, fall 2026 planting)

### 2. Wildwood (North Side)
- **Boundaries:** North fence on top; house on west side; Fernwood on east side; back yard / Edgeworth strip on south side
- **What's there:** The main fenced garden — most production beds live here
  - Bloomveil (cut flower garden)
  - Honeyglow Patch (pollinator bed)
  - Tomato Circle / Potion Patch (medicinal C-shape, Phase 3 direct sow May 3)
  - Spiral Herb Garden (sacred geometry — French Provençal heritage)
  - Frog Pond ("Alchemist's Pond")
  - 8 veggie beds
  - Corn Circle
  - Pumpkin patch
  - Watermelon patch

### 3. Fernwood (NE Corner)
- **Size:** ~40 × 40 ft, with arc shape extending out from the corner
- **Light:** North-facing, mostly shade
- **Existing features:** Hostas (3 varieties), hardy ferns, hellebores, foxglove, bleeding heart, lily of the valley, joe pye weed, goldenrod, oakleaf hydrangea, bluebells, **ginkgo at SW corner** (Fernwood's western boundary)
- **Trees lining fence/wall:** Mimosa + various others
- **Future:** Northern Sea Oats (from Fireflies Forever collection — see redistribution below)

### 4. Back Yard (East of House)
- **Boundaries:** House's east wall to the brick east wall; ~100+ ft deep
- **Lawn:** Renaturalizing — just mowed, no chemical treatment, no overseeding. This is intentional and ancestrally valuable (free plantain, daisy, clover, chickweed, johnny jump-ups, mosses)
- **Defined features:**
  - **Gloam:** Just east of back door (~10 ft), under the **full-grown Southern Magnolia** (evergreen, big tree). Currently planned as "Fireflies Forever" shade garden — see notes below for revised plant list (most original plants didn't fit dry magnolia shade)
  - **Cat Garden:** Middle of back yard (~100 ft from door), ~10 ft east of the Gloam's southern magnolia. Center feature is a **tulip magnolia** (deciduous, with ~30 sprouts being managed)
  - **Back-yard bird feeder tree + Jungle Pocket:** A tree with bird feeders sits inside a brick raised bed/wall structure. Wrapping the outside of that brick wall is **The Jungle Pocket** — a curved tropical bed (6–7 ft deep × 12 ft long, ~60–75 sq ft) featuring Basjoo Banana, Elephant Ears, Hardy Hibiscus, Cannas, and the existing Bird of Paradise containers. Replaces 4 existing bushes. See `garden-beds/12-jungle-pocket/Jungle_Pocket.md`

### 5. South Side / Southeast (the Ritual / Burning Side)

Listed roughly east → west along the south part of the property:

- **Wayward Meadow:** SE corner. 30–50 ft west of east wall, halfway up the east wall. Northern boundary = the southern magnolia (Gloam). Prairie naturalization. **Future 'Brodie' Cedar at the north edge** (between Cat Garden and Southern Magnolia)
- **Fire Pit:** Between the shed and Wayward Meadow / SE corner, on the south fence side. Wheel-of-the-Year fires, ancestral burning location
- **Shed:** On the south fence, in the back yard area
- **Swingnook:** Between the Fire Pit and Edgeworth Corner. Pergola, swing bench, fern groundcover. The shaded retreat
- **Edgeworth Corner:** Against the screen room (south side of house). Sunny bed (10×15 ft) + shade bed (6×4 ft against the screen room wall). Pollinator garden with dry creek pathway

> **Ritual flow:** Cedar (north edge of Wayward) → Fire Pit (south of Wayward) is a short walk. Cut juniper, walk south, burn it. This is intentional ancestral architecture per the Ancestral Herb Garden plan.

## The Renaturalizing Lawn (Back Yard Open Space)

The mowed-but-untreated lawn between the back door and the Cat Garden is **already an ancestral plant zone** without any deliberate planting:

| Plant likely present | Tradition | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Plantain (*Plantago* spp.) | All four traditions | Universal healing herb — wound poultice, edible greens |
| Daisy (*Bellis perennis*) | Celtic | Healing, innocence, transformation |
| White Clover | All | Bee forage, nitrogen fixer |
| Chickweed | Celtic | Edible salad, gentle healing |
| Johnny Jump-Ups | Celtic (fairy lore) | Already self-seeding across the property |
| Mosses | Universal | Firefly habitat, soft ground texture |
| Self-heal (*Prunella*) | Celtic | Wound herb (if it shows up) |

**Optional:** Stop mowing a small section to let plantain + self-heal grow tall enough for harvest. Otherwise, just keep mowing untreated — the meadow ecosystem builds itself.
