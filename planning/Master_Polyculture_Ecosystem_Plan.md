# Master Polyculture Ecosystem Plan

> **The Vision:** A vegetable garden that's equally productive, ecological, and witchy. Everything feeds everything else. No priorities—just everything.

---

## The Core Philosophy

You're not building separate gardens. You're building **one integrated living system** where:

- **Productivity & Witchiness** are woven into the ecosystem design (tomatoes, three sisters, medicinal herbs, cut flowers, tea plants)
- **Ecological Health** is the foundation (pollinators, beneficial insects, soil building, native plants, companion relationships)
- **Evolution & Improvement** happen naturally (self-seeding, perennial establishment, yearly deepening)
- **Low Maintenance** emerges from good design (companion plants handle pest control, nitrogen fixation, soil building — you just guide it)

---

## The Three New Herbs You Want

### **Astragalus** (Protective, Long-Game Investment)
- **What it is:** Perennial adaptogen herb (immune support, used in TCM)
- **Your role:** Plant it now, let it establish 2-3 years, then harvest roots forever
- **Where:** The Gloam (medicinal garden) or Potion Patch as a structural perennial
- **Ecological bonus:** Delicate flowers attract pollinators
- **Witchy correspondences:** Protection, resilience, long-term planning, grounding
- **Growing notes:** Needs good drainage, minimal fuss once established

### **Fenugreek** (Quick Payoff, Tea Habit)
- **What it is:** Annual herb, fast-growing, self-seeding
- **Your role:** Plant in spring, harvest seeds/leaves by summer, let some self-seed for next year
- **Where:** Potion Patch (fits front/edge zone), Wildwood's medicinal area, or scattered in The Gloam
- **Your tea:** Seeds are literally what you brew — no processing needed
- **Ecological bonus:** Attracts pollinators, nitrogen-fixing legume (feeds soil)
- **Witchy correspondences:** Abundance, nourishment, grounding, manifestation
- **Growing notes:** Prolific self-seeder — managed easily by selective harvesting

### **Nettle** (Nutritional Powerhouse, Contained Safely)
- **What it is:** Perennial, spreads aggressively, SO worth containing
- **Your role:** Grow in dedicated pot (5-gal minimum), harvest year-round
- **Where:** Potted beside the herb spiral, near Wildwood kitchen path, or greenhouse edge
- **Your tea:** Unlimited nourishing supply — harvest top 6" anytime
- **Ecological bonus:** Host plant for butterfly larvae, deep roots mine nutrients
- **Witchy correspondences:** Protection, grounding, women's health, resilience, wild power
- **Growing notes:** Pot prevents takeover, moisture-loving, basically indestructible

7. Compost → feeds Bloomveil, The Gloam, Fernwood, everywhere
8. System stabilizes and requires less work each year

## Planting Calendar: What Goes Where & When

### **ASTRAGALUS** (Perennial, Protective)
| Season | Action | Location |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Late March** | Buy as small start or seed indoors in Feb | The Gloam (middle row) or Potion Patch (back section) |

| **Year 2-3** | Grows larger, flowers appear (pollinators love) | Leave in place to establish deep roots |
| **Year 3+** | Optional: gentle root harvest (take some, leave some) | Dig in fall, replant side roots to spread |

### **FENUGREEK** (Annual, Abundance)
| Season | Action | Location |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Early April** | Direct sow seeds (don't transplant, roots don't like it) | Potion Patch (front/edge), The Gloam, scattered in Wildwood |
| **May-June** | Thin to 6-8" spacing, begins rapid growth | Sun, moderate water |
| **July-Aug** | Harvest leaves fresh for tea (anytime), watch for seed pods | Pods dry on plant, seeds inside ready in late Aug |
| **Late Aug** | Harvest mature seed pods, dry indoors, thresh seeds | Seeds = your tea supply for next 6 months |
| **Let some self-seed** | Don't deadhead everything — let 10-20% mature + drop | Expect volunteer crop next spring |
| **Year 2+** | Self-seeded volunteers appear in spring (thin if crowded) | Repeat above, increasingly light touch |

### **NETTLE** (Perennial, Nutritional Powerhouse)
| Season | Action | Location |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Anytime spring-summer** | Buy small nettle start (often sold as weed!) or start from seed | Dedicated 5-gal pot (critical!) |
| **Fill pot** | Use good potting soil + compost mix, keep moist | Place near kitchen for easy access |
| **May onward** | Harvest weekly: pinch off top 6" (includes tender leaves) | Endless supply, regenerates after each harvest |
| **Year 1** | Gets established, no plant stress | Keep pot moist (not waterlogged) |
| **Year 2** | Root-bound, thriving, consider second pot if desired | Divide in spring (separate roots with knife, repot sections) |
| **Ongoing** | Never needs fertilizer, nutrient miner (deep roots pull minerals) | Basically indestructible in pot |

## The Companion Web: What Feeds What

### **Nitrogen Fixation (Free Fertilizer!)**
- **Three Sisters corn circle:** Beans fix nitrogen for corn + squash
- **Fenugreek:** Legume, adds nitrogen to surrounding soil
- **Clover + speedwell:** "Weeds" that fix nitrogen (let them stay in paths/edges)
- **Result:** You never buy fertilizer; nitrogen cycles endlessly

### **Pollinator Magnet Plants**
Plant these throughout to keep pollinators visiting veggies:
- **Borage** (scattered in Potion Patch + other beds) = bees love it
- **Calendula** (The Gloam + Potion Patch) = bees + beneficial wasps
- **Chamomile** (spreads naturally) = tiny bee favorite
- **Echinacea** (The Gloam + perennials) = iconic pollinator flower
- **Yarrow** (The Gloam + scattered) = beneficial wasp attractor
- **Cut flowers** (cosmos, zinnias, sunflowers, dahlias) = constant pollinator visitors
- **Bee balm, hyssop** (medicinal beds) = native bee magnets
- **Fenugreek flowers** (before seed production) = attracting insects
- **Astragalus flowers** (delicate, spring blooms) = early pollinators

**Result:** Pollinators that visit Potion Patch medicinals cross to tomatoes + peppers + squash → natural pest control + pollination

### **Pest Control Companions**
| Problem | Solution | How It Works |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Tomato hornworms | Basil + beneficial wasps | Basil disrupts pests, wasps parasitize eggs |
| Nematodes in soil | Marigolds | Toxins repel nematodes, turned under at end of season |
| Aphids on veggies | Nasturtiums (trap crop) | Pests prefer nasturtiums to veggies, sacrifice the trap crop |
| Slugs + snails | Frog pond in herb spiral | Frogs eat hundreds of slugs/snails nightly |
| General pests | Borage + beneficial wasps + hover flies | Create habitat for beneficial insects |

### **Soil Building Companions**
| Plant | What It Does | Where |
|-------|------------|-------|
| **Marigolds** | Repel pests + add organic matter when turned under | Tomato circle + scattered in Wildwood |
| **Legumes** (beans, fenugreek, clover) | Fix nitrogen | Corn circle + edges + Fenugreek area |
| **Comfrey (if added)** | Deep roots bring up minerals, accumulator | Herb spiral or edge (optional) |
| **Fallen leaves** | Natural mulch + slow decomposition feeds soil | Don't rake, mulch beds instead |
| **Spent plants** | Compost in place over winter = nutrient return | Leave + mulch instead of removing |

## Year-Round Maintenance (The Rhythm)

### **Spring (March-May)**
- Plant Astragalus, Fenugreek, tomato companions
- Succession-plant lettuce/carrots every 2 weeks
- Direct sow cut flowers, medicinals
- Pot up Nettle
- Watch for self-seeded volunteers (thin if needed)

### **Summer (June-August)**
- Weekly deadheading (high-touch plants like Yarrow)
- Harvest Fenugreek seeds as mature
- Weekly Nettle harvest
- Continuous tomato/veggie harvesting
- Weekly cutting of cut flowers
- Water medicinal beds during drought
- Thin seedlings as needed

### **Fall (September-November)**
- Let self-seeders mature (no deadheading)
- Harvest medicinal roots/seeds at peak
- Leave seed heads standing (overwintering insects)
- Collect fallen leaves (mulch, don't bag)
- Plant garlic + spring bulbs
- Compost spent plants in place (slow winter decomposition)
- Document successes + what to change

### **Winter (December-February)**
- Plan next season
- Nettle pot might need dividing in spring
- Minimal watering
- Prune perennials
- Review + update this plan
