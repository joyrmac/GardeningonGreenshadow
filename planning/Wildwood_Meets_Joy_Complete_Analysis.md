## Your Health + Wildwood

### ADHD: Sensory-Rich, Dopamine-Hit Tasks

**What ADHD needs:** Immediate, sensory, engaging tasks with quick wins. Not long-term plans (your brain hates those).

**How Wildwood serves this:**

✓ **Short, defined tasks by bed:** "Pick cucumbers today" vs. "maintain the garden." Specific, sensory, done.
✓ **Color zones for focus:** Each bed has its own color story, so you know what you're looking at.
✓ **Tactile work:** Trellis-training cucumbers, harvesting melons, hand-watering companions.
✓ **Succession planting:** Every 2-3 weeks you're planting sunflowers. Dopamine hit: something new is starting.
✓ **New melon/cucumber bed:** A brand-new system to learn, organize, and obsess over (in the good way).

**Specific tasks that hit ADHD brain:**
- Water melon bed (sensory: feel soil moisture, smell water, see green leaves darken)
- Train cucumber tendrils on trellis (manual, engaging, visible progress)
- Hand-pollinate flowers (tool use, close attention, immediate result)
- Harvest borage for edible flowers (pretty, useful, done in 10 min)

### Anxiety: Grounding + Nervous System Regulation

**What anxiety needs:** Grounding (literally in dirt), sensory input, control, safety.

**How Wildwood serves this:**

✓ **Three hidden solitude zones with water views:** Pisces Moon needs this. Water + quiet = nervous system rest.
  - Thinking Chair (west, near Alchemist's Pond) — listen to fountain, watch water
  - Maker's Corner (east, near new melon bed) — create/sketch with plants nearby
  - Story Spot (quiet path) — journal, write, sit
✓ **Barefoot paths:** Bare feet in soil/grass = literal grounding
✓ **Water feature (frog pond):** Hearing moving water = parasympathetic nervous system activation
✓ **Lavender + chamomile + lemon balm everywhere:** Your anxiety herbs are growing, ready to brew
✓ **Mint expansion:** Spearmint + peppermint + chocolate mint tea available at all times

**The mechanism:** You're anxious, you walk to your Thinking Chair, you sit with a mug of fresh spearmint tea (from the Mint Bed), you listen to the frog pond fountain, you feel the earth under your feet, your nervous system settles. Not theoretical — built into the design.

### PTSD: Agency + Control + Safe Spaces

**What PTSD needs:** Feeling safe, having control, places to retreat, knowing you get to decide.

**How Wildwood serves this:**

✓ **Hidden spaces:** No one can see you in your Thinking Chair or Maker's Corner. You control who has access.
✓ **Three garden zones:** You own three different sensory experiences. You choose which one you need today.
✓ **Solitude time:** Scheduled garden work is YOUR time. You get to decide what happens here.
✓ **Frog pond:** A place to control water, create a mini-ecosystem, make it your sanctuary
✓ **Raised beds:** Everything is contained. Nothing's spreading into places you didn't plant it.

**The mechanism:** PTSD needs to feel that the present moment is safe and that you have power. A garden where you decide what grows, where you hide when you need to, where you make all the choices — that's PTSD medicine.
