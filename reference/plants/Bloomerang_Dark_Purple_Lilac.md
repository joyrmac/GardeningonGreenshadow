## Zone 7b Note (Important)

Proven Winners rates this for **Zones 3–7**. Greenshadow is **Zone 7b borderline 8a**, so we are right at the **warm edge** of its tolerance. Implications:

- Plant where it gets **morning sun and a little afternoon shelter** if possible — full afternoon sun in our July/August can stress the plant in its first 1–2 years.
- **Mulch heavily** in summer to keep roots cool.
- Lilacs need a **winter chill period** to bloom well. We get enough chill hours in 7b, but in mild winters bloom may be lighter.
- Don't expect peak performance compared to a Zone 5–6 garden — but the Bloomerang line is bred to rebloom, which gives us a second chance each year if spring bloom is light.

## Why Bloomerang?

Unlike traditional lilacs, which bloom once in spring and then sit there for 11 months, Bloomerang:

1. Blooms heavily in **mid–late spring**
2. Takes a brief rest to grow new wood
3. **Reblooms mid-summer through frost**

That's the same plant flowering twice (or continuously, in good years) instead of once — which makes it earn its space in a small garden far better than a once-a-year shrub.

It also blooms on **both new and old wood**, so a hard prune doesn't wipe out next year's flowers the way it does on traditional lilacs.

## Care

**Fertilizing:**
- Proven Winners Continuous Release Plant Food in **spring**
- Again in **mid-summer** (to fuel the rebloom)

**Pruning:**
- A **light trim** just after spring bloom keeps the plant tidy and encourages a stronger rebloom.
- Trimming will *delay* the rebloom slightly but boost it.
- Trimming is **not required** to get a rebloom — it will rebloom either way.
- Do NOT do hard pruning in late summer/fall — even though it blooms on new wood, you'll cut off old-wood spring buds.

**Watering:**
- Water deeply the first 1–2 years to establish.
- Once established, drought-tolerant.
- Clay-tolerant (good for our NC soil).
