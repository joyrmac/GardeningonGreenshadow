# Catnip Crafts Guide

A practical guide for putting your catnip harvest to good use — for the cats and for you.

---

## Harvesting & Drying

Before crafting, you'll want properly dried catnip for most projects.

- **When to harvest:** Cut stems when the plant is flowering for peak potency (the essential oil nepetalactone is strongest then).
- **How to dry:** Bundle 4–6 stems together, hang upside down in a warm, dry spot out of direct sunlight for 1–2 weeks. Alternatively, use a dehydrator on the herb/low setting.
- **Storage:** Strip dried leaves and flowers from stems. Store in airtight jars or ziplock bags in a cool, dark place. Stays potent for about 6 months to a year.

## Cat Toy Projects

### 1. Classic Catnip Sock Toy
The simplest craft. Great starter project.

- Take a clean sock (mismatched ones work perfectly)
- Fill the toe with a generous handful of dried catnip
- Tie a tight knot to close it off
- Optional: double up with a second sock over the first for durability

### 2. Catnip Kicker Toy
Cats love to grab these with their front paws and bunny-kick.

- Cut two rectangles of fabric (about 4" x 10" — sturdy cotton or fleece works well)
- Sew three sides together (inside out), then flip right-side out
- Stuff with a mix of polyester fiberfill and dried catnip
- Hand-stitch the opening closed
- Optional: add a crinkly layer (a piece of cellophane or parchment paper inside) for extra stimulation

### 3. No-Sew Catnip Pouch
Quick and easy — no sewing skills needed.

- Cut a square of felt or fleece (about 6" x 6")
- Place a tablespoon of dried catnip in the center
- Gather the edges together and tie tightly with string, ribbon, or a rubber band
- Trim excess fabric if desired

### 4. Catnip Mouse
A classic shape cats go wild for.

- Cut two mouse-shaped pieces of fabric (teardrop/oval shape, about 3–4" long)
- Sew together leaving a small opening
- Stuff with fiberfill and catnip
- Before closing, tuck in a piece of string or leather cord for a tail
- Stitch closed

### 5. Catnip Tea Bag Toys
Tiny, replaceable, and easy to refresh.

- Cut small squares of muslin or cheesecloth (3" x 3")
- Place a teaspoon of catnip in the center
- Tie shut with kitchen twine
- Toss directly to the cats, or stuff inside a larger toy to refresh it

## Around the House

### 6. Catnip Scratching Post Refresh
Revive an old scratching post your cats have lost interest in.

- Rub dried catnip directly into the sisal or carpet surface
- Or make a catnip paste: mix crushed dried catnip with a tiny bit of water, rub it in, let it dry
- Sprinkle loose catnip around the base of the post

### 7. Catnip Spray
Useful for redirecting scratching, encouraging cats to use new beds, or refreshing old toys.

- Pack a jar with dried catnip (about 1 cup)
- Pour 2 cups of boiling water over it
- Let steep until fully cooled (or overnight for extra strength)
- Strain into a spray bottle
- Store in the fridge — lasts about a week, or freeze in ice cube trays to thaw as needed

### 8. Catnip Garden Border
If you've got more catnip than you know what to do with, let it keep growing strategically.

- Catnip is a natural mosquito and pest deterrent — plant it around garden borders or seating areas
- It attracts pollinators (bees and butterflies love it)
- Let some go to seed to keep the supply going

## For You (Yes, Humans Can Use It Too)

### 9. Catnip Tea
Catnip has a long history as an herbal tea for people. It's in the mint family and has a mild, slightly minty flavor.

- Steep 1–2 teaspoons of dried catnip in a cup of boiling water for 5–10 minutes
- Strain and enjoy — add honey or lemon to taste
- Traditionally used for relaxation and settling an upset stomach
- *Note: Avoid during pregnancy. Check with your doctor if you take medications.*

### 10. Catnip Sachets (Drawer Freshener & Moth Deterrent)
- Fill small muslin bags with dried catnip and lavender
- Place in drawers, closets, or linen storage
- The scent deters moths and other insects while keeping things fresh

## Tips for Maximum Cat Enjoyment

- **Rotate toys.** Put catnip toys away after 15–20 minutes and bring them out again in a few days. Cats habituate to catnip quickly but the sensitivity resets after a break.
- **Not all cats respond.** About 30% of cats don't react to catnip at all — it's genetic. Kittens under 6 months usually don't respond either.
- **Silver vine as a backup.** If some of your cats are catnip-indifferent, silver vine (Actinidia polygama) triggers a similar response in many cats that ignore catnip.
- **Fresh vs. dried.** Fresh catnip is milder. For toys and crafts, dried and crumbled is much more potent.
- **Refresh old toys.** Open a seam, add fresh catnip, and re-stitch — or just rub crushed catnip on the outside.
