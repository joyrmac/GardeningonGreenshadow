## Shade Plant Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Fern fronds brown at tips | Low humidity | Mist in morning, add mulch for moisture |
| Hosta leaves with holes/slugs | Slug damage | Beer traps (dusk), hand-pick at night with flashlight |
| Astilbe flowers drooping | Too dry or too much sun creep | Add shade cloth or increase watering (don't overwater though) |
| Irish Moss turning brown | Drying out | Water lightly but frequently; mist if very hot |
| Bleeding Heart yellowing early | Normal dormancy | Don't dig it up—it's just going to sleep. Ferns will fill the gap |
| Foxglove not blooming | Year 1 growth (biennial) | Be patient—flowers come in year 2 |
| Hostas getting sparse/thin | Slugs or overcrowding | Beer traps, remove dead leaves, divide if 5+ years old |
| Hellebore drooping | Waterlogged soil | Check drainage—add grit/compost if too wet |

## Critical Cautions

⚠️ **Lily-of-the-Valley is TOXIC** to children and pets (all parts: leaves, flowers, berries, roots). If young kids or cats frequent the garden, consider removing it or fencing it off. Not negotiable.

⚠️ **Bleeding Heart goes dormant mid-summer.** Foliage yellows and disappears by July — this is NORMAL. Plant dense ferns around it to fill the summer gap. Don't dig it up thinking it died.

⚠️ **Hostas are slug magnets.** Check under leaves regularly. Beer traps work well (shallow dish sunk into soil). Copper tape also deters them. Check after rain.

⚠️ **Don't over-water shade gardens.** Shade soil stays moist longer. Stick your finger 1" into soil—if moist, skip watering. Soggy roots = root rot (especially ferns).

⚠️ **Foxglove is biennial.** Year 1 = leaves only. Year 2 = flowers. Then it dies. Let it self-seed or start new seeds each spring for continuous blooms.

⚠️ **Joe Pye Weed gets ENORMOUS** (5-7 ft tall, 3-4 ft wide). Place it at the very back or it will shade everything else. Don't put it in the middle.

## Design Philosophy: Why Fernwood Works

Fernwood is your **shade recycler** — the place where light filters through, where fallen leaves become soil, where moisture lingers. It's not trying to be sunny. It celebrates the shade:

- **Ferns** bring texture (feathery, fine, bold)
- **Hostas** add structure and color contrast
- **Hellebores** bloom when nothing else dares (winter/early spring)
- **Astilbe** softens with plumes and fern-like foliage
- **Bleeding Heart** brings delicate spring charm
- **Foxglove** adds height and drama (year 2)
- **Groundcovers** tie it all together in a cool, mossy feel

Seating in the center lets you experience the quiet, cool, textured space. This is design for rest.
