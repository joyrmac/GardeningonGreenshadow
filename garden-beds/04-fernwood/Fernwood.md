# Fernwood (Shade Garden)

> Shaded north side -- woodland retreat with ferns, hostas, and shade-loving perennials.

---

## Dimensions & Conditions

- **Size:** 6 ft x 12 ft (72 sq ft)
- **Light:** Mostly shade -- north-facing, under tree canopy
- **Soil:** Rich, moisture-retaining, amend with leaf mold and compost
- **Mood:** Quiet, cool, textured -- a place to sit and breathe

## Cautions

- **Lily-of-the-Valley is toxic** to children and pets. All parts -- leaves, flowers, berries, roots. If young kids or cats will be in this garden, skip it or fence it. Not negotiable.
- **Bleeding Heart goes dormant in summer.** The foliage will yellow and disappear by July. This is normal -- don't dig it up thinking it died. Plant ferns around it to fill the gap.
- **Hostas are slug magnets.** Check under leaves regularly. Beer traps (shallow dish of beer sunk into the soil) work well. Copper tape around containers also deters them.
- **Don't over-water shade gardens.** Shade soil stays moist longer. Check before watering -- stick your finger 1" into the soil. If it's moist, skip it. Soggy roots = root rot, especially for ferns.
- **Foxglove is biennial.** It grows leaves the first year and flowers the second year, then dies. Let it self-seed for continuous plants, or start new seeds each spring.
- **Joe Pye Weed gets enormous** (5-7 ft). Place it at the very back or it will shade everything else.

---

## Quick Fixes

- **Fern fronds turning brown at tips?** Low humidity. Mist in the morning or add mulch to retain moisture
- **Hosta leaves with holes?** Slugs. Beer traps at dusk. Or hand-pick slugs at night with a flashlight (gross but effective)
- **Foxglove not blooming?** It's probably first-year growth. Be patient -- flowers come year two
- **Irish Moss turning brown?** Needs more water. It dries out faster than it looks. Water lightly but frequently
- **Astilbe flowers drooping?** Needs more shade or water. It's telling you it's stressed
