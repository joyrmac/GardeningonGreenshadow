## ITEM-BY-ITEM GUIDE

### 1. PLANT LABELS/MARKERS

**What to get:**
- Rustic wooden stakes (6-12" tall) or slate markers
- Weatherproof labels (engraved or hand-painted)
- Witchy aesthetic: copper, dark wood, or slate

**Where to place:**
- **One per plant type** (not every plant, just the species)
- **Front of each ring** = visible as you enter
- **Example positions:**
  - Valerian label (north center)
  - Echinacea label (front of Ring 1)
  - Yarrow label (front of Ring 2)
  - Calendula label (front of Ring 3)
  - Fenugreek label (outer east)
  - Chamomile label (outer west)

**Budget:** $20-40 for 8-10 nice markers

**Witchy touch:**
- Engrave with plant name + astrological symbol or moon phase
- Use dark stain or copper finish
- Handwritten labels look more intentional

---

### 2. MIRROR (Hidden, Low)

**What to get:**
- Small round or oval mirror (8-12" diameter)
- Weather-resistant backing (not cheap plastic)
- Dark frame or frameless

**Where to place:**
- **Low in center area**, partially hidden by mulch
- **Tilted to catch light** from south opening
- **Between plants** (not obviously visible at first glance)
- **Purpose:** Bounce southern light deeper into center + witchy "reflection" magic

**Budget:** $15-30

**Installation:**
- Place on mulch bed
- Angle toward south (test to see light bounce)
- Surround with small stones to anchor + look intentional

**Witchy reasoning:**
- Reflects light = reflects intention
- Hidden mirrors = secret power
- Catches sun = captures solar energy

---

### 3. BEE CUPS (Watering stations)

**What to get:**
- 3-4 shallow ceramic or terracotta dishes (4-6" diameter)
- Flat pebbles or marbles
- Water source nearby (hose)

**Where to place:**
- **Scattered in outer rings** (Ring 3 mainly)
- **One on east side** (sun exposure)
- **One on west side** (shade in afternoon)
- **One or two in Ring 2** (for medium-height pollinators)

**Setup:**
1. Place dish on ground level (not elevated)
2. Fill with water
3. Add pebbles so bees can land without drowning
4. Refill daily (especially hot days)

**Budget:** $10-20

**Why this works:**
- Pollinators need to drink while working flowers
- Pebbles prevent drowning (landing pad)
- Keep near high-pollinator plants (Borage, Calendula, Echinacea)

---

### 4. BUTTERFLY PUDDLING STATION

**What to get:**
- One larger shallow dish (8-10" diameter, ceramic or terracotta)
- Pebbles/sand/small rocks
- Water source

**Where to place:**
- **Southeast corner** of outer ring (Ring 3)
- **Sunny spot** (butterflies like warmth)
- **Visible** but not in your walking path

**Setup:**
1. Fill dish with sand/pebbles
2. Add water so it's damp (like mud)
3. Butterflies land to drink minerals + moisture
4. Refresh water every 2-3 days

**Budget:** $8-15

**Why SE corner:**
- Gets afternoon sun (warmth)
- Away from stepping stone path (less foot traffic)
- Close to nectar plants (Calendula, Borage, Yarrow)

---

### 5. BEE HOUSE (Native bee hotel)

**What to get:**
- Pre-made bee house (mason bee + leafcutter bee types)
- OR DIY: wooden box with reed/straw tubes
- Should have roof overhang + drainage

**Where to place:**
- **Back north side**, tucked in tall plants
- **Shaded in afternoon** (bees don't like extreme heat)
- **6-8 ft high** ideally (nailed to stake or hung from tomato structure)
- **Facing east** (morning sun, no harsh western heat)

**Budget:** $25-60 (pre-made) or $15-25 (DIY materials)

**Why back north:**
- Protected from wind
- Afternoon shade
- Close to pollen sources (tall Ring 1 plants)
- Out of your way for harvesting

**Maintenance:**
- Clean/refresh tubes yearly (fall)
- Protect from predators
- Keep dry

### 6. STEPPING STONES

**What to get:**
- 3-5 flat stepping stones (12-18" square, natural stone preferred)
- OR round pavers (looks more intentional)

**Where to place:**
- **Path from south opening to center**
- **Spaced about 2 ft apart** (normal stride)
- **Through shortest outer ring plants** (not through tall ones)

**Layout:**

### 7. HARVEST BASKET HOOK

**What to get:**
- One sturdy hook (wrought iron or brass, 4-6" long)
- Mounted on stake, post, or tomato obelisk edge
- Should hold weight of basket full of herbs

**Where to place:**
- **Southwest side** of outer ring (Ring 3)
- **At arm height** (about 5-6 ft high on stake)
- **Near Calendula + Chamomile + easy-harvest plants**
- **Easy reach when harvesting**

**Setup:**
1. Drive stake 2-3 ft into ground on Ring 3 edge
2. Mount hook at arm height
3. Hang basket there while you pick

**Budget:** $15-30

**Why SW side:**
- West sun warms basket (keeps herbs fresh)
- Away from tall plants (easy visibility)
- Natural gathering point as you harvest east → west

---

### 8. DRYING RACK

**What to get:**
- Folding A-frame drying rack (wood or metal frame, fabric/mesh shelves)
- OR hanging drying rack (bundles hanging from frame)
- Weather-resistant but not fully weatherproof (keep under roof/covered)
- Size: 2-4 ft tall, 2-3 ft wide

**Where to place:**
- **East side** of outer ring (Ring 3), near south opening
- **Dappled shade** (afternoon protection from intense sun)
- **Protected from rain** (ideally under eave or tree canopy, or covered)
- **Close to harvest basket hook** (harvest → immediately dry)

**Why this works:**
- Dry flowers + leaves RIGHT THERE
- Turns garden into working apothecary
- Calendula, Chamomile, Fenugreek dry quickly in warm weather
- Makes medicine-making seamless (harvest → dry → store)

**Budget:** $30-80 (collapsible rack) or $50-150 (sturdy wooden frame)

**Setup:**
1. Place on level ground, secure against wind
2. Position for morning sun + afternoon shade
3. Cover with shade cloth (30-50%) if full sun exposure
4. Rotate/stir herbs daily during drying (3-7 days typical)

**Witchy touch:**
- Drying herbs in moonlight = more potent
- Work with harvest timing (new moon = inward, full moon = outward)
