## PLANTING ORDER (Easiest to manage)

### WEEK 1 (Late March)
1. Mark 10 ft circle with string
2. Mark center standing point
3. Divide into 3 rings with spray paint
4. Mark 2 ft south opening (C-shape)

### WEEK 2 (Early April)
5. **Plant Ring 1** - All perennial starters (Valerian, Echinacea, Angelica, Mullein if seedling)
6. Direct sow Mullein seeds if not using starters

### WEEK 3 (Mid-April)
7. **Plant Ring 2** - All perennial/biennial starters (Astragalus, St. John's Wort, Yarrow, Hyssop, Horehound)

### WEEK 4 (Late May / Early June - AFTER FROST DATE)
8. **Direct sow Ring 3** - All annual seeds (Calendula, Chamomile, Borage, Fenugreek, Marigold, Anise, Hollyhock)
9. Water daily for 2 weeks for germination
10. Thin seedlings at 2-4 weeks to target spacing

## SPACING TIPS BY PLANT TYPE

**Tight spacing (6-10"):** Calendula, Chamomile, Fenugreek
- Creates dense, full look
- More flowers/leaves per area
- Need regular thinning

**Medium spacing (12-18"):** Yarrow, Borage, Anise, Hyssop, Marigold, Echinacea
- Standard herb spacing
- Room to grow without crowding
- Easy to manage

**Wide spacing (18-24"):** Valerian, Astragalus, St. John's Wort, Angelica
- Larger mature plants
- Deep root systems
- Need room to develop fully

**Scatter/Self-seed:** Mullein, Chamomile
- No strict spacing
- Expect them to pop up randomly
- Thin heavily to prevent overcrowding
