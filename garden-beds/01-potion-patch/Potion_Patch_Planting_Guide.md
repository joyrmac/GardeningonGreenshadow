# Potion Patch — Concentric Garden Planting Guide

**Garden Size:** 10 ft diameter C-shaped bed (opening south)
**Zones:** 3 concentric rings + mulched center
**Timeline:** 3-year establishment (Year 1 planting → Year 3 full production)

---

## QUICK REFERENCE: PLANT ZONES & SPACING

### CENTER RING (18-24" inside diameter) — TALL FOCAL PLANTS

| Plant | Qty | Spacing | Height | Placement | Notes |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Valerian** | 2 | 24" apart | 4-5 ft | Dead center (north side) | Perennial, deep roots |
| **Mullein** | 3-5 | Self-seeding | 3-5 ft | Scattered center | Biennial/short-lived, thin to prevent crowding |
| **Echinacea** | 2-3 | 12-18" apart | 24-36" | Center-to-middle | Perennial, iconic pollinator |
| **Angelica** | 2-3 | 18" apart | 20-36" | Center-middle zone | Biennial, architectural |
| **Yarrow** | 4-6 | 12" apart | 18-24" | Middle ring | Perennial, spreader (thin) |

**Total center zone diameter:** ~5 ft (3-4 ft planted + 1-2 ft outer edge)

---

### MIDDLE RING (5-8" width, 18-36" height)

| Plant | Qty | Spacing | Height | Placement | Notes |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Astragalus** | 1-2 | 18" apart | 24-30" | Middle ring scattered | Legume, nitrogen-fixer, slow-build |
| **St. John's Wort** | 1-2 | 18" apart | 18-24" | Middle ring (away from Echinacea) | Perennial, yellow flowers |
| **Hyssop** | 6-8 | 12" apart | 18-24" | Middle-to-outer transition | Spike flowers, moderate spreader |
| **Horehound** | 1 | Single | 18-24" | Middle ring (spacious) | Hairy texture, unique |

**Ring width:** ~18-24" (varies by plant)

---

### OUTER RING (18" width, 6-18" height) — ABUNDANT ANNUALS & SPREADERS

| Plant | Qty | Spacing | Height | Placement | Notes |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Calendula** | 8-10 | 10" apart | 12-18" | Perimeter scattered | Annual, prolific bloomer |
| **Chamomile** | 10-15 patches | 10-12" apart | 8-12" | Scattered throughout | Heavy self-seeder (manage by thinning) |
| **Borage** | 4-6 | 12" apart | 12-18" | Scattered throughout | Blue star flowers, moderate spreader |
| **Fenugreek** | 10-12 | 6-8" apart | 12-18" | Outer ring perimeter | Annual, your tea plant |
| **French Marigold** | 8-10 | 10" apart | 12-15" | Scattered perimeter | Annual, compact |
| **Hollyhock (French Dwarf)** | 5-8 | 12" apart | 12-18" | Middle-outer edge | Biennial, vertical accent |
| **Anise** | 8-10 | 12" apart | 18-24" | Middle-outer zone | Annual, feathery foliage |

**Ring width:** ~18" outer + 8-12" gap to tomato circle

## STEP-BY-STEP PLANTING TIMELINE

### BEFORE YOU START: Bed Preparation
1. **Measure & mark:** 10 ft diameter circle, mark C-shape opening (south)
2. **Soil:** Fill with 10-12" good garden soil + compost mix
3. **Center mulched area:** 18-24" diameter, 2-3" wood chips
4. **Outer mulch buffer:** 2-3" between herb ring + tomato circle

---

### PHASE 1: EARLY SPRING (Late March - Early April)

**Focus: Establish keystone perennials for structure**

**Plant these first (they take longest to establish):**

1. **Center Ring Perennials** (first):
   - Valerian (2 plants, center, 24" apart)
   - Echinacea (2-3 plants, 12-18" apart, center-middle)

2. **Middle Ring Perennials**:
   - Astragalus (1-2 plants, 18" apart)
   - St. John's Wort (1-2 plants, 18" apart, north side)
   - Horehound (1 plant, spacious position)

**Tools needed:** Spade, hand trowel, soil, compost, watering can

**Instructions:**
- Dig holes slightly wider + same depth as root ball
- Backfill with soil + compost mix
- Water thoroughly
- Mulch around (not touching stem)

---

### PHASE 2: MID-SPRING (April)

**Focus: Add remaining perennials + prepare for annuals**

1. **Center Ring** (biennial starters):
   - Mullein (thin to 3-5 seedlings, center zone)
   - Angelica (2-3 plants, scattered center-middle)
   - Yarrow (4-6 plants, 12" apart, middle ring)

2. **Middle Ring**:
   - Hyssop (6-8 plants, 12" apart, middle-outer transition)

3. **Prepare outer ring:** Remove weeds, loosen soil, add 1" compost

**Instructions:**
- Same planting as Phase 1
- Space carefully (measure before digging)
- Water well
- Mulch gaps

---

### PHASE 3: LATE SPRING (May-early June)

**Focus: Direct sow annuals in outer ring**

**Direct sow (let seeds germinate in place):**

1. **Outer ring perimeter:**
   - Calendula (direct sow, thin to 8-10 plants, 10" apart)
   - Chamomile (direct sow, thin RUTHLESSLY 2-4 weeks later to 10-15 patches)
   - Borage (direct sow, thin to 4-6 plants, 12" apart)
   - Fenugreek (direct sow, thin to 10-12 plants, 6-8" apart)
   - French Marigold (direct sow, thin to 8-10 plants, 10" apart)

2. **Middle-outer zone:**
   - Hollyhock (direct sow, thin to 5-8 plants, 12" apart)
   - Anise (direct sow, thin to 8-10 plants, 12" apart)

**Instructions:**
- Scatter seeds where you want plants
- Press into soil (light cover for small seeds)
- Water gently daily for 2-3 weeks until sprouted
- Thin seedlings at 2-4 weeks (keep strongest, remove others)
- Space to match chart above

**CRITICAL:** Don't try to plant all at once. Plant clusters of same species together for easier thinning.

---

### PHASE 4: SUMMER MAINTENANCE (June onwards)

**Weekly tasks:**

1. **Deadheading:**
   - Calendula: Pinch spent flowers (continuous harvest)
   - Chamomile: Deadhead 50% of flowers (prevent self-seeding chaos)
   - Borage: Let some flowers mature for seed, deadhead others
   - Fenugreek: Pinch leaves fresh, let some flowers mature for seed
   - Yarrow: Deadhead 50% (prevent aggressive self-seeding)
   - Hyssop: Mix (deadhead + let seed)

2. **Watering:** Deep weekly (1-2"), adjust for rain

3. **Observation:** Watch for volunteers, pest pressure, pollinator activity

4. **Thinning seedlings:** Chamomile especially—thin continuously as it self-seeds

## PLANTING QUANTITIES AT A GLANCE

**Total plants needed (~60-100 plants/seedlings):**

- **Perennials:** ~12-15 plants (Valerian, Echinacea, Angelica, Astragalus, St. John's Wort, Horehound, Yarrow, Hyssop)
- **Biennials:** ~8-13 plants (Mullein, Hollyhock)
- **Annuals from seed:** ~50-65 seeds scattered (Calendula, Chamomile, Borage, Fenugreek, Marigold, Anise)

**Budget estimate:**
- Perennial starter plants: $40-60 (if buying, or from other beds/friends)
- Seeds: $20-30 (if buying bulk packets)
- Soil/compost: Depends on depth (estimate 2-3 cubic yards)
- Mulch (center + buffer): ~1-2 cubic yards wood chips ($15-30)

---

## SUCCESS INDICATORS (Year 1)

**By end of summer:**
- ✓ Perennials established (not yet blooming full)
- ✓ Annuals flowering continuously (Calendula, Chamomile, Fenugreek)
- ✓ C-shaped opening visible (herbs radiating from center)
- ✓ Pollinators visiting daily
- ✓ You've harvested some Calendula flowers + Fenugreek leaves

**By fall:**
- ✓ Fenugreek seeds mature (first seed harvest)
- ✓ Chamomile volunteers scattered (self-seeding begins)
- ✓ Echinacea/Yarrow showing fall structure
- ✓ Perennials starting to establish deep roots

---

## YEAR 2 & 3 NOTES

**Year 2:**
- Perennials bloom fuller + bigger
- Mullein first-year rosettes → second-year flowers (watch for height!)
- Angelica rosettes → tall flower spikes
- Chamomile + Borage volunteers everywhere (thin ruthlessly)
- Start harvesting St. John's Wort flowers (June solstice)
- Fenugreek seeds mature again

**Year 3+:**
- Astragalus roots ready to harvest (year 3+)
- System self-sustaining with minimal replanting
- Perennials full-sized + productive
- Annual self-seeding handles much of replacement
- You're in full "tend + harvest" mode
