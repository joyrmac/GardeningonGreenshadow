# 🌴 The Jungle Pocket

> Tropical / exotic-foliage bed wrapping the brick wall around the back-yard bird feeder tree. Heat-reflecting microclimate against the brick, dappled shade from the canopy above. Built around three plants you already own (Basjoo Banana + 2 Bird of Paradise).

> **Status (May 2026):** Conceptual — bed design complete, plants pending. Existing 4 bushes to remove. Bloomerang Lilac currently planted here will relocate.

---

## Location

- **Where:** Back yard, wrapping around the outside of the brick raised bed that contains the bird feeder tree
- **Shape:** Curved bed (follows the brick wall arc)
- **Dimensions:** ~6–7 ft deep × ~12 ft long curve = roughly 60–75 sq ft of planting area
- **North edge:** Brick wall — heat-reflecting, wind-sheltering microclimate
- **Overhead:** Tree canopy (dappled shade in upper area, sun reaches the outer/front edge)
- **Adjacent:** Patio, screen room visible from inside the house

## Why This Spot Works for Tropicals

- **Brick wall = thermal mass.** Holds heat into the night, radiates warmth, protects from wind. Tropicals love this.
- **Sheltered from wind** — big-leaf plants (banana, elephant ears) shred in wind. This corner is protected.
- **Visible from screen room + patio** — you see it constantly from inside, which is the whole point of a dramatic display bed.
- **Existing brick architecture** anchors a "destination" feel — the bed becomes a focal point, not just a planting.

---

## The Concept

A small, dramatic tropical "pocket" you walk past to or sit beside. Big leaves, deep greens, jewel-tone flowers. Year-round structure (Basjoo banana base + perennial roots), explosive summer growth, lift-and-store annuals at the front. **Fun + kitschy + alive** — fits the garden philosophy.

Like a tropical greenhouse exploded onto a single bed.

## Demolition / Prep (Before Planting)

The 4 existing bushes need to come out first. **Don't just cut them off — get the rootballs out** so they don't resprout and compete.

1. **Cut each bush back to stumps** (8–12" stems)
2. **Dig around each stump** with a spade and digging bar — most decorative shrubs have a 12–18" rootball
3. **Pry/pull out** the rootball; root fragments left in soil are fine if small
4. **Rake out old mulch + spent soil** from the bed surface
5. **Amend the entire bed** with 3–4 bags of compost worked into the top 12" — these tropicals are heavy feeders
6. **Add worm castings** as a slow-release fertilizer (~5 lbs across the bed)
7. **Rake level**, prepare to plant

Estimated prep time: 1 full day (4–6 hours including hauling out the cut shrubs).

## Bird Feeder Seed Drop — A Real Issue

The feeders are directly above this bed. Implications:

1. **Spilled seed germinates** into grassy weeds that compete with tropicals
2. **Seed attracts voles** (confirmed on property) — **the Basjoo banana corm is vole-vulnerable** in its first year
3. **Ground-feeding birds** will forage in the bed (this is fine, but they'll scratch)

### Mitigations

- [ ] **Switch to hulled bird seed** (no shells = drastically less mess, fewer rodents)
- [ ] **Sweep the bed weekly** during establishment year
- [ ] **3" mulch layer (pine bark)** smothers germinating seeds
- [ ] **Sharp grit + ¼" hardware cloth basket around Basjoo corm at planting** (vole protection — required, not optional)
- [ ] **Consider moving feeders slightly** so seed-drop zone isn't directly on the banana

## Winter Care Plan

| Plant | What to do in fall |
|---|---|
| **Basjoo Banana** | Let frost kill foliage. Cut stalks to ~12" tall. Pile 12–18" of mulch (straw or shredded leaves) over the crown. Mulch comes off in early April when new growth starts. |
| **Elephant Ears 'Pink China'** | Cut foliage after frost. Heavy mulch (8–10") over the crown. Stays in ground in 7b. |
| **Other Elephant Ears** | Dig tubers after first frost. Clean off soil. Store in paper bag with peat moss in cool dry basement (50–60°F). Replant May. |
| **Hardy Hibiscus** | Cut to ground after frost. Mulch lightly. Comes back from roots — late to emerge in spring (often May), don't panic. |
| **Cannas** | Heavy mulch in 7b usually keeps them. If you have a particularly cold winter forecast, dig rhizomes and store like elephant ears. |
| **Bird of Paradise (Blue + Orange)** | **Bring containers inside before first frost** (typically mid-Nov NC). Sunny window or grow light, water sparingly through winter, back outside after last frost (mid-April). |
| **Caladium + Sweet Potato + Coleus** | Treat as annuals. Or lift Caladium bulbs and store like elephant ears. |

## Vole Protection (Confirmed Voles)

For all in-ground plantings:

- **Basjoo Banana corm:** ¼" hardware cloth basket around the corm at planting — **required** (banana corm is high-value vole food)
- **Hardy Hibiscus + Cannas + Elephant Ears:** Sharp grit ring at base
- **Mulch back 2–3" from all stems** — no mulch-tunnel cover for voles
