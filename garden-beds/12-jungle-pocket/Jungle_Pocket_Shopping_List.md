### What to Look For at the Nursery (Plant-Picking Tips)

- **Alocasia:** Look at the corm at the base. Firm = good. Mushy or moldy = pass. Should have at least 1 leaf already unfurled.
- **Hibiscus 'Kopper King':** Foliage should be burgundy/copper-tinted. Slow waker in spring — might still be small; that's fine.
- **Cannas:** Rhizomes (bagged) should be firm with visible "eyes" (growth points). Potted = should have green shoots emerging.
- **Caladium bulbs:** Firm, knobby. Bigger bulbs = bigger plants. Plant knobby side UP.

## Substitution Notes

If a nursery doesn't have something:
- **No Alocasia macrorrhiza?** 'Calidora' or 'Portora' substitute (both upright big-leaf)
- **No 'Mojito'?** Any large Colocasia works — 'Black Magic' (darker), 'Pink China' (hardiest)
- **No 'Phasion'?** Look for 'Tropicanna' — exact same plant
- **No 'Kopper King'?** 'Lord Baltimore' (red), 'Cherry Cheesecake' (pink/red bicolor), 'Blue River II' (white)
- **No 'Florida Moonlight'?** Any white-leaf caladium works
- **No 'Florida Sweetheart'?** Any pink strap-leaf caladium works (or just any pink caladium)
