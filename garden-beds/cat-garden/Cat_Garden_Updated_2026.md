## Perimeter Design: NC Native Shade-Tolerant Plants

**Philosophy:** Contained, ecosystem-beneficial natives. No ornamentals. Shade-loving. Pollinator food. Won't escape like cat grass.

### Outer Perimeter (8x8' Square Edges)

**North Side (afternoon shade from magnolia):**
- **Aromatic Aster** (Symphyotrichum oblongifolium) — Fall bloomer, native, 2-3' tall
  - Purple-pink flowers, long bloom (Sep-Nov), essential for late-season pollinators
  - **Spacing:** 18-24" apart (3-4 plants)

**East Side (morning sun, afternoon shade):**
- **Virginia Mountain Mint** (Pycnanthemum virginianum) — Native, 12-18", aromatic
  - Narrow, contained growth (won't sprawl)
  - Pollinator magnet, medicinal
  - **Spacing:** 12-15" apart (3-4 plants)

**South Side (most sun exposure):**
- **Blue Mistflower** (Conoclinium coelestinum) — Soft blue flowers, 2-3' tall
  - Shade-tolerant, elegant, manageable if pinched back
  - Attracts butterflies, bees, fall bloomer
  - **Spacing:** 18" apart (3-4 plants)

**West Side (mixed sun/shade):**
- **Catmint varieties** (Nepeta × faassenii or Nepeta subsessilis) — Shade-tolerant, 12-24"
  - Already planned to have some here anyway
  - Purple-blue flowers, fragrant, bees love it
  - Very contained, won't spread wildly
  - **Spacing:** 12-18" apart (3-4 plants)

### Inner Perimeter (Around Magnolia/Sand Area)

**Low Ground Cover Between Outer Ring & Sand:**
- **Creeping Thyme** or **Lamb's Ear (Stachys byzantina)** — Soft, shade-tolerant
  - Creates soft edges for cats to walk on
  - Stachys especially: fuzzy leaves cats might enjoy
  - **Spacing:** 12" apart as transition layer

## Magnolia Shoots Decision Tree

### Option A: Single-Stem Tree (Tidy, Classic Look)
- Pick the **strongest, straightest shoot** coming from the base
- Remove the other 29 shoots
- Train it upward as main trunk
- **Pros:** Traditional tree shape, easier to manage, better for sunbathing under
- **Cons:** Requires intervention now

### Option B: Multi-Stem Tree (Natural, Bushy Look)
- Keep **2-3 of the strongest shoots** (remove the weakest 27)
- Let them grow together as a multi-stem specimen
- Creates more open space underneath, more dramatic
- **Pros:** Natural look, less pruning work, interesting architecture
- **Cons:** More complex shape, less clear center lounging space

**Recommendation:** Single-stem if you want clean lines + clear center sunbathing spot. Multi-stem if you like the wild, natural look.
