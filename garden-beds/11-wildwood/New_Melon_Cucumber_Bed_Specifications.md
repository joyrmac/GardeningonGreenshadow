# New Melon & Cucumber Bed (10' x 15'): Complete Build Specifications

*The detailed how-to for building your creative vegetable garden*

## Quick Overview

- **Dimensions:** 10 ft (E-W) × 15 ft (N-S) = 150 sq ft
- **Location:** Where old Bed 6 was (south/east area of Wildwood)
- **Front 5 ft:** Cucumber trellis (Virgo structure)
- **Back 10 ft:** Melon sprawl (Leo abundance)
- **Build timeline:** March 2026 (prep) → May 2026 (plant)

## SITE SELECTION & PREP

### **Location Criteria**

✓ 6-8 hours direct sun (minimum 6, preferably more)
✓ Good drainage (not a low spot)
✓ Path access on at least 2 sides (for harvesting + picking)
✓ Near water source (for hand-watering in hot spells)
✓ Visible from sitting area (so you notice when ripe)

### **Soil Testing (Do This First)**

**Test for:**
- pH (should be 6.0–6.8)
- Fertility (N-P-K levels)
- Compaction/drainage

**Where:** Local Extension Office (free or cheap, use your soil sample)

**Timeline:** Do this in early March so you have time to amend

## STEP-BY-STEP BUILD INSTRUCTIONS

### **Phase 1: Prep the Ground (Week 1, March)**

1. **Clear the area**
   - Remove any existing plants/weeds
   - Rough out the 10 ft × 15 ft footprint with string + stakes

2. **Soil test**
   - Take soil sample to Extension Office (see above)
   - Amend based on results (usually add compost)

3. **Deep till** (if not raised bed)
   - Loosen soil 12–18" deep with a broadfork or rented tiller
   - Remove rocks, roots, debris
   - IMPORTANT: Don't work wet soil (compacts it)

4. **Add amendments**
   - Aged compost: 3–4" worked into top 6"
   - Aged manure if available (not fresh)
   - pH amendment if needed (lime to raise, sulfur to lower)

### **Phase 2: Build Trellis Frame (Week 2, March)**

1. **Mark trellis placement**
   - Front 5 ft of bed (the 10 ft side)
   - Use string + stakes to mark exact spots

2. **Prepare post holes (if using posts)**
   - Option A (permanent): Dig 2-3 ft holes, set T-posts in concrete
   - Option B (portable): Just drive posts 1-2 ft into ground, can remove later

3. **Install cattle panels**
   - Lean panels against posts (6 ft up minimum)
   - Secure with bolts or heavy-duty wire (not nails; they pop out)
   - Test stability (push hard, it should not wobble)

4. **Paint trellis (optional)**
   - Use outdoor-rated paint (deep blue, purple, or deep green)
   - Dry 24 hrs before planting
   - Your Leo will appreciate the drama

5. **Mark spacing on trellis**
   - With tape or paint, mark 10" intervals (vertical or horizontal)
   - This guides your planting

### **Phase 3: Final Soil Prep (Week 3–4, March)**

1. **Top-dress with compost**
   - Add another 2–3" of aged compost (total 5–6" top layer now)
   - Mix into top 2–3" with garden fork

2. **Add mulch**
   - Pine needles: 2–3" across entire bed
   - OR wood chips (softer, more aesthetic)
   - Avoid dyed bark (chemicals)

3. **Water deeply**
   - Settle the soil, start mycorrhizal activity
   - Water 2–3 days before planting if possible

4. **Path edging** (optional)
   - Mark paths around bed with mulch or wooden edge
   - Clear, defined pathways look intentional

## PLANTING CALENDAR

### **Timeline**

| Date | Task | Details |
|------|------|---------|
| **Late April** | Start seeds indoors OR buy starts | Cucumbers: direct seed or buy 6-packs |
| **May 1–10** | Final bed prep + trellis check | Water bed, let settle, check trellis stability |
| **May 10–15** | Plant cucumbers | Direct seed at trellis base (if temps ≥ 60°F nights) |
| **May 15–25** | Plant melons | Direct seed in mounds (if temps ≥ 70°F nights) |
| **May 20+** | Plant companions | Nasturtium, borage, calendula around melon mounds |
| **June onwards** | Maintenance | Water, train vines, monitor pests |
| **July–October** | Harvest | Cucumbers continuously, melons when ripe |

### **Frost Risk Check (NC Zone 7b)**

- Last frost: ~April 10 (but don't plant until soil warms)
- Cucumbers: Plant when soil is 60°F+ (late May is safe)
- Melons: Plant when soil is 70°F+ (mid–late May)
- First fall frost: ~November 1 (plenty of time)

**Total:** 6–8 mounds depending on exact spacing

**Seeding method:**

1. **Create mound** (per variety):
   - Gather soil/compost into dome shape
   - Pat down lightly (not hard)
   - Make 3–4" deep well at center

2. **Planting:**
   - Plant 3–4 seeds per mound, 1" deep
   - Space seeds in triangle (not all same spot)
   - Cover lightly with soil

3. **Watering:**
   - Water immediately after planting
   - Keep moist (not soggy) for germination

4. **Thinning:**
   - Once sprouted, thin to strongest 1–2 plants per mound
   - Pinch off weaker seedlings (don't pull, damages roots)

**Timing:** Plant mid–late May (soil 70°F+)

## MAINTENANCE THROUGH GROWING SEASON

### **Watering**

| Growth Stage | Frequency | Amount | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Germination** | Daily until sprouted | ½–1" | Keep consistently moist |
| **Early growth** | 2–3x per week | 1–1.5" | Establish deep roots |
| **Flowering** | 2x per week | 1.5–2" | Critical for fruit set |
| **Fruit ripening** | 1–2x per week | 1–2" | Reduce slightly to concentrate flavor |
| **1 week before harvest** | Reduce | Less water | Stops fruit splitting, concentrates sugars |

**Method:** Soaker hose or drip irrigation (not overhead—prevents mildew)

**Best time:** Early morning or evening

---

### **Fertilizer Schedule**

| Time | Fertilizer | Application | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **At planting** | 10-10-10 balanced | 1 tbsp per mound/trellis plant | Granular, worked into soil |
| **2 weeks** | Fish emulsion (liquid) | Spray foliage or drench roots | Boosts early growth |
| **Flowering** | 5-10-10 (high phosphorus) | Liquid or granular | Encourages blooms, not just leaves |
| **Fruit set** | 0-0-50 (potassium) | Liquid or granular | Strengthens fruits, sweetens melons |
| **Every 3 weeks** | Compost tea (optional) | Drench at root zone | Feeds soil microbes |

**Application rates:** Follow package directions; don't over-fertilize (causes excess vines, fewer fruits)

---

### **Pest & Disease Management**

| Problem | Signs | Solution | Prevention |
|---------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Powdery mildew** | White powder on leaves | Neem oil spray (early morning) | Don't overhead water; thin crowded vines |
| **Cucumber beetles** | Spotted beetles, damage | Hand-pick, yellow sticky traps | Nasturtiums as trap crop |
| **Squash bugs** | Brown bugs, wilting | Hand-pick eggs (underside of leaves) | Borage/calendula companions |
| **Aphids** | Tiny green/black bugs, curled leaves | Spray with insecticidal soap OR squirt with hose | Nasturtiums trap them |
| **Mildew (melons)** | White powder, brown spots | Ensure good air circulation, thin vines | Space plants adequately |

**Your philosophy:** Companion planting first, organic sprays second, chemical last (and probably never).

---

### **Trellis Management (Cucumbers)**

**Training:**
- Gently guide vine growth along trellis
- Weave tendrils through cattle panel as they grow
- Support heavy fruits with pantyhose slings if needed (rarely needed for cucumbers—they're light)

**Pruning:**
- Remove lower leaves once vine is 3–4 ft up (improves air flow)
- Thin crowded areas if mildew threatens
- Don't over-prune (leaves feed the plant)

---

### **Melon Vine Management**

**Training:**
- Let melons sprawl naturally, but steer vines AWAY from pathways
- Guide them toward the center of the bed
- Coil wandering vines back toward mounds

**Pruning:**
- Remove SECONDARY vines (after 2–3 fruits set per plant)
- Keep removing new runners
- This focuses energy on ripening existing fruits

**Fruit support:**
- Once melons are melon-sized, place under-sling support (pantyhose, old t-shirt strips)
- Especially for heavy watermelons on trellises (you're NOT trellising these, so no issue)

## HARVESTING

### **Cucumbers**

**When to pick:**
- 6–8" long (depending on variety)
- Firm, bright green
- Skin not soft or yellowish

**How to pick:**
- Cut with knife (don't pull, damages vine)
- Pick every 2–3 days (encourages more production)
- Leaving big cucumbers on vine = plant stops producing

**Yield:** ~1–2 lbs per plant over season (12–15 plants = lots of cucumbers)

**What to do:**
- Eat fresh (salads, snacks)
- Pickle (dill variety especially)
- Ferment (brined cuc

umbers)
- Gift (excess always happens)
- Preserve (dehydrate, ferment)

---

### **Watermelons**

**When ripe:**
- **Sound:** Hollow thump when thumped (finger-knock test)
- **Color:** Dark stripe fades, single yellow spot (ground spot) on bottom
- **Smell:** Sweet, fragrant
- **Size:** Full size for variety (8–15 lbs depending on variety)
- **Firmness:** Slight give at blossom end (not hard, not mushy)

**How to harvest:**
- Cut stem with knife (don't twist—bruises fruit)
- Taste test: Cut into one to check ripeness (you'll learn the pattern)

**Yield:** 5–10 watermelons per season per plant (3–6 plants = abundant supply)

---

### **Cantaloupes**

**When ripe:**
- **Smell:** Very fragrant, musky-sweet (the main indicator)
- **Color:** Netted exterior fully developed, golden between netting
- **Feel:** Slight give at blossom end
- **Separation:** Vine releases fruit with slight twist (not hanging on)
- **Size:** 4–6 lbs typically

**How to harvest:**
- Gentle twist; if it comes free, it's ready
- If vine holds tight, it's not ripe yet

**Yield:** 8–12 cantaloupes per plant per season (2–3 plants = many melons)

## CROP ROTATION (For Next Year)

**What's in this bed THIS year:**
- Cucurbits (melons, cucumbers)

**What goes in NEXT year:**
- NOT another cucurbit (rotate families)
- Good options: Solanaceae (tomatoes, peppers), Brassicas (cabbage, broccoli), Alliums (onions, garlic), Legumes (beans, peas)
- OR let it rest and plant cover crop (clover, hairy vetch)

**Why:** Prevents soil-borne disease buildup, balances soil nutrients

## TROUBLESHOOTING QUICK REFERENCE

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| No flowers | Too much nitrogen, immature plant | Wait, reduce fertilizer |
| Flowers drop | Heat stress, inconsistent water | Water consistently, mulch well |
| Fruit doesn't set | Poor pollination | Hand-pollinate (use paintbrush) |
| Bitter cucumbers | Stress (heat, water), overripe | Consistent care, pick younger |
| Small melons | Too many fruits, poor pollination | Thin fruits, hand-pollinate |
| Mushy bases on melons | Overwatering, poor drainage | Reduce water, check drainage |
| Cracked watermelons | Overwatering late season | Reduce water as ripening approaches |
| Powdery mildew | High humidity, poor air flow | Thin vines, spray neem oil |
