### **HIDDEN SITTING ZONES (Pisces Solitude)**

Add these throughout Wildwood to create retreat spaces:

#### **Nook 1: "The Thinking Chair"**
- Location: West side of Wildwood, under crepe myrtle shade (near Rhubarb bed)
- Setup: Wooden bench or adirondack chair, facing the Herb Spiral Pond
- Screening: Tall shrubs (butterfly bush, spirea) behind to create privacy
- Why: You can see the pond/water, hear the fountain, be hidden from the main garden
- Vibe: Peaceful, reflective, contemplative

#### **Nook 2: "The Maker's Corner"**
- Location: East side of Wildwood, quiet corner
- Setup: Small table (2x2 ft), stool, weather-proof paper/sketching supplies
- Screening: Tall perennials (coneflowers, black-eyed susans, goldenrod) around edges
- Why: Close to melon/cucumber bed so you can sketch plants while sitting with them
- Vibe: Creative, focused, hands-nearby gardening

#### **Nook 3: "The Story Spot"**
- Location: Anywhere quiet along a path (consider between veggie beds and cut flowers)
- Setup: Bench with back support
- Screening: Planted screening (bamboo, tall ornamental grasses)
- Why: A pause point in the garden journey where you can sit and notice
- Vibe: Storytelling space (writing, journaling, sharing)

**Design principle:** Each nook should:
- Have a view of water (fountain, pond, or bird bath sound/sight)
- Be screened from main paths (not on display)
- Have shade overhead
- Invite sitting for 15-30 minutes
- NOT be decorated or precious — just quiet

## WILDWOOD ORGANIZATION SYSTEM (Virgo Detail)

### **Master Plant Map**

Create a large laminated map showing:
- All bed locations (numbered 1-8, plus special beds)
- What's planted in each section
- Companion planting (marked with colors)
- Path directions

Hang this at Gourd Gate for reference while working.

### **Plant Labels**

- Metal tags with permanent marker at EVERY plant/seed row
- Format: `PLANT NAME | VARIETY | SPACING | SEED DATE`
- Example: `CARROT | Purple Dragon | 3" | Planted 4/15`

### **Maintenance Calendar**

Posted at Gourd Gate:
- **Weekly:** Pick beans/cucumbers (every 2-3 days), deadhead cut flowers
- **Bi-weekly:** Check water, labels, pest damage
- **Monthly:** Succession planting, fertilizer schedule, prune/train vines
- **Seasonal:** Harvest, soil amendment, compost turning

### **Garden Journal (Your Record-Keeping)**

Keep a notebook with:
- What was planted when
- What succeeded/failed
- Pollinator notes (when did bees arrive?)
- Flavor/yield notes (which variety did you love?)
- Next year's ideas

## THE NEW MELON/CUCUMBER BED IN DETAIL

### **Site Prep (Virgo Phase)**

**Measurements:**
- 10 ft (E-W) x 15 ft (N-S)
- Locate in zone receiving 6-8 hrs direct sun
- Path access on at least two sides

**Soil prep:**
- Deep till 12-18"
- Amend with aged compost (3-4")
- pH test (should be 6.0-6.8)
- Mulch with pine needles (2-3")

**Trellis setup (Cucumber side, front 5 ft):**
- Cattle panel, 6 ft tall, set in concrete footings
- Paint it purple or deep blue (Leo statement)
- Mark 10" intervals with paint or tape (for plant spacing)

### **Planting (Leo + Virgo Balance)**

**FRONT 5 FT: CUCUMBERS (Virgo structure)**

| Item | Detail | Quantity |

| **Variety C** | Lemon (yellow, round) | 3-4 plants |
| **Total** | | 12-15 plants |
| **Spacing** | 10" apart, direct to trellis base | — |
| **Labeling** | Metal tag at EACH plant | — |

**BACK 10 FT: MELONS (Leo abundance)**

| Item | Detail | Quantity |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Watermelon A** | Garden Leader Monster | 1-2 mounds (7 ft) |
| **Watermelon B** | Bush Sugar Baby | 3 mounds (4 ft apart) |
| **Cantaloupe** | Sierra Gold | 2-3 mounds (5 ft) |
| **TOTAL MOUNDS** | | 6-8 mounds |

**COMPANIONS (Bold color):**

| Plant | Color | Function | Count |
|-------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Nasturtium** | Orange/red flowers | Trap crop + edible + spills over edges | 8-12 plants |
| **Borage** | Blue flowers | Pollinator magnet + edible flowers (cucumber taste) | 6-8 plants |
| **Calendula** | Golden yellow | Edible petals + medicinal + cheerful | 4-6 plants |

**Mulch:** Pine needles (soft, aesthetic, water-retaining)

**Spacing:** Let melons sprawl 4-7 ft. Don't constrain them.

### **Watering & Fertilizer Schedule**

| Time | Task | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Planting** | 10-10-10 fertilizer | One handful per plant |
| **2 weeks** | Fish emulsion | Liquid feed, spray or soil drench |
| **Flowering** | 5-10-10 | Boost phosphorus for blooms |
| **Fruit set** | 0-0-50 (potassium) | Once melons/cucumbers visible |
| **Ongoing** | Deep water 2x/week | Cucumber: consistent moisture. Melon: reduce water 1 week before harvest |

### **Harvesting & Celebration**

**Cucumbers:**
- Pick every 2-3 days (encourages more production)
- Pick in early morning when firm
- Bring inside, eat fresh, pickle, gift

**Melons:**
- When ripe, vine should release fruit with slight twist
- Watermelon: hollow sound when thumped = ripe
- Cantaloupe: fragrant, slight give at base
- **Harvest as celebration, not obligation.** Enjoy the process.
