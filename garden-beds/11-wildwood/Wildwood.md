# Wildwood Garden

> The main enclosed garden. Fenced area containing vegetable beds, cut flower garden, pumpkin patch, watermelon patch, spiral herb garden, corn circle, and the Gourd Gate entrance.

---

## Overview

Wildwood is the working garden -- food, cut flowers, and herbs. Enclosed by fencing (to keep deer and kids out). The **Gourd Gate** trellis is the entrance. Inside, sub-areas are organized by purpose.

## 3. Corn Circle (Three Sisters Design)

**Layout:** Sunburst/radial -- central point with 7 radiating spokes.
**Diameter:** 5-7 ft (mark center, use stick and string as compass)

### The Three Sisters

| Crop | Varieties | Count | Spacing |
|------|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Corn** (7 rays, 5 per ray) | Glass Gem, Mini Blue Popcorn, Montana Sunburst, Damaun KS Super Sweet, Amish Butter, Kandy Korn, Strawberry Popcorn | 35 total | 1 ft within each ray |
| **Beans** (at base of each stalk) | Tender Pod Garden Bean (pole) | 35-70 total | 1-2 per corn stalk |
| **Squash** (7 mounds between spokes) | Summer Melody Blend, Winter Spaghetti | 14-21 total | 2-3 seeds per mound |

**Groundcover:** Buckwheat (10 lbs, American Meadows) -- **PLANTED 3/8/26** as living mulch/groundcover over the Three Sisters patch.

**Timing:** Corn first (mid-to-late April). Beans 2 weeks after corn emerges. Squash same time as beans.

**Caution:** Different corn varieties WILL cross-pollinate if they tassel at the same time. This matters for popcorn and ornamental corn -- the kernels won't come true. Stagger planting by 2 weeks OR plant only one variety intended for eating and use the others as ornamental.

## 4. Cut Flower Garden (L-Shaped, North Wall)

**Layout:** Tallest in back, mid-height middle, short in front.

| Row | Plants | Spacing |
|-----|--------|---------|
| **Back (5+ ft)** | Sunflowers (Autumn Beauty, Teddy Bear, Giant), Dahlias (Pacific Jewel, Ben Huston, Otto's Thrill, Fairway Pilot, Bantling, Rocco, Jowey Nicky), Amaranth (Trailing Red & Green) | 18-24" |
| **Middle (3-4 ft)** | Snapdragons (Appleblossom, Tall Deluxe, Orange Wonder), Cosmos (Sea Shells, Apricotta, Mandarin Orange, Sensation), Coneflowers (Parrot, Pink Double Delight, Marmalade, Blueberry Cheesecake, Green Wizard) | 12-18" |
| **Front (1-2 ft)** | Zinnias (California Giant, Cupcake Pink, Double Enchantress, Tequila Lime), Baby's Breath, Sweet Alyssum | 6-12" |

**Also scatter through:** Poppies (Frosted Salmon, Jelly Beans, Lilac PomPom, Purple Peony)

**How to cut flowers:** Cut in early morning when stems are full of water. Cut at a 45-degree angle. Strip leaves below the waterline. Change vase water every 2 days. Cutting flowers ENCOURAGES more blooms -- the more you cut, the more you get.

**Succession plant sunflowers** every 2-3 weeks from late April through June for continuous blooms all summer.

### Frog Pond ("The Alchemist's Pond")

**Size:** ~5-7 ft long, 2-3 ft wide, up to 2 ft deep
**Liner:** 15 x 12 ft flexible rubber pond liner

| Zone | Depth | Plants |
|------|-------|--------|
| Shallow shelf (3-6") | Edge | Pickerelweed, Arrow Arum, Golden Club, Creeping Jenny, Marsh Marigold, Soft Rush |
| Plant zone (6-12") | Mid | Hornwort, American Waterweed, Water Starwort, Duckweed (25% surface cover) |
| Deep zone (18-24") | Center | Additional oxygenators |
| Pond edge (soil) | Outside | Cardinal Flower, Sensitive Fern, Lavender, Bee Balm |

**Hardware needed:** Solar fountain pump (150-250 GPH), rocks/gravel (~1 cubic yard), rubber liner, rainwater barrel (50-100 gal)

**Caution:** A still-water pond WILL breed mosquitoes. The solar fountain pump is not decorative -- it's essential. Moving water prevents mosquito larvae from surviving. If the pump fails, add mosquito dunks (Bt-based, safe for frogs and fish) within 48 hours.

**Size:** 10 ft (E-W) x 15 ft (N-S). 3 rows, 5 ft apart.

| Row | Variety | Hills | Spacing | Companions |
|-----|---------|-------|---------|------------|
| 1 (west) | Garden Leader Monster Watermelon | 2 hills | 7 ft apart | Nasturtium, Basil |
| 2 (middle) | Bush Sugar Baby Watermelon | 3 hills | 4 ft apart | Marigold, Catnip |
| 3 (east) | Sierra Gold Cantaloupe | 3 hills | 5 ft apart | Nasturtium, Borage |

**Soil:** Deep tilled 12-18", amended with aged compost. pH 6.0-6.8. Mulch with pine needles.

**Fertilizer schedule:**
1. 10-10-10 at planting
2. Fish emulsion at 2 weeks
3. 5-10-10 at flowering
4. 0-0-50 (potassium) once fruit sets

**Caution:** Don't over-water once fruit is ripening -- excess water dilutes flavor and can split melons. Cut back watering 1 week before expected harvest. Ripe watermelon sounds hollow when thumped.

## 7. Pumpkin Patch

**Size:** 10 ft x 10 ft
**Varieties:** Jack O'Lantern, Small Sugar
**Spacing:** 4 ft apart (mounded beds)
**Companions:** Nasturtiums, Radishes

**Caution:** Pumpkin vines spread 15-20 ft. They WILL escape a 10x10 patch. Train vines back toward the center or into the Gourd Gate trellis area. Prune secondary vines after 2-3 fruits set per plant, or you'll get lots of tiny pumpkins instead of good-sized ones.

## 8. Rhubarb Bed (West Side, Under Crepe Myrtles)

**Size:** 4 ft x 8 ft raised bed
**Location:** West edge of Wildwood, under the crepe myrtle dripline. Morning sun from the east, dappled afternoon shade from the crepe myrtles.
**Soil:** Deep (12-18"), rich, well-drained. Heavy compost and aged manure. pH 6.0-6.8.

| Plant | Variety | Qty | Source | Spacing | Notes |
|-------|---------|-----|--------|---------|-------|
| Rhubarb | Crimson Red | 2 | Renee's Garden (shipped with 2 roots) | 3-4 ft | Red stalks, compact — BOTH PLANTED |
| Rhubarb | Victoria | 3 | Johnny's Selected Seeds (ships week of 3/16) | 3-4 ft | Classic green/pink, vigorous |

### Layout

```
        WEST (crepe myrtles above)
  +----+----+----+----+----+
  | V1 | V2 | V3 | CR | CR |   4 ft deep
  +----+----+----+----+----+
              8 ft wide
```
- V1, V2, V3 = Victoria crowns (2 ft from edges, 3-4 ft apart)
- CR, CR = Crimson Red (2 roots, planted 3-4 ft apart)

### Care

- **Mulch heavily** (3-4") with straw or shredded leaves to retain moisture and cool roots in summer
- **Water deeply** once a week; more in extreme heat
- **Do NOT harvest year 1** -- let crowns establish strong roots
- **Year 2:** Light harvest only (a few stalks per plant)
- **Year 3+:** Full harvest -- pull stalks when 12-18" tall by twisting and pulling at the base (don't cut)
- **Fertilize** in early spring with composted manure or 10-10-10
- **Remove flower stalks** immediately if they bolt -- flowering weakens the crown
- **Never eat the leaves** -- rhubarb leaves contain oxalic acid and are toxic

### NC Heat Notes

- Rhubarb may go semi-dormant in July-August heat -- this is normal
- The crepe myrtle afternoon shade is critical for surviving NC summers
- If leaves wilt badly in heat, water deeply in the evening and add extra mulch
- Consider a shade cloth for the worst weeks if plants look stressed

## Wildwood General Tips

- **Rotation matters.** Don't plant the same family in the same bed two years running. Tomatoes/peppers/eggplant (nightshades) in one rotation. Squash/cucumbers/melons (cucurbits) in another. Beans/peas (legumes) fix nitrogen -- plant them BEFORE heavy feeders like corn
- **Label everything.** You will not remember which tomato is Cherokee Purple vs Early Doll in August. Use stakes with permanent marker or metal plant tags
- **Harvest regularly.** Leaving ripe vegetables on the plant signals the plant to stop producing. Pick beans, cucumbers, and zucchini every 2-3 days for maximum yield
- **Keep a garden journal.** Note what worked, what failed, and when things happened. Future-you will thank present-you
