              C-OPENING (south)
              ~3 ft of opening

**Tools needed:** tape measure, 4–6 stakes or flags, twine optional

**Quick measurement method:**
- Drive a center stake
- Tie a 5 ft string to it; walk it around in a circle, marking the outer edge with flags every few feet → that's your 10 ft outer circle
- Shorten string to ~3 ft 6" → mark middle/outer ring boundary
- Shorten to ~2 ft → mark center/middle ring boundary
- Shorten to ~12" → that's the inner edge of the center "standing room" mulched zone
- Mark the C-opening on the south arc (~3 ft wide gap)

## STEP 4 — Plant Order (Center → Middle → Outer)

Once placed and you like the layout:

### Order of operations

1. **Center ring perennials FIRST** — these are the structural anchors
2. **Middle ring perennials SECOND** — fill in around the center
3. **Step back, look** — adjust if anything looks crowded or empty
4. **Outer ring live plants if any** — last
5. **Sharp grit ring** at each plant base (vole protection)
6. **DO NOT MULCH** the planting zones today — let plants breathe; mulch later

### Per-plant planting technique

For each perennial:

1. **Hole:** Dig slightly wider than the rootball, same depth. Don't dig deeper than the rootball — settling = the plant sinks too low.
2. **Test fit:** Set the rootball in. Top of rootball should be level with surrounding soil, NOT below.
3. **Backfill:** With native soil, no compost amendment for these (lean prairie/medicinal plants don't want rich soil).
4. **Tamp gently:** Press soil around the rootball with your hands. No stomping.
5. **Don't water:** Saturated soil + 16 hours of rain coming = nothing to add. (For Echinacea and other prairie plants, overly wet soil at planting can rot the crown — don't water in.)
6. **Sharp grit ring:** Sprinkle a small handful of pea gravel / sharp grit at the soil surface in a 6–8" ring around the crown. Voles hate digging through it.
7. **Move to next plant.**

### Planting position rules

- **Center ring:** plant on the NORTH side of the bed (tall plants don't shade short plants — south sun streams in)
- **Don't plant inside the C-opening** (south, ~3 ft gap kept open for visual entry + sun)
- **Walking room around the center "standing room"** — keep that 18–24" mulched core open

- [ ] **Light mulch** around perennials (NOT pressed against stems — keep mulch back 1–2")

### Sowing technique reminder

- Loosen the top inch of soil in each marked zone
- Scatter seeds at the spacing for that plant (see `Potion_Patch_Planting_Guide.md`)
- **Press in but don't bury small seeds** (chamomile, etc. need light to germinate)
- Water with fine spray if soil is dry; skip if soil is still moist
- Mark each zone with a label so seedlings aren't mistaken for weeds

## Vole Protection Quick Reference (Confirmed Voles)

For every plant going in today:

- **Sharp grit ring** at base (small handful pea gravel / coarse sand around crown)
- **No mulch piled against stems** when you do mulch later
- Optional: **castor oil-based repellent** sprinkled at the bed perimeter

Voles hate digging through sharp materials and dislike castor oil. Both methods are safe for snakes/owls/hawks (the natural vole control).
