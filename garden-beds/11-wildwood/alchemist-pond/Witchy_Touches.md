# Alchemist Pond — Witchy Touches

> The Alchemist Pond isn't just a frog pond. It's a scrying pool, a moon mirror, a threshold between the garden you tend and the world beneath the surface.

---

## 1. Dark Gazing Ball / Scrying Orb

**What:** A black or deep blue glass gazing ball, 6-10 inches.

**Where to place:** At the water's edge on the herb spiral side, partially nestled in the rim rocks. It should look like it rolled there and stopped. Not centered, not perfect — off to one side like it has its own gravity.

**Why it matters:**
- On full moons, the orb catches the moonlight and the water reflects it back
- Traditional scrying tool — gaze into it at dusk and let your eyes unfocus
- During the day it reflects the garden around it — a tiny dark window into another version of Wildwood

**Buy from:** Etsy (search "black gazing ball garden" or "obsidian garden sphere"), Amazon, or garden centers.

---

## 2. Moon Phase Garden Stakes

**What:** Metal or wooden stakes showing the 8 phases of the moon — new, waxing crescent, first quarter, waxing gibbous, full, waning gibbous, third quarter, waning crescent.

**Where to place:** Along the curve of the kidney shape, following the rim. Start with the new moon at the narrow waist of the kidney and end with the full moon at the widest part. The waxing phases follow the curve as the pond expands — the moon grows as the pond grows.

**Why it matters:**
- Marks the pond as a lunar feature in the garden
- Connects water (tides, cycles) to moon (cycles, intuition)
- Looks incredible at night with fairy lights catching the metal

**Buy from:** Etsy (tons of options — search "moon phase garden stakes"), Amazon.

---

## 3. The Cauldron

**What:** A small cast iron or ceramic cauldron, 6-8 inches across.

**Where to place:** On the ground at the south edge of the pond (the entry side), slightly off to one side. Plant water mint in it, or fill it with smooth black river stones, or leave it empty as a vessel. Your call.

**Options:**
- **Planted cauldron:** Water mint or creeping jenny spilling over the edges — alive, growing, witchy
- **Stone cauldron:** Smooth black river stones piled in it — grounding, heavy, permanent
- **Empty cauldron:** A vessel waiting to be filled — symbolic, open, ready

**Why it matters:**
- The Alchemist Pond is about transformation (tadpole → frog, water → life, garden → medicine)
- A cauldron is the original vessel of transformation
- Placed at the entry, it's the first thing you see approaching the pond

**Buy from:** Etsy, Amazon, thrift stores (cast iron cauldrons are common at antique shops).

---

## 4. Solar Fairy Lights

**What:** Warm white solar string lights (not cool white, not colored — warm like firelight).

**Where to place:** Two options:
- **Wrapped around a nearby branch** overhanging the pond — creates reflection on the water at night
- **Woven along the herb spiral** next to the pond — the spiral glows and the pond catches the light

**Why it matters:**
- At night, the pond surface becomes a dark mirror. Warm lights reflecting off it look like scattered stars.
- Creates ambiance for evening garden time without electricity
- Pairs with the gazing ball — light catches the orb and bounces off the water

**Buy from:** Amazon, Target, Dollar Tree (solar fairy lights are everywhere and cheap). Get warm white, 20-30 ft length.

---

## 5. PVC Frog Hotels

**What:** 4-inch diameter PVC pipe cut into 8-12 inch sections.

**Where to place:** Partially buried around the pond edge, 3-5 of them scattered at different points. Angle them slightly downward so rain drains out. Cover with leaf litter, moss, or mulch so they disappear into the landscape.

**Why it matters:**
- Frogs need damp, dark hiding spots during the day
- PVC stays cool inside even on hot days
- Hidden under leaves and moss, they look like natural hollows
- Toads especially love these — they'll move right in

**Witchy upgrade:** Paint the inside of each pipe with a different moon phase, or mark the outside with a rune before burying. Nobody will see it. That's the point.

**Buy from:** Home Depot or Lowe's — a 10ft section of 4" PVC pipe costs about $5-10. Cut it yourself.

---

## 6. Moss Encouragement

**What:** Not something you buy — something you invite.

**How:**
- Mist the rim rocks every morning for a week or two
- Transplant small patches of moss from shady spots in your yard
- Place them on the north-facing side of the rim rocks (shadier, stays damp longer)
- Stop mowing the grass right at the pond edge — let it get wild and mossy

**Why it matters:**
- Moss makes everything look like it's been there for a hundred years
- Aged, mossy stone is the aesthetic difference between "I built a pond" and "there's always been a pond here"
- Frogs love mossy surfaces — damp, textured, cool

---

## 7. Moonlight Harvesting (No Purchase Required)

**What:** Positioning the pond to catch moonlight.

**How:**
- Check where the moon rises from your pond's location (generally east/southeast)
- Clear any overhanging branches on the east side so moonlight hits the water surface directly
- On full moon nights, the entire pond surface becomes a mirror
- The gazing ball catches it, the water holds it, the fairy lights frame it

**Full moon ritual (if you want one):**
- Sit by the pond after dark on a full moon
- Let the water go still (turn off the aerator for the evening)
- Look into the surface
- That's it. That's the whole ritual. The pond does the rest.

---

## 8. Threshold Stones

**What:** Two larger stones flanking the south approach to the pond — a symbolic gateway.

**Where to place:** On either side of the path as you walk toward the pond from the garden. Not huge — knee height or lower. Just enough to mark: "you are entering the pond's space."

**Why it matters:**
- Every sacred space has a threshold
- The Potion Patch has its south opening. Fernwood has its Christmas fern entrance. The Alchemist Pond gets threshold stones.
- Walk between them and you're in the pond's world

**Source:** Scavenge from the yard, or pick up two nice stones from a landscape supply. Free to $20.

---

## Installation Order

1. **Threshold stones** — place these when you build the pond (Phase 2)
2. **Rim rocks + begin moss encouragement** — during build (Phase 4)
3. **PVC frog hotels** — bury these around the edge during build
4. **Cauldron** — place at south entry after pond is filled
5. **Gazing ball** — place at water's edge after plants are in
6. **Moon phase stakes** — install along the kidney curve
7. **Fairy lights** — add last, once everything is placed and you can see what looks best lit up
8. **Moonlight harvesting** — clear branches on east side anytime
