### Why 3 Tiers

- **Shelf 1 (6 inches):** Shallow edge where frogs hop in and out, birds drink, marginal plants root. The gentle slope is critical — frogs need to be able to walk in and climb out easily.
- **Shelf 2 (12 inches):** Middle depth for taller water plants and tadpole habitat. This is where most of the underwater life happens.
- **Deep Zone (24-30 inches):** Center — frogs hibernate here in winter mud. If it's too shallow, the whole thing freezes solid and kills overwintering frogs. This depth is non-negotiable.

## Critical Rules

- **No fish.** Fish eat frog eggs and tadpoles. This is a frog pond.
- **No chemicals.** Frogs absorb everything through their skin. No algaecides, no chlorine, no fertilizer runoff.
- **Don't clean it too well.** Some algae and leaf litter is good. That's habitat, not mess.
- **Gentle water movement only.** Frogs want calm, still-ish water for breeding. A soft bubbler, not a waterfall.
- **Gentle slopes, not walls.** Frogs and tadpoles need to climb out. Steep sides = death trap.
