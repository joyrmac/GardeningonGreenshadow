# Alchemist Pond — Build Guide

> Step-by-step construction from your current 3ft tarp hole to a proper 6-10ft frog sanctuary.

---

## What You're Starting With

- A ~3 ft round hole with a tarp in it
- Full of water and tree litter
- Next to the herb spiral
- Frogs already laying eggs in standing water around the yard

## What You're Building

- 6-10 ft kidney-shaped pond
- 3 tiers (6", 12", 24-30" deep)
- Lined with proper pond liner (you have this)
- Edged with natural stone
- Gentle slopes for frog access

---

## Tools You'll Need

- Shovel (round point for digging, flat for shaping shelves)
- Garden rake
- Level (or a straight board + level)
- Garden hose (to mark the shape AND to fill)
- Wheelbarrow (for moving dirt)
- Tape measure
- String and stakes (for marking)
- Utility knife (for trimming liner)
- Gloves

---

## Phase 1: Plan & Mark the Shape

### Step 1: Drain the Current Hole
- Bail or pump out the water
- Remove the old tarp
- Save any frog eggs or tadpoles! Scoop them into a bucket of the pond water and keep in shade. You'll put them back.

### Step 2: Lay Out the Kidney Shape
- Use a garden hose to outline the shape on the ground
- Kidney shape = two overlapping circles, one larger than the other
- The narrow "waist" of the kidney should be at least 3-4 ft wide
- Total length: 8-10 ft, total width at widest: 5-6 ft
- Orient with the curve toward the herb spiral (north side) so it feels like it belongs there
- Walk around it. Look at it from the bench area. Adjust until it feels right.
- Mark the outline with spray paint, flour, or sand once you're happy

### Step 3: Mark the Tiers
- The shallow shelf (6") runs all the way around the outside edge, about 12-18 inches wide
- The middle shelf (12") is inside that, about 12-18 inches wide
- The deep zone (24-30") is the center
- Think of it like a bathtub with two steps going down

## Phase 2: Dig

### Step 4: Dig the Shallow Shelf First
- Remove turf/grass from the entire marked area
- Dig the outer 12-18 inches to 6 inches deep all the way around
- Keep the edges gently sloped (NOT vertical walls)
- Use the level across a board to check depth is consistent
- Pile excavated dirt nearby — you may want some for backfilling

### Step 5: Dig the Middle Shelf
- Inside the shallow shelf, dig down to 12 inches
- Again, 12-18 inches wide all around
- Slope gently between shelves (no sharp drop-offs — frogs need gradual transitions)

### Step 6: Dig the Deep Zone
- Center of the pond, dig down to 24-30 inches
- This doesn't need to be huge — even a 2-3 ft diameter deep zone is enough for overwintering frogs
- Slope gently from the middle shelf down to the deep zone
- The bottom can be slightly rounded (bowl shape)

### Step 7: Check Your Work
- Stand back and look at the shape
- Run water from the hose to see how it fills — does it look natural?
- Check all slopes are gentle (stick your hand in — could a frog climb out anywhere?)
- Remove any sharp rocks, roots, or debris from the hole
- Compact the soil by pressing down with your feet or the back of the shovel

**Common mistake:** Making the sides too steep. Frogs, toads, and small wildlife need to walk in and out. Think beach, not swimming pool.

## Phase 3: Line the Pond

### Step 8: Lay the Underlayment
- Drape the geotextile underlayment fabric over the entire hole
- Press it into all the shelves and the deep zone
- Let it overlap the edges by at least 12 inches all around
- This protects your pond liner from punctures (roots, rocks, etc.)
- If you don't have underlayment, old carpet, old blankets, or thick layers of sand work too

### Step 9: Lay the Pond Liner
- Drape the pond liner over the underlayment
- Start from the center and work outward, pressing it into each tier
- Leave plenty of excess at the edges (at least 12-18 inches beyond the rim) — you'll trim later
- Don't try to get every wrinkle out. Some wrinkles are fine and will settle once water fills.
- On hot, sunny days the liner is more flexible and easier to work with

### Step 10: Secure the Edges Temporarily
- Place heavy rocks or bricks on the edges to hold the liner while you fill
- Don't trim the excess liner yet — wait until the pond is full and settled

### Liner Sizing Guide
For a 6-10 ft pond that's 30 inches deep:
- **Length of liner needed:** Pond length + (2 x max depth) + 2 ft overlap = ~10 + 5 + 2 = **17 ft**
- **Width of liner needed:** Pond width + (2 x max depth) + 2 ft overlap = ~6 + 5 + 2 = **13 ft**
- So you need roughly a **17 x 13 ft** piece minimum. Check your liner size.

---

## Phase 4: Add Structure

### Step 11: Add Gravel to the Shelves
- Cover Shelf 1 (6" shallow) with 1-2 inches of washed pea gravel or river pebbles
- Cover Shelf 2 (12" middle) with 1-2 inches of gravel
- This gives frogs and tadpoles grip, provides surface for beneficial bacteria, and looks natural
- Don't put gravel in the deep zone — leave that as bare liner so frogs can burrow into sediment that will naturally collect there

### Step 12: Place Rim Rocks
- Lay flat rocks all around the edge of the pond, overlapping the liner
- The rocks should overhang the water by an inch or two (creates shadow = frog hiding spots)
- Vary the sizes for a natural look — not a perfect ring of identical stones
- Press rocks firmly into the ground so they don't shift
- Now trim the excess liner — cut it so it's hidden under the rim rocks

### Step 13: Build the Frog Shelter
- On one edge (east or north side recommended), stack rocks or place a hollowed log
- Create gaps and crevices — frogs hide in dark, damp spaces during the day
- Half on land, half touching the water is ideal
- Moss will grow on this naturally if it stays damp

---

## Phase 5: Fill & Settle

### Step 14: Fill With Water
- Use a garden hose, fill slowly
- Watch the liner settle into the shape as water weight pushes it down
- Adjust any rocks or liner edges as it fills
- Fill to about 1-2 inches below the rim rocks

### Step 15: Wait 2-3 Days
- Let the water sit before adding plants
- If you're on city water, chlorine will evaporate in 24-48 hours (frogs are sensitive to chlorine)
- The water will look murky at first — that's normal
- If you saved tadpoles/frog eggs, put them back in now

### Step 16: Install Solar Aerator
- Place the solar panel where it gets good sun (south-facing, on a rock or stake)
- Run the air line into the water, place the bubble stone on Shelf 2 or in the deep zone
- Set to LOW/SOFT bubble mode — gentle aeration, not a jacuzzi
- This keeps mosquitoes from breeding while keeping frogs happy

---

## Phase 6: Plant & Decorate

### Step 17: Add Water Plants
- See **[Plant_Guide.md](Plant_Guide.md)** for full details
- Place potted plants on the shelves (don't dump bare root plants into the water)
- Floating plants (water lettuce, duckweed) just go on the surface
- Give plants a week to settle before fussing with them

### Step 18: Add Witchy Touches
- See **[Witchy_Touches.md](Witchy_Touches.md)** for the full plan
- Gazing ball, moon stakes, cauldron, fairy lights, etc.

### Step 19: Return Any Rescued Tadpoles
- Gently pour the bucket of saved tadpoles/eggs into the shallow shelf
- They'll find their way around

## Troubleshooting During Build

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Liner won't lay flat | Too cold outside | Wait for a warm day, or let the liner sit in the sun to soften |
| Water leaking | Liner puncture or edge too low | Check for sharp objects under liner. Ensure rim rocks are above waterline all the way around |
| Shelves crumbling | Loose soil | Compact more firmly, or use flat rocks to reinforce shelf edges |
| Can't get deep enough (hit clay/rock) | Subsoil conditions | 24 inches is the minimum for overwintering. If you can't dig deeper, mound dirt around the edges to create depth above ground level |
| Pond fills unevenly | Ground not level | Check with a level. Build up the low side with packed soil UNDER the liner |
