# Alchemist Pond — Maintenance Guide

> A frog pond is mostly hands-off. The ecosystem does the work. Your job is to not mess it up.

---

## The Golden Rule

**Less is more.** A healthy pond looks a little wild, a little green, a little messy. That's not neglect — that's habitat. Resist the urge to clean it like a swimming pool.

---

## Monthly Calendar

### SPRING (March - May)

**March:**
- Check the pond after winter — is the water level low? Top off with hose water.
- Look for frog eggs! Gelatinous clumps floating or attached to plant stems = you did it. Leave them alone.
- Check the liner — did anything shift over winter? Reposition rim rocks if needed.
- Clean out excessive leaf buildup from fall/winter (leave SOME — it's tadpole food).

**April:**
- Tadpoles should be hatching. You'll see tiny dark shapes wiggling in the shallows.
- Start the solar aerator for the season (soft mode).
- Check plants from last year — are they emerging? Divide anything that's outgrown its pot.
- Add new floating plants (water lettuce, duckweed) after last frost (~April 10 for Zone 7b).
- Mist rim rocks to encourage moss growth.

**May:**
- Plants fill in. Iris may bloom (blue-purple flowers).
- Tadpoles are growing. You'll see legs starting to form on the older ones.
- Top off water if it drops (evaporation increases with warmth).
- Remove excess duckweed if it's covering more than 60% of the surface — tadpoles and frogs need open water too.

### SUMMER (June - August)

**June:**
- Tadpoles start metamorphosing — front legs appear, tails shrink.
- Tiny froglets will climb out onto the rim rocks and frog hotels. This is the best day.
- Dragonflies should be visiting. They eat mosquitoes.
- Water level drops faster in heat — top off weekly or as needed.
- If water turns very green (algae bloom), it usually clears on its own. Don't add chemicals. Floating plants shade the water and outcompete algae for nutrients.

**July:**
- Froglets are out and about. You'll hear them at night.
- Remove excess floating plants if they're choking the surface.
- Check the aerator — is the solar panel clean? Wipe off dust/pollen.
- Water mint may need trimming if it's escaping its pot.
- Enjoy the firefly show at dusk near the water.

**August:**
- Heat peak. Water evaporates fastest. Top off as needed.
- Some plants may look tired — that's normal.
- Frogs are fully grown, eating slugs and insects in the garden. Thank them.
- This is the most hands-off month.

### FALL (September - November)

**September:**
- Plants start going dormant. Let them — don't cut back water plants yet.
- Falling leaves will land in the pond. Leave SOME (they become food and habitat).
- Remove only large accumulations that would smother the water completely.

**October:**
- Frogs start seeking out the deep zone for winter hibernation.
- Cut back dead plant material above the waterline if it looks messy, but leave root systems intact.
- Move the solar aerator to low/intermittent mode or turn it off — less oxygen demand in cold water.
- Consider placing a net over the pond if leaf fall is extreme (remove once leaves are done).

**November:**
- Frogs have burrowed into the mud in the deep zone. They're hibernating.
- Leave the pond alone from now until March.
- Turn off the aerator for winter.
- If you have water lettuce, it will die with frost. That's normal — buy new in spring.

### WINTER (December - February)

**December - February:**
- **Leave it alone.**
- The pond surface may ice over. That's okay if the deep zone is 24-30 inches — it won't freeze solid.
- **DO NOT break ice by hitting it.** The vibrations can kill hibernating frogs. If you need a hole in the ice for gas exchange, pour hot water gently to melt a spot.
- Don't add water, don't clean, don't touch.
- Snow on top of ice is fine.
- The frogs are alive under there. Trust the deep zone.

## Water Management

### Water Level
- Top off as needed (especially June-August)
- Use a garden hose — city water chlorine evaporates in 24 hours
- If you have a rain barrel, rainwater is better (no chlorine, no minerals)
- Never let the water level drop more than 3-4 inches below the rim — the shallow shelf plants dry out

### Water Quality
- **Green water (algae):** Normal, especially in spring when it's warm but plants haven't filled in yet. Floating plants fix this by shading the water and absorbing nutrients. Don't add chemicals.
- **Clear water:** You've achieved balance. Plants, algae, tadpoles, and bacteria are all in equilibrium.
- **Smelly water:** Too much decaying organic matter. Remove excess leaves/debris. Check that the aerator is working.
- **Oily film on surface:** Usually pollen. Skim with a paper towel or newspaper laid flat on the surface then lifted off.

### Topping Off vs. Water Changes
- **Top off:** Add water to replace evaporation. Do this regularly. Fine.
- **Water change:** Drain and replace water. **Almost never necessary.** Only if something toxic gets in (chemical spill, pesticide drift). A healthy pond never needs a water change.

## Plant Maintenance

| Task | When | How |
|------|------|-----|
| Divide overgrown plants | Spring (March-April) | Lift pot, split plant, repot in fresh aquatic soil |
| Trim water mint | Summer (as needed) | Cut back runners escaping the pot |
| Remove excess floating plants | Summer | Scoop with a net. Compost the excess. |
| Cut back dead foliage | Late fall (October) | Cut dead stems above waterline. Leave roots. |
| Replace tropical floaters | Spring (April) | Water lettuce dies in winter. Buy new each spring. |
| Refresh aquatic soil | Every 2-3 years | Lift pots, replace soil, replant |

## Frog Lifecycle at the Alchemist Pond

| Stage | When | What You'll See |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **Eggs** | Early spring (March-April) | Gelatinous clumps or strings attached to plants or floating. Leave them alone. |
| **Tadpoles** | Spring (April-May) | Tiny dark shapes wiggling. They eat algae and decaying leaves. |
| **Legs appear** | Late spring/early summer (May-June) | Back legs first, then front legs. Tail starts shrinking. |
| **Froglets** | Summer (June-July) | Tiny perfect frogs climbing out onto rim rocks. Amazing. |
| **Adult frogs** | Summer-fall | Eating slugs, insects, mosquitoes. Sitting on rim rocks at dusk. Singing at night. |
| **Hibernation** | Late fall-winter (Nov-Feb) | Burrowed into mud in the deep zone. Invisible. Alive. |
| **Return** | Spring (March) | They come back. They always come back. |

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Algae bloom (green water) | Excess nutrients, too much sun, not enough plants | Add more floating plants for shade. Don't fertilize near the pond. Wait — it usually balances itself. |
| Mosquito larvae | Stagnant water, aerator off | Turn on the aerator (even soft mode disrupts mosquito breeding). Frogs and tadpoles also eat mosquito larvae. |
| No frogs | Pond is new, or something is deterring them | Give it time. Frogs find water. Avoid pesticides in the whole yard. Make sure there's shelter (frog hotels, rock piles). |
| Frogs leaving | Water quality issue or no shelter | Check for chemical contamination. Add more hiding spots. Make sure shallow slopes let them exit easily. |
| Heron eating frogs | Heron found your pond | Add hiding spots (submerged plants, rock overhangs). A motion-activated sprinkler near the pond deters herons. |
| Raccoons digging | Raccoons disturbing the edge | Secure rim rocks more firmly. A motion sensor light may deter them. |
| Water level dropping fast | Evaporation (normal in summer) or liner leak | Top off regularly. If it drops more than an inch per day in cool weather, check for liner puncture. |
| Ice covering entire surface | Winter | Normal if pond is deep enough. Only melt a hole if you're worried about gas exchange. Don't break ice — pour hot water. |
| Plants dying in winter | Tropical plants can't survive frost | Water lettuce is annual in Zone 7b. Buy new in spring. Hardy plants (iris, cattail, rushes) go dormant but come back. |

## Annual Checklist

### Spring Startup (March)
- [ ] Check water level, top off
- [ ] Remove excessive leaf debris (leave some)
- [ ] Check liner and rim rocks
- [ ] Look for frog eggs
- [ ] Start solar aerator (soft mode)
- [ ] Divide overgrown plants
- [ ] Add new floating plants after last frost

### Summer Monitoring (June-August)
- [ ] Top off water weekly
- [ ] Remove excess floating plants monthly
- [ ] Clean solar panel
- [ ] Trim water mint if needed
- [ ] Watch for froglets emerging
- [ ] Enjoy it

### Fall Shutdown (October-November)
- [ ] Remove excessive leaves (leave some)
- [ ] Cut back dead plant foliage above waterline
- [ ] Turn off aerator
- [ ] Leave the pond alone until March
