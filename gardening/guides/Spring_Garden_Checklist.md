## MARCH 2026

### Week 1: March 9–15 — Direct Sowing & Indoor Starts

**Sunday, March 9 — Direct Sow Day (Full Day)**

*Morning:*
- [ ] Direct sow **carrots** (Danvers, Parisienne, Purple Dragon, Rainbow, Tendersweet)
  - Where: Wildwood beds
  - How: 1/4" deep, rows 12" apart, loose soil
  - Window: Closes March 16

- [ ] Direct sow **peas** (Sugar Daddy)
  - Where: Wildwood near future trellis
  - How: 1" deep, 2" apart
  - Window: Closes March 16

- [ ] Direct sow **spinach** (Bloomsdale Long Standing)
  - Where: Wildwood, partial shade OK
  - How: 1/2" deep, 3" apart
  - Window: Closes March 16

- [ ] Direct sow **dill**
  - Where: Herb spiral or veggie bed edge
  - How: Surface sow, press lightly
  - Window: Closes March 16

- [ ] Direct sow **chives**
  - Where: Herb spiral or bed border
  - How: 1/4" deep
  - Window: Closes March 16

*Afternoon:*
- [ ] Scatter **poppy seeds** on Bloomveil soil (Frosted Salmon, Purple Peony, Lilac PomPom, Jelly Beans)
  - How: Press in, don't cover. Need cool soil + light.

- [ ] Start **cantaloupe** (Sierra Gold) indoors in trays
  - Keep at 65-75F
  - Window: Closes March 16

- [ ] Start **cucumber** (Mexican Sour Gherkin) indoors
  - Keep at 65-75F
  - Window: Closes March 16

- [ ] Start **watermelon** (Bush Sugar Baby, Garden Leader Monster) indoors
  - Keep at 65-75F
  - Window: Closes March 16

**Monday, March 10 — 5 min**
- [ ] Check all indoor seed trays — mist if dry, keep 65-75F

**Tuesday, March 11 — 15 min**
- [ ] Check trays
- [ ] Direct sow **parsley** (Italian Flat Leaf)
  - Where: Herb spiral or veggie bed edge
  - How: Scatter, press lightly
  - Window: Mar 8–23

**Wednesday, March 12 — 20 min**
- [ ] Check trays
- [ ] **Magnolia care** — hose off mealyworm clusters, prune sticky/damaged twigs

**Thursday, March 13 — 15 min**
- [ ] Check trays
- [ ] **Magnolia part 2** — spray neem oil on trunk and lower branches, add mulch ring around base

**Friday, March 14 — 15 min**
- [ ] Check trays
- [ ] Direct sow **onions** (Evergreen White Nebuka + Yellow Sweet Spanish)
  - Where: Wildwood beds
  - How: 1/2" deep
  - Window: Mar 8–30

**Saturday, March 15 — Bee Balm + Raspberry Transplant (Half Day)**

*Bee Balm Division:*
- [ ] Find overgrown bee balm clumps (dense mats of square stems)
- [ ] Water the night before or morning of
- [ ] Use sharp spade to divide into 3-5 sections (each needs roots + stems)
- [ ] Shake off excess dirt, toss dead woody pieces
- [ ] **Plant immediately:**
  - 2-3 clumps → **Wayward Meadow** (18-24" apart, water in)
  - 1-2 clumps → **Alchemist's Pond edge** (in buried container, north side)
  - Extras → Pots for giveaway/storage
- [ ] Water deeply, mulch around all transplants

*Raspberry Transplant:*
- [ ] Dig up 4 Red Latham canes (keep root balls intact, wrap in damp burlap)
- [ ] Mark row 3-4 ft south of blueberry row
- [ ] Mix planting soil: dirt pile + brush pile compost + peat moss
- [ ] Dig 4 holes (4-5 ft apart, 18" wide x 12" deep)
- [ ] Plant at same depth as original
- [ ] Set 2 trellis posts at row ends
- [ ] Run wire/jute at 2 ft and 4 ft heights
- [ ] Sink root barrier 12" deep along south edge
- [ ] Mulch 4-6" thick (keep 6" from stems)
- [ ] Mulch/clear 3-4 ft picking aisle between rows
- [ ] Scatter chive seeds along both row edges
- [ ] Water deeply

*If time:*
- [ ] Direct sow **turnips** if desired (Window: Mar 8–30)
- [ ] Confirm all 5 direct-sow crops + 3 indoor starts done

  - Remember: 47 vegetables need homes (use inventory audit)

**Friday, March 21 — 10 min**
- [ ] Check trays

**Saturday, March 22 — Lettuce + Weeding + Butterfly Puddler (Half Day)**

- [ ] **Transplant lettuce seedlings** outdoors if you have starts
  - Window: Mar 23–Apr 20
  - Prep lettuce/greens row in Wildwood first

- [ ] **Weed pass**
  - Sunflower bed
  - Blueberry patch
  - Herb spiral area

- [ ] Lightly weed under Tulip Magnolia

- [ ] **Build butterfly puddler** near Bloomveil (see Butterfly_Puddler_Styling_Guide.md)

  - Nestle 2-4 raw crystals (clear quartz, amethyst, citrine)

  - Ring with creeping thyme, sedum, or moss

  - Surround with low herbs (thyme, lavender, sedum)
  - Plant companions nearby: butterflyweed (2-3), coneflowers (2-3)
  - Optional: solar lantern + hanging bell (add in May)

## APRIL 2026

### Early April (April 5-10) — Strawberry Bed Prep

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Receive transplants** (should arrive by now)

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Confirm bed location**
  - Full sun, well-draining, accessible

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Buy soil amendments**
  - [ ] Compost (3-4 cu ft bulk) — $15-$25
  - [ ] Peat moss (2-3 cu ft) — $8-$12
  - [ ] Perlite (1 cu ft bag) — $5-$8
  - [ ] Straw mulch (1-2 bales) — $5-$10
  - [ ] Drip irrigation kit (optional) — $30-$50

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Prep soil**
  - [ ] Clear site (remove grass, weeds, rocks)
  - [ ] Spread compost + peat + perlite on top (6-8" total)
  - [ ] Till or dig in to 8-10" depth
  - [ ] Break up clumps, remove rocks
  - [ ] Rake smooth, level
  - [ ] Water gently to settle

### Mid-April (April 15-20) — Strawberry Planting Day + Tender Crops

**🍓 STRAWBERRY: PLANTING (2-3 hours)**
- [ ] Mark 3 rows with string (spacing: 2-3 ft apart)
- [ ] Dig holes 12-18" apart in each row
- [ ] **Plant 15-21 strawberry transplants**
  - Set crown exactly at soil level (critical!)
  - Firm soil gently around crown
  - Water immediately after each plant
- [ ] Mulch with 2-3" straw (not touching crown)
- [ ] Set up drip irrigation or plan hand-watering schedule

**🍓 STRAWBERRY: April Care Plan**
- [ ] Water: 1" per week if no rain
- [ ] Weed: 2x weekly
- [ ] Pinch: Remove all flowers until June 1 (focus on roots/runners)
- [ ] Runners: Remove all except 1-2 per plant
- [ ] Monitor: Slugs, pests, rot

**Tender Crop Planting (After April 15, all frost danger passed):**
- [ ] **Bloomveil annuals** (after last frost April 6, plant after April 15)
  - Zinnias, cosmos, dahlias, snapdragons, etc.
  - See Bloomveil.md for spacing/placement
  - Mark zones, plant according to color themes

- [ ] **Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant** (if hardened off and 60F+ soil)
  - Stake/cage tomatoes at planting time
  - Pinch first flower clusters on peppers

- [ ] **Cantaloupe, watermelon, cucumber** transplants
  - Soil must be 60F+ consistently
  - Harden off before planting
  - Direct sow option if not already started

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: HARVEST SEASON BEGINS**

  - Start weekly harvest journal

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Ongoing care**
