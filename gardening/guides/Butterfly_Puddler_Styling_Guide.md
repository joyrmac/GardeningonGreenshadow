# Butterfly Puddler — Witchy Garden Edition

> A shallow water station for butterfly hydration, styled as a magical focal point in Bloomveil. Built March 22, 2026.

---

## What It Is

A **butterfly puddler** is a shallow dish filled with water, sand, and pebbles. Butterflies drink from the moist sand (they absorb water and minerals). It's both functional and gorgeous—a perfect witchy garden focal point.

**Size:** 12-18" diameter, 1-2" deep

**Lifespan:** Season-long (refill water every 2-3 days in hot weather). Drain and store or treat for winter by November.

---

## Build: Step-by-Step

### Materials Needed

| Item | Qty | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Vessel** | 1 | Vintage ceramic bowl, shallow stone dish, brass basin, or terracotta saucer. Must be at least 1-2" deep. |
| **Sand** | 2-3 lbs | Play sand, fine construction sand, or silt (NOT coarse sandpaper grit). Rinse before use to remove dust. |
| **Pebbles** | 2-3 lbs | River stones, pea gravel, small pebbles (butterflies perch here to drink). Mix sizes. |
| **Mica flakes or crystals** | Handful | Optional but magical — raw mica adds shimmer. Clear quartz chunks (2-4) are perfect. |
| **Water** | As needed | Collected rainwater preferred. Refill every 2-3 days. |
| **Border plants** (optional) | 3-5 | Creeping thyme, sedum, moss, small Irish moss (Sagina subulata). |

### Assembly

1. **Choose location:** Near Bloomveil southern edge, visible from main garden paths, with dappled shade in afternoon (prevents algae overgrowth, keeps water cool).

2. **Place the vessel:** Set on stable, level ground. If uneven, use sand to level the base.

3. **Layer the bottom:**
   - **Bottom:** 1" of damp sand
   - **Middle:** 1" of pea gravel mixed with pebbles
   - **Top optional:** Mica flakes scattered across surface for shimmer and sparkle

4. **Nestle crystals:** Place 2-4 raw quartz chunks (clear, amethyst, or citrine) in/around the sand so tips poke out slightly. Bury them just enough to look intentional—not embedded, not floating.

5. **Add water:** Fill the vessel to just below the rim so water saturates the sand layer. Butterflies will drink from the wet sand.

6. **Optional border:** Ring the puddler with creeping plants:
   - **Creeping thyme** (Thymus serpyllum) — 3 plants, 12" apart
   - **Sedum** (dragon's blood or wooly sedum) — 2-3 plants
   - **Moss** — scatter around base for woodland witchy vibe

## Styling: Making It Magical

### Crystal Placement

- **Center arrangement:** 1 large clear quartz point (3-4") centered in sand
- **Flanking stones:** 2-3 smaller chunks (amethyst, citrine, rose quartz) placed at the cardinal points (N, S, E, W)
- **Buried shimmer:** 3-4 mica flakes just under the surface for a secret sparkle

**Intention:** Crystals amplify butterfly attraction and add earth/air magic. As water reflects light, crystals catch the shimmer—pure photography gold.

### Surround & Backdrop

1. **Immediate ring (0-12" out):**
   - Moss-covered stones or branches
   - Low herb clusters (thyme, sedum)
   - Strategically placed feathers (from birds in garden)
   - Small scattered pebbles for texture

2. **Secondary ring (12-24" out):**
   - Butterflyweed (orange, attracts monarchs) planted to the south
   - Lavender or hyssop to the west
   - Low coneflowers or zinnias to the east
   - Tall cosmos or joe-pye weed to the north (backdrop)

3. **Optional decor elements:**
   - Small hanging bell from a shepherd's crook (placed 2-3 ft back)
   - Vintage brass planter or watering can (placed to the side, not blocking view)
   - Round mirror (12" diameter, garden-safe) placed facing south to catch morning light

## Month-by-Month Care

### March (Build Month)

- [ ] Gather vessel and materials
- [ ] Prep location (clear weeds, level ground)
- [ ] Assemble puddler (March 22, 2026)
- [ ] Plant border herbs immediately
- [ ] Water daily for first week to settle plants

### April–May

- [ ] Top-up water every 2-3 days (more often in warm weather)
- [ ] Deadhead border flowers
- [ ] Butterflies begin visiting (first sightings usually late April)
- [ ] Refresh crystals monthly (rinse gently, reposition if moved)

### June–August (Peak Season)

- [ ] Check water daily—it evaporates fast in heat
- [ ] Stir sand occasionally to prevent algae (but don't dump out)
- [ ] Watch for mosquito larvae (they appear as tiny lines in water). If present, dump water + sand, refill with fresh sand/water
- [ ] Deadhead border plants to keep them blooming
- [ ] Note butterfly species visiting (monarchs, swallowtails, etc.)
- [ ] Take photos at dusk when light hits crystals and mirrors

### September–October

- [ ] Water less frequently (cooler temps)
- [ ] Collect fallen feathers, arrange around puddler
- [ ] Plant fall perennials nearby if desired
- [ ] Brush away autumn leaf litter weekly (prevents rot)

### November (Winter Prep)

- [ ] Drain puddler completely
- [ ] Remove vessel and store indoors (prevents frost cracks)
- [ ] Collect and store crystals
- [ ] Leave border plants (most are perennial); they'll come back in spring
- [ ] Optional: cover area with mulch, place a winter sign ("Sleeping Garden")

### December–February

- [ ] Storage month. Inspect vessel for cracks
- [ ] Rinse crystals, store in cloth bag in cool place
- [ ] Plan spring refresh (new plants? new mirror? new crystals?)

## Troubleshooting

### Mosquito Larvae in Water
**Problem:** Tiny worm-like lines appear in the water 1-2 weeks after filling.
**Fix:** Drain everything. Scrub vessel with stiff brush. Refill with fresh sand + water. Add a pinch of sand on top to create micro-habitat for good bacteria. Change water weekly in hot weather.

### Sand Turning Green/Slimy
**Problem:** Algae growth.
**Fix:** Stir sand to break up algae mats. If severe, replace sand. Add 1 leaf from border plants (shade helps prevent algae). Don't use chlorinated tap water.

### No Butterflies Visiting
**Problem:** Built puddler but no butterflies.
**Solution:** Ensure nearby flowering plants are in bloom (Spice Drift, Berry Patch nearby). Butterflies visit *peak* bloom season (June–August). Wait a few weeks. Make sure area isn't sprayed with pesticides.

### Crystals Going Cloudy
**Problem:** Rainwater or exposure dulls crystal surface.
**Fix:** Rinse gently with collected rainwater. Let dry. Doesn't affect magical properties—cloudiness is protective coating!

### Vessel Cracks in Winter
**Problem:** Ceramic bowls can frost-crack if left full of water.
**Fix:** Always drain completely by November. Store indoors.
