## Detailed Tasks

### Strawberry Harvest Routine

**How to Pick (Optimally):**
- **Time:** Early morning (6-8 am) — berries firmest, sweetest
- **Method:** Gently twist at the stem, don't crush
- **Ripe:** Fully red (even light pink isn't ripe), soft, fragrant
- **Frequency:** Every 2-3 days (don't let overripe fruit rot on plant)
- **Where to keep:** Shallow container (not piled high — crushing), refrigerate immediately

**Daily Harvest Tracking:**
- Keep journal: date, variety, qty, condition (perfect/slightly soft/overripe)
- Expected June total: Chandler should give 10-20 lbs by month-end
- Day-neutral (Albion, San Andreas): Light harvest 2-5 lbs combined
- Seascape (everbearing): Light 1-2 lbs

**Pest Management During Harvest:**
- **Remove immediately:** Any rotting or diseased berries (don't let disease spread)
- **Slugs:** Most problematic at harvest. Hand-pick under mulch, use beer traps.
- **Earwigs:** Stuff rolled newspapers near base at night, shake into soapy water morning
- **Birds:** Netting if damage excessive (usually not major in June)

**Summer Care During Harvest Season:**
- **Water:** 1.5-2" per week (more critical now than ever!)
- **Fertilize:** Weekly (10-10-10 or strawberry formula) — heavy harvest drains plants
- **Runners:** Still manage (keep 1-2 per plant)
- **Weeds:** Remove any competitors
- **Mulch:** Keep 2-3" but away from crown (helps moisture retention + pest reduction)

### Bloomveil Peak Season Care

**Watering (Critical in June Heat)**
- Target: 1.5-2" per week in heat
- Best method: Deep watering 2x weekly rather than frequent shallow
- Time: Early morning (6-8 am)
- Watch for wilting — signal of stress

**Deadheading (Tedious but Essential)**
- Daily passes (or every other day minimum)
- Cut or pinch spent flower heads
- This forces continuous new blooms all summer
- Leave a few flowers on tall plants (let seeds form if desired)

**Staking/Support**
- By mid-June, some flowers may flop in heat/rain
- Install stakes if wind damage occurring
- Use soft ties (fabric strips, not plastic ties)

**Fertilizing**
- Weekly 10-10-10 balanced or flower-specific formula
- Keeps plants pushing continuous blooms in heat stress
- Follow package directions

**Pest Management (Heat = Pest Season)**
- **Spider mites:** Most common in June heat. Signs: tiny webbing on leaves, mottled color. Fix: Hose plants mid-morning with water, or neem oil.
- **Powdery mildew:** If humidity + heat combo. Thin plants for air circulation, sulfur dust if severe.
- **Slugs:** Still active. Hand-pick, beer traps, eggshell rings.
- **Earwigs:** Eating petals. Newspaper traps at night.
- **Caterpillars:** Less common. Remove by hand.

**Mulch Management**
- Keep 2-3" depth (moisture retention)
- Keep 1" away from stems (rot prevention)
- Top-up as it decomposes
- Benefits: moisture conservation, slug reduction, temperature regulation

### Butterfly Puddler Active Season

**Daily Maintenance**
- **Refill water:** Water evaporates FAST in June heat
  - May need filling daily!
  - Keep sand moist to saturation
- **Monitor mosquitoes:** If tiny worm-like larvae appear, change water/sand
- **Clean rim:** Remove pollen, debris

**Butterfly Documentation**
- **Watch for visitors:** Should be regular (monarchs, swallowtails, tiger swallowtails, etc.)
- **Best times:** Mid-morning (9-11 am) or late afternoon (5-7 pm)
- **Camera ready:** Have camera/phone handy for photos
- **Journal:** Record species, behaviors, plant interactions

**Surrounding Plant Care**
- Butterflyweed, coneflowers, lavender, zinnias all in bloom nearby
- Keep deadheaded (encourages more blooms = more nectar/pollen)
- Water regularly (butterflies need blooms!)
- Pest management: any damage = fewer flowers = fewer butterflies
