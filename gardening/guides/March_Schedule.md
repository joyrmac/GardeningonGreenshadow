### Saturday, March 15 — Bee Balm + Raspberry Transplant (half day)

**Bee Balm division (do this first):**
1. Find the overgrown clumps. Look for dense mats of square stems with runners spreading out from the center
2. Water the clumps the night before (or morning of) so the soil is soft
3. Use a sharp spade to cut straight down through the root mass — divide each clump into 3-5 sections. Each section needs roots + a few stems
4. Shake off excess dirt. Toss any dead/woody center pieces on the brush pile — the edges are the healthiest growth
5. **Plant divisions immediately** — don't let roots dry out. Where they go:

| Division | Destination | How |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| 2-3 clumps | **Wayward Meadow** | Direct in ground, 18-24" apart. Water in. These will naturalize on their own |
| 1-2 clumps | **Alchemist's Pond edge** | Plant in a **buried container** — cut the bottom off a nursery pot, sink 10" deep. North side of pond area |
| Extras | **Pots** to give away or hold for later | Any standard pot with drainage |

6. Water all transplants deeply. Mulch around them. They'll look sad for a week — that's normal

**Raspberry transplant to Blueberry Bed:**
- [ ] Dig up 4 Red Latham canes from current spot. Keep root balls intact, wrap in damp burlap/bag
- [ ] Mark a row 3-4 ft south of (parallel to) the blueberry row
- [ ] Scoop planting soil from dirt pile + compost from brush pile bottom, mix with peat moss
- [ ] Dig 4 holes, 4-5 ft apart, 18" wide x 12" deep. Plant at same depth they were growing
- [ ] Set 2 trellis posts at each end of row (brush pile forked branches or T-posts)
- [ ] Run wire or jute twine at 2 ft and 4 ft heights
- [ ] Sink root barrier 12" deep along south (outer) edge
- [ ] Mulch 4-6" thick with brush pile material (keep 6" from stems)
- [ ] Mulch or clear the 3-4 ft picking aisle between blueberry and raspberry rows
- [ ] Scatter chive seeds along edges of both rows
- [ ] Water deeply

*(See Small_Gardens.md "Blueberry & Raspberry Bed" for full details)*

- [ ] **Build/install butterfly puddler** near Bloomveil (see *Butterfly_Puddler_Styling_Guide.md* for full details)

  **Quick build (1–2 hours):**
  - Vintage ceramic bowl or stone dish (12-18" diameter) as vessel
  - Layer: 1" damp sand + 1" pea gravel + pebbles mixed with river stones
  - Scatter mica flakes for shimmer
  - Nestle 2-4 raw crystals (clear quartz, amethyst, citrine) in the sand
  - Fill with water (saturate sand layer)
  - Ring with creeping thyme, sedum, or moss

  **Styling touches:**
  - Place near Bloomveil southern edge (dappled afternoon shade)
  - Surround with low herbs: thyme, lavender, sedum
  - Plant companions nearby (south-side): butterflyweed (2-3), coneflowers (2-3)
  - Optional: solar lantern nearby (add in May), small hanging bell on shepherd's crook

  **Maintenance:** Refill water every 2-3 days. Butterflies will visit once nearby flowers bloom (late April onward).

  **Shopping list:** See *Bloomveil_Witchy_Decor_Shopping_List.md* for crystals, vessels, plants, decor budget.
