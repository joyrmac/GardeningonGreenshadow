# Soil Notes

> Soil types, amendments, and pH requirements referenced across the garden plans.

---

## General Property Soil

- **Soil test:** Not yet done (recommend getting one from local extension office — costs ~$4-15 and tells you pH, nutrients, and organic matter)
- **Typical NC Piedmont soil:** Clay-heavy, slightly acidic (pH 5.5-6.5), holds moisture but drains slowly
- **Typical IL soil:** Silty clay loam, slightly acidic to neutral (pH 6.0-7.0), fertile but compacts easily

---

## Amendments by Purpose

| Goal | Amendment | How Much | Notes |
|------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Improve drainage (clay soil) | Coarse sand, perlite, or pine bark fines | 2-3" worked into top 6-8" | Never add sand alone to clay — it makes concrete. Always mix with organic matter |
| Add organic matter | Compost, aged manure, leaf mold | 2-4" top-dressed or worked in | The universal fix. Do this every year |
| Acidify (lower pH) | Elemental sulfur, peat moss, pine fines | Per soil test instructions | For blueberries (pH 4.5-5.5), azaleas, hydrangeas |
| Raise pH | Garden lime (calcium carbonate) | Per soil test instructions | Rarely needed in NC/IL — most garden soil is already slightly acidic |
| Nitrogen boost | Blood meal, fish meal, alfalfa meal | Sprinkle and scratch in | For heavy feeders (corn, tomatoes, squash) |
| Phosphorus (root/bloom) | Bone meal | Mix into planting holes | Helps transplants establish. Good for bulbs and tubers |
| Potassium (overall health) | Wood ash, kelp meal, greensand | Light application | Don't overdo wood ash — it raises pH fast |
| Worm castings | Worm castings | 5-10% of soil mix | Gentle all-purpose fertilizer. Great for containers |

## Soil Needs by Garden

| Garden | Soil Type | Key Requirements |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Bloomveil** | Rich, well-draining | Amend clay with compost + perlite. Dahlias rot in wet soil — drainage is critical |
| **Edgeworth Corner** | Average to rich | Compost at planting. Most pollinator plants tolerate poor soil |
| **Wildwood (veggie beds)** | Rich, fertile | Heavy compost, worm castings. Corn and tomatoes are heavy feeders |
| **Wildwood (corn circle)** | Rich | Buckwheat cover crop planted 3/8/26 — turn under before corn planting to add organic matter and nitrogen |
| **Fernwood (shade)** | Rich, moisture-retaining | Amend with leaf mold and compost. Shade soil stays moist — don't over-amend with water-retaining material |
| **Cat Garden** | Average | No special needs. No cedar mulch (toxic to cats) |
| **Blueberry & Raspberry Bed** | Acidic, well-draining | Both crops need pH 4.5-6.5. Amend with peat moss, pine fines, elemental sulfur. Dirt pile + brush pile compost as base |
| **Wayward Meadow** | Lean, unamended | Prairie plants prefer poor soil. Do NOT fertilize or add compost — rich soil favors weeds |
| **Herb Spiral** | Well-draining, lean | Most herbs prefer poor-to-average soil. Rich soil makes herbs leggy with weak flavor |
| **Porch Planters** | Container mix | 70% existing + 20-25% compost + 5-10% worm castings. Refresh top 4-6" annually |

## Mulch Guide

| Mulch Type | Best For | Avoid For |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| Pine bark / pine straw | Blueberries, raspberries, acid-lovers | -- |
| Shredded hardwood | General beds, paths | -- |
| Straw | Veggie beds, overwintering | Ornamental beds (looks rough) |
| Leaf mold | Shade gardens, ferns | -- |
| Cedar mulch | General beds | **Cat Garden** (cedar oil toxic to cats) |
| Gravel / river stone | Herb spiral, dry creek | Anywhere you want to dig later |

**General rule:** 2-3" depth, 1" gap from plant stems (prevents rot).

## Cover Crops

| Crop | When to Plant | When to Turn Under | Purpose |
|------|--------------|-------------------|---------|
| Buckwheat | Spring/summer | Before flowering finishes (6-8 weeks) | Fast organic matter, attracts pollinators, suppresses weeds |
| Crimson Clover | Fall | Early spring before planting | Fixes nitrogen, beautiful flowers |
| Winter Rye | Late fall | Early spring (3-4 weeks before planting) | Prevents erosion, adds organic matter, suppresses weeds |

**Corn Circle:** Buckwheat cover crop planted 3/8/26. Turn under before planting corn/beans/squash.

## Quick Reference

- **Finger test:** Stick finger 1" into soil. Moist = don't water. Dry = water.
- **Soil temp for dahlia planting:** 60F+ consistently (use a soil thermometer)
- **Soil temp for corn/beans/squash:** 60F+
- **Soil temp for tomatoes/peppers:** 65F+
- **Never work wet clay soil** — it compacts into bricks. Wait until it crumbles when squeezed.
