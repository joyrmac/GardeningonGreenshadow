**SUNFLOWER & BLUEBERRY GARDEN PLAN**  
  
*35’ x 4’ Bed | Sun-drenched, soul-fed, pollinator-blessed*  
  
⸻  
  
**Vision & Vibe**  
  
A radiant, wildflower-anchored garden where sunflowers grow in joyful, sun-kissed disarray and blueberries form a grounded, fruitful front row. Structured, dreamy, and intentional—with just enough wild to feel like a summer memory come alive.  
  
⸻  
  
**GARDEN LAYOUT**  
  
**Orientation:** East to West rows (for ideal sun exposure)  
**Style:** Blueberries = structured / Sunflowers = loose & wild / Overall = romantic order  
  
**Back Wall (North Edge)**  
	•	**Boston Ivy** – 2–3 starts spaced 2–3 ft apart  
*Climbing backdrop for a dreamy garden frame.*  
  
**Back Row – Tall Sunflowers (24” apart)**  
	•	American Giant Hybrid  
	•	Mammoth Russian  
	•	Mammoth (Burpee)  
  
**Middle Row – Medium Sunflowers (18” apart)**  
	•	Busy Bee  
	•	Mexican Sunflower Torch  
  
**Front Row – Short Sunflowers (12” apart)**  
	•	Giant Teddy Bear  
	•	Tithonia Mexican Fiesta Del Sol  
  
**Front Edge (South) – Blueberry Row**  
	•	**O’Neal** (earliest ripening – late May)  
	•	**Patriot** (early June)  
	•	**Bluecrop** (mid-June)  
	•	**Legacy** (late July)  
  
*Spacing: 4–5 ft apart. Structured, evenly spaced along the front of the bed. Mulch heavily and acidify soil if needed.*  
  
⸻  
  
**Soil Preparation**  
	•	**Clear the bed** of weeds and debris  
	•	**Loosen soil** 12” deep  
	•	Add **Soil3 Veggie Compost**:  
	•	Spread 2–3 inches across the bed  
	•	Work gently into the top 6–8 inches  
	•	Optional for blueberries: Mix in peat moss, pine fines, or elemental sulfur to maintain pH between 4.5–5.5  
	•	Apply 10-10-10 NPK fertilizer (or a berry-safe blend) during prep for sunflowers  
  
⸻  
  
**Sowing & Planting Calendar (Zone 7b - Raleigh Area)**  
  
**Mid-Late March (Now through April 1)**  
	•	Prepare soil with Soil3  
	•	Plant/transplant blueberry bushes  
	•	Start Boston Ivy  
	•	Direct sow sunflowers if the soil is **consistently 55–60°F** (or wait until early April)  
  
**Early April (April 1–10)**  
	•	Direct sow **short & medium sunflowers**  
	•	Continue transplanting Boston Ivy & nasturtiums  
  
**Mid April (April 10–20)**  
	•	Direct sow **tall sunflowers** (warmer soil)  
	•	Plant thyme or yarrow if using  
	•	Mulch heavily under blueberries with pine straw or bark  
  
**Late April**  
	•	Stake tall sunflowers (American Giants, Mammoths)  
	•	Add stepping stones or light layer of decorative mulch  
	•	Install solar lights and signs  
  
⸻  
  
**Mulch & Maintenance**  
	•	Use straw, pine bark, or wood chips to retain moisture  
	•	Deep water 1–2x weekly (avoid overhead watering)  
	•	Thin sunflower seedlings at 6” tall  
	•	Fertilize sunflowers again when they reach 12” tall  
	•	Prune blueberries in winter  
	•	Remove old or dead sunflower heads post-bloom to prolong show  
  
⸻  
  
**Essential Companions (Just a Few, Darling)**  
	•	**Nasturtiums** – plant at corners for color + aphid control  
	•	**Creeping Thyme** – between blueberries or stones for low, fragrant cover  
	•	**Yarrow** – just 2–3, tucked softly in sunny spots for pollinator love  
  
⸻  
  
**Magical Touches**  
	•	**Solar Path Lights** – warm, amber glow at dusk  
	•	**Garden Signs/Plaques** – laser-etched stone, woodburned scrap, or salvaged tin  
	•	**Name Markers for Each Blueberry** (e.g., “Legacy – July Queen”)  
	•	**Stone Stepping Path** along front or side  
	•	**Bee Watering Dish** – shallow saucer with pebbles  
  
⸻  
  
**Name Ideas for the Garden**

	•	**The Sunberry Arc** – golden and fruitful  
	•	**Bluebeam Garden** – light + fruit  
	•	**Sunspun Edge** – whimsical, romantic

**2025 Garden Care Calendar – Blueberries & Sunflowers**  
  
*Zone 7b | Raleigh | Soil3 Veggie Mix | March–December*  
  
⸻  
  
**MARCH**  
  
**Blueberries (in pots)**  
	•	Place in full sun  
	•	Water 2–3x/week if dry (Soil3 drains fast)  
	•	Remove any flower buds from *O’Neal*  
	•	Begin prepping ground for future transplant (add pine bark or peat if needed)  
  
**Sunflower Bed**  
	•	Clear bed of weeds & debris  
	•	Loosen soil 12” deep  
	•	Mix in **Soil3 Veggie Compost** across full bed  
	•	Apply **10-10-10 fertilizer** or organic bloom booster  
	•	Plant Boston Ivy starts on north back wall  
	•	Map bed with string or stone markers: 3 rows (Tall, Medium, Short)  
  
⸻  
  
**APRIL**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	Mid-month: Transplant all 4 bushes into their in-ground homes  
	•	Space 4–5 ft apart along front (south) edge of bed  
	•	Blend Soil3 with pine fines or peat moss if needed  
	•	Mulch 2–3” deep with pine straw or bark  
	•	Begin consistent watering after transplant (1–2x/week)  
	•	Place stepping stones or plant markers by each bush  
  
**Sunflowers**  
	•	Early April: Direct sow **short & medium** varieties  
	•	Mid-April: Direct sow **tall varieties** (once soil is warm)  
	•	Sow in loose patterns—not rigid rows—for a wild look  
	•	Water gently after planting  
	•	Add straw or wood chip mulch between rows for moisture retention  
	•	Optional: Tuck a few **nasturtiums** at corners or thyme under berries  
  
⸻  
  
**MAY**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	Keep watering 1–2x/week  
	•	Watch O’Neal & Patriot for first blooms—remove flowers from *O’Neal* if still young  
	•	Add small dose of berry fertilizer mid-month  
	•	Top off mulch if needed  
  
**Sunflowers**  
	•	Thin seedlings when 6” tall—keep the strongest  
	•	Stake Mammoths & American Giants early  
	•	Add solar lights or bee bath  
	•	Watch for aphids—hand-remove or spray with soapy water  
  
⸻  
  
**JUNE**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	**Harvest O’Neal + Patriot** if they’re ready!  
	•	Begin harvesting **Bluecrop** mid-month  
	•	Water deeply during dry weeks  
	•	Optional: Apply compost tea or light organic feed post-harvest  
  
**Sunflowers**  
	•	Water 1–2x/week at base (avoid overhead watering)  
	•	Remove faded blooms to prolong flower show  
	•	Snap photos of the wild beauty unfolding!  
  
⸻  
  
**JULY**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	**Harvest Legacy** through mid to late July  
	•	Light prune for shape if needed (no hard cuts yet)  
	•	Water deeply in heat waves  
  
**Sunflowers**  
	•	Continue watering  
	•	Let some heads dry on the stalk if you want to save seeds  
	•	Stake or tie up any floppers  
  
⸻  
  
**AUGUST**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	Lightly top-dress with compost or fresh mulch  
	•	Water as needed  
	•	Let them rest after final fruiting  
	•	Remove any suckers or water sprouts  
  
**Sunflowers**  
	•	Harvest seeds once heads turn brown on back  
	•	Let some drop naturally for wildlife  
	•	Pull dead stalks gradually if the area gets messy  
  
⸻  
  
**SEPTEMBER**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	Clean up base of plants—remove weeds, old mulch if needed  
	•	Observe **fall color on Legacy**—gorgeous red  
	•	No fertilizer—just let them wind down  
  
**Sunflowers**  
	•	Remove spent plants  
	•	Save seeds in a paper bag (dry, dark spot)  
	•	Prep bed for winter rest (optional: sow cover crop)  
  
⸻  
  
**OCTOBER–NOVEMBER**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	Add mulch for winter insulation  
	•	Water only if fall is very dry  
	•	Reflect on which varieties did best  
  
**Sunflower Bed**  
	•	Let Boston ivy keep climbing—prune only if needed  
	•	Tuck away garden signs or clean up edges  
	•	Plan next year’s seed swaps or adjustments  
  
⸻  
  
**DECEMBER–JANUARY**  
  
**Blueberries**  
	•	Rest! No pruning yet  
	•	Watch for any rodent damage or heavy ice  
	•	Dream and plan for pruning in February
