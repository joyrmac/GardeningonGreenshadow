# Veggies  
  
Thanks for the detailed breakdown! Let’s work through your **veggie bed layout** based on everything you’ve planned, including the **different plant types, their needs**, and how you can use **companion planting** to your advantage. I’ll integrate your **potato tower**, **strawberries in hanging grow bags**, and **cucumbers in hanging baskets** as well. We’ll maximize vertical space and ensure **good plant health** with the right companions. 🌱✨  
  
⸻  
  
**1. Melon Patch (East Side of Pollinator Garden) – 10x10 Mound Beds**  
  
**Planting**:  
Melons need **space to sprawl**, but **mounding** them will allow for better **drainage** and more **vertical growth**.  
	•	**Plants**:  
	•	**Watermelon (Bush Sugar Baby, Garden Leader)**  
	•	**Cantaloupe (Sierra Gold)**  
	•	**Companions**:  
	•	**Basil**: Plant basil around melons to **boost flavor** and repel pests like aphids and mosquitoes.  
	•	**Marigolds**: Use **marigolds** for pest control, especially against **aphids** and **nematodes**.  
	•	**Additional tips**: Use **compost** to help with the **mound’s fertility**, and allow the melons to sprawl naturally. Consider **trellising smaller melon varieties** if space allows.  
  
⸻  
  
**2. The Gourd Gate (2–3x6 Beds + Cattle Panel Trellis)**  
  
This is the perfect setup for **vining crops** like **pumpkins, cucumbers**, and **squash**, taking full advantage of the **vertical space**.  
  
**Planting**:  
	•	**Pumpkins** (e.g., **Jack O’Lantern**, **Small Sugar**)  
	•	**Squash** (e.g., **Spaghetti Squash**, **Summer Melody Blend**)  
	•	**Cucumbers** (e.g., **Mexican Sour Gherkin**, **Burpless Cucumber**)  
	•	**Companions**:  
	•	**Nasturtiums**: Keep pests like **squash bugs** away and **attract pollinators** with **bright blooms**.  
	•	**Beans**: Consider **pole beans** like **Tender Pod Garden Beans**, which will climb the trellis as well.  
	•	**Additional tips**: The **cattle panel trellis** will support the **climbing plants**. Plant **squash at the base of the trellis**, and **train vines upward**. Use **mulch** to keep the base of the plants cool and to **retain moisture**.  
  
⸻  
  
**3. Rhubarb Area**  
  
Rhubarb needs **cooler temperatures**, and its **spreading habit** makes it a perfect **border** or **isolated section**.  
  
**Planting**:  
	•	**Rhubarb** (e.g., **Crimson Red**, **Victoria**)  
	•	**Companion**:  
	•	**Garlic or Chives**: These help deter **pests** and **prevent fungal infections** in the rhubarb.  
	•	**Additional tips**: Ensure you **don’t overwater** as rhubarb prefers slightly **dry conditions** once established. Place in a **shady spot** if possible.  
  
⸻  
  
**4. Two Smaller Beds (3x4 with Narrow Trellis)**  
  
These smaller beds will be great for **compact, bushy vegetables** and **climbing plants** with the narrow trellis providing vertical support.  
  
**Planting**:  
	•	**Cucumbers**: You can plant **small, bushy cucumber varieties** (like **Bush Pickle** or **Spacemaster**) and **train them to climb the trellis**.  
	•	**Tomatoes**: Consider **determinate tomato varieties** (like **Yellow Pear** or **Small Red Cherry**) that don’t need as much space and are easier to grow vertically.  
	•	**Companions**:  
	•	**Basil**: Helps with **tomato flavor** and repels pests.  
	•	**Dill**: Good for **tomatoes** and will help **attract beneficial insects** like ladybugs to control aphids.  
	•	**Additional tips**: Use **garden ties** to attach the **cucumbers and tomatoes** to the trellis, and keep **tomatoes well-staked** with bamboo to avoid over-crowding.  
  
⸻  
  
**5. Blueberry & Sunflower Garden**  
  
The **blueberries** will form a **border** along the **south edge** of your garden, while the **sunflowers** will tower in the back, providing **vertical interest** and **pollinator support**.  
  
**Planting**:  
	•	**Blueberries** (O’Neal, Patriot, Bluecrop, Legacy)  
	•	**Sunflowers** (e.g., **American Giant Hybrid**, **Mammoth**, **Busy Bee**, **Giant Teddy Bear**)  
	•	**Companion**:  
	•	**Comfrey**: This **dynamic accumulator** will help add nutrients to the soil.  
	•	**Marigolds**: Plant around **blueberries** to repel **pests** like aphids.  
	•	**Additional tips**: Make sure to **mulch heavily** around the blueberries with **pine bark** or **wood chips** to help maintain **soil acidity**. **Sunflowers** should be spaced about **24 inches apart** to prevent overcrowding.  
  
⸻  
  
**6. Raspberry Garden Around the Fire Pit**  
  
This area is perfect for **raspberries**, as they need **space to spread** and can handle partial shade.  
  
**Planting**:  
	•	**Raspberries** (e.g., **Red Latham** and **Golden Harvest**)  
	•	**Companion**:  
	•	**Horseradish**: Plant **horseradish** near **raspberries** to repel the **Japanese beetles** that often affect raspberry plants.  
	•	**Garlic**: Plant **garlic** near the raspberry canes for **disease prevention**.  
	•	**Additional tips**: Install a **trellis system** or **supporting wires** to keep the raspberry canes upright and easy to harvest.  
  
⸻  
  
**7. New Bed on the West Side**  
  
This is where you’ll place the **remaining crops**. Given that you have **space for more vining plants**, this bed can hold a mix of **climbing** and **compact vegetables**.  
  
**Planting**:  
	•	**Bush Beans** (e.g., **Landreth Stringless**, **Blue Lake Bush**)  
	•	**Edamame (Fresh Soybeans)**  
	•	**Onions** (e.g., **Yellow Sweet Spanish** and **Evergreen White Nebuka**)  
	•	**Companion**:  
	•	**Basil**: Place near **beans** and **tomatoes** for a great **herb companion**.  
	•	**Additional tips**: Use a **small oblique trellis** for beans and soybeans and **mulch around onions** to keep weeds down.  
  
⸻  
  
**8. Potato Tower, Strawberries in Hanging Grow Bags, Cucumbers in Hanging Baskets**  
	•	**Potato Tower**: Can go in **a sunny corner** where you have **vertical space**.  
	•	Plant in a **tall container or stacked box**, which will allow you to **grow multiple layers** of potatoes.  
	•	**Strawberries in Hanging Grow Bags**: Hang near the **edges** of the garden for easy access and to save ground space.  
	•	**Cucumbers in Hanging Baskets**: Hang these **near the trellis** or **wooden obliques** for vertical growing, ensuring they get plenty of sunlight.  
  
⸻  
  
**Final Summary of Where Everything Goes:**  
	1.	**Melon Patch**: 10x10 mounds on the east side of the Pollinator Garden.  
	2.	**The Gourd Gate**: 2-3x6 beds with cattle panel trellis for pumpkins, squash, cucumbers, and beans.  
	3.	**Rhubarb**: Separate shaded or partially shaded area.  
	4.	**Two Smaller Beds**: 3x4 beds with a narrow trellis for tomatoes, cucumbers, and peppers.  
	5.	**Blueberry & Sunflower Garden**: Blueberries in front with sunflowers in the back row.  
	6.	**Raspberry Garden Around the Fire Pit**: Raspberries with horseradish, garlic, and other companions.  
	7.	**New Bed on West Side**: Bush beans, edamame, onions, and other vertical-growing plants.  
	8.	**Potato Tower**: In a sunny, vertical space.  
	9.	**Hanging Strawberries & Cucumbers**: In hanging bags and baskets, using vertical space.
