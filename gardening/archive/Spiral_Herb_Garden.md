	•	Morning sun and partial shade make this section perfect for parsley, chives, and other delicate herbs.

	•	Positioned near the pond, this area will be great for cilantro, dill, and other moisture-loving plants.

**UPDATED ZONE BREAKDOWN – Native & Magical Edition**  
  
**1. Shallow Zone (North-Northeast Edge, 3–6” deep)**  
  
*Perfect for marginal plants—frog entry points, water-edge color, and pollinator attractors.*

	•	**Pickerelweed** (*Pontederia cordata*) – purple blooms, pollinator magnet

**2. Plant/Oxygenation Zone (East & South Edges, 6–12” deep)**  
  
*Great for submerged oxygenators and floating plants that help clean water and keep it cool.*  
  
**Plant Allies:**  
	•	**Hornwort** (*Ceratophyllum demersum*) – underwater forest, no root needed  
	•	**American Waterweed (Elodea)** (*Elodea canadensis*) – classic oxygenator  
	•	**Water Starwort** (*Callitriche spp.*) – soft floating rosettes, delicate and dreamy  
	•	**Duckweed** (*Lemna minor*) – tiny surface floater, frog-approved  
	•	**Blue Lobelia** (*Lobelia siphilitica*) – planted near this zone for moisture and magic  
  
**3. Deep Zone (Center to South Side, 18–24” deep)**  
  
*Cool, stable water area for frogs to shelter during hot days and dry spells. Deep enough for overwintering wildlife.*  
  
**Plant Allies:**  
	•	**Cabomba** can be skipped unless you’re set on it—go with:  
	•	**More hornwort or Elodea** in the deep center  
	•	**Floating duckweed over part of it** for shade  
	•	**Flat basking rocks or half-submerged logs** for frog perches

**HOW TO BUILD THE ALCHEMIST’S POND**  
  
**STEP 1: Choose Your Shape & Size**  
	•	**Go organic and curved**—bean, oval, crescent, or free-form.  
	•	Aim for **5–7 feet long** minimum and **2–3 feet wide**, but make it as big as you’re feelin’.  
	•	Place the **North-Northeast edge under the crepe myrtle** for the shallow zone.  
  
⸻  
  
**STEP 2: Mark & Dig Your Zones**  
  
**ZONE A: Shallow Shelf (3–6” deep) – 25% of pond**  
	•	Located on the **North-Northeast edge**.  
	•	Gentle slope for frogs to come and go.  
	•	Use large flat stones for frog basking, and small gravel at the bottom.  
  
**ZONE B: Plant Zone (6–12” deep) – 35% of pond**  
	•	Curve it around the **East and South sides**.  
	•	Dig this part a little deeper with sloped sides.  
	•	Add pockets of gravel and compost for rooted plants.  
  
**ZONE C: Deep Zone (18–24” deep) – 40% of pond**  
	•	This is the **center and Southwest side**.  
	•	Make it bowl-shaped and smooth, but don’t make it too steep.  
	•	This is the cool zone for frogs, oxygenators, and water life.  
  
⸻  
  
**STEP 3: Layer the Base**  
	•	Smooth the entire pond floor with your hands or a rake.  
	•	Lay **underlayment fabric** (optional but great for protecting the liner).  
	•	Add your **pond liner** and gently press it into curves—leave at least a foot of liner around the edges.  
  
⸻  
  
**STEP 4: Add Hardscaping**  
	•	Tuck **large rocks and stones** around the rim.  
	•	Use **gravel, logs, or slate** inside shallow zones for basking spots.  
	•	Add stepping stones around the East edge to connect to the Alchemist’s Spiral.  
  
⸻  
  
**STEP 5: Fill the Pond**  
	•	Use **rainwater or dechlorinated hose water**.  
	•	Fill slowly, watching how the levels change in each zone.  
	•	As it fills, **adjust rocks and liner edges**.  
  
⸻  
  
**STEP 6: Plant Your Zones**  
  
**SHALLOW ZONE (North/Northeast)**  
	•	Pickerelweed  
	•	Arrow Arum  
	•	Golden Club  
	•	Creeping Jenny (edge and in stone cracks)  
	•	Marsh Marigold  
	•	Soft Rush  
  
**PLANT/OXYGENATION ZONE (East/South)**  
	•	Hornwort (floating or lightly anchored)  
	•	Elodea (plant into gravel or compost)  
	•	Duckweed (sprinkle on surface)  
	•	Water Starwort  
	•	Blue Lobelia (edge)  
  
**DEEP ZONE (Center/Southwest)**  
	•	Extra hornwort and Elodea  
	•	Submerged logs or rocks for frog shelter  
	•	Float duckweed over this zone to help with algae  
  
⸻
