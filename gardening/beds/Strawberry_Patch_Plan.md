# Strawberry Patch Planning Guide 2026

> Multi-variety strawberry patch for continuous spring-through-frost harvests. Three-variety system: day-neutral (year-round), everbearing (spring/summer/fall), and June-bearing (traditional peak harvest).

---

## Variety Selection

### Recommended Mix (12–20 plants total)

| Variety | Type | Harvest Season | Yield | NC Notes |
|---------|------|-----------------|-------|----------|
| **Albion** | Day-neutral | April–November | High, consistent | **PRIMARY CHOICE** — Best for NC. Produces all season. Sweet, excellent flavor. |
| **San Andreas** | Day-neutral | April–November | High | Backup day-neutral. Similar to Albion, slightly firmer. Both planted = continuous harvest. |
| **Seascape** | Everbearing | May, July–Oct | Medium | Classic everbearing. Reliable, good flavor. Fills gaps between day-neutral peaks. |
| **Chandler** | June-bearing | May–June | Very high (one flush) | **Peak season specialist.** Gets massive in June. Traditional, romantic, photo-worthy. One heavy harvest. |

### Plant Quantities
- **Albion:** 6–8 plants (primary producer)
- **San Andreas:** 4–6 plants (extend season reliability)
- **Seascape:** 3–4 plants (fill spring/summer gap)
- **Chandler:** 2–3 plants (June spectacle, not year-round)

**Total: 15–21 plants** = consistent harvest from April through first frost.

## Location & Bed Setup

### Location Options

**Option A: Raised Bed (Recommended)**
- **Size:** 4' x 8' x 8" tall (standard garden bed)
- **Position:** Full sun (8+ hours), well-draining, close to water source
- **Good near:** Herb spiral, Blueberry & Raspberry Bed (companion area)
- **Avoid:** Heavy shade, soggy ground, near walnut trees (toxic to berries)

**Option B: In-Ground (If Space Limited)**
- Create 2–3 rows, 12–18" wide, 2–3 ft apart
- Amend native soil heavily with compost

## Timeline & Implementation

### SPRING OPTION (Plant Now — April)

**Pros:** Get some berries this year (from Chandler + light harvest from day-neutral by June)

**Cons:** Plants stressed by summer heat, need heavy watering, won't be full strength until year 2

**If doing spring:**

| Date | Task |
|------|------|
| **March 25–April 5** | Order transplants (6-pack plugs from specialty nurseries: Stark Bros, Nourse Farms, local growers) |
| **By April 10** | Prep bed site (clear weeds, level ground) |
| **April 15–20** | Assemble raised bed + soil mix |
| **April 22–25** | Plant 15–21 transplants at 12–18" spacing |
| **April–May** | Water deeply 2x/week, mulch with 2" straw |
| **June** | First light harvest (Chandler will be impressive). Continue removing runners except 1–2 per plant. |
| **July–Sept** | Deadhead spent flowers. Manage runners. Water during heat waves (1.5" per week). |
| **Oct–Nov** | Last harvest. Begin runner plantlets collection for propagation. |
| **November** | Mulch heavily (4–6") for winter. Plan fall refresh. |

---

### FALL OPTION (Plant August–September) — **RECOMMENDED FOR NC**

**Pros:** Plants establish strong roots over winter, massive harvest next spring, less summer heat stress, longer lifespan

**Cons:** Wait 6 months for first real harvest (but better quality)

**If doing fall:**

| Date | Task |
|------|------|
| **May–July** | Prep bed site, assemble raised bed, gather materials |
| **July** | Order transplants for August delivery |
| **August 1–15** | Plant 15–21 transplants, mulch, set up irrigation |
| **Aug–Sept** | Light watering, let plants establish roots |
| **Oct–Nov** | Minimal care. Plants going dormant (normal). |
| **Dec–Feb** | Dormant. Protect with 4–6" mulch (prevents freeze-thaw heaving). |
| **March 2027** | Remove mulch as soil warms. Runners begin sprouting. |
| **May 2027** | **PEAK HARVEST SEASON.** Chandler at full production. |
| **June–Nov 2027** | Day-neutral + everbearing keep producing all season. |

## Planting Instructions

### Spacing & Layout

```
4' x 8' Raised Bed - Three-Row System

Row 1 (North):    [A]  [A]  [A]  [A]  (Albion — day-neutral)
                  12-18" apart

Row 2 (Middle):   [SA] [SA] [SA] [SE] (San Andreas + Seascape)
                  12-18" apart

Row 3 (South):    [C]  [C]  [C]  [SA] (Chandler + San Andreas)
                  12-18" apart

[A] = Albion (6–8)
[SA] = San Andreas (4–6)
[SE] = Seascape (3–4)
[C] = Chandler (2–3)
```

### Planting Steps

1. **Prep bed:** Fill with soil mix (40/40/20), water gently to settle
2. **Mark rows:** Use string or chalk to outline 3 rows
3. **Dig holes:** 12–18" apart, holes just large enough for roots
4. **Plant:** Set crown (where leaves emerge) *exactly at soil level* — not buried, not exposed
5. **Firm soil:** Press gently around crown
6. **Water deeply:** Saturate after planting
7. **Mulch:** 2" straw around plants (not touching crown)
8. **Label:** Mark each variety with stakes

**Critical:** Crown depth is everything. Too deep = rot. Too shallow = roots dry out. Exactly at soil level = healthy.

## Year-by-Year Care

### Year 1 (Establishment Year)

#### Spring (Planting → June)
- **Water:** 1" per week if no rain (drip irrigation ideal)
- **Flowers:**
  - **Chandler:** Let bloom, get June harvest
  - **Day-neutral/Everbearing (if spring planted):** Pinch flowers until June, then let them bloom
- **Runners:** Remove all except 1–2 strongest per plant (they drain energy)
- **Weeds:** Weekly pull. No competition allowed.
- **Mulch:** Maintain 2" layer. Top-up monthly.

#### Summer (July–August)
- **Water:** Critical. 1.5" per week during heat. Berries crack if inconsistent.
- **Deadhead:** Remove spent flowers immediately (encourages new blooms)
- **Runner management:** Let 1–2 strongest runners root nearby. Pin down with landscape staple if desired, or collect plantlets in pots.
- **Fertilize:** Monthly with balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) or strawberry formula
- **Pest watch:** Check for slugs (eggshells help!), spider mites (hose off), bird damage (netting if needed)

#### Fall (September–October)
- **Water:** Reduce slightly as temps drop
- **Harvest:** Day-neutral & everbearing still producing until first frost
- **Runner management:** Finish cleanup, collect best plantlets for propagation (optional)
- **Cleanup:** Remove dead leaves, tidy plants

#### Winter (November–February)
- **Mulch heavily:** 4–6" of straw after ground freezes (prevents heave = keeps roots from popping out)
- **Dormancy:** Plants resting. This is GOOD. They need cold period.
- **Spring prep:** Plan runner management, research next-year layout

### Year 2 (Production Year)

- **Spring:** Remove mulch as soil warms (March)
- **Runners sprout:** Let them establish. Thin to 1–2 per plant. These become your year 3 replacement plants.
- **Flowers bloom:** Let all bloom. Heavy harvest May–June.
- **Summer–Fall:** Day-neutral/everbearing produce continuously. Deadhead, water, manage runners.
- **Fall:** Propagate runners in pots for refreshing bed in year 3

### Year 3+ (Maintenance)

- Replace oldest plants with rooted plantlets (year 2–3 is typically peak production, year 4–5 declining)
- Keep rotating in fresh plants from runner propagation
- Or, refresh entire bed every 3 years for maximum yield

## Pest & Problem Management

### Common Issues & Fixes

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Slugs eating berries** | Damp mulch, nearby shelter | Crushed eggshell border (you have plenty!). Nightly hand-pick. Beer traps. |
| **Berries cracking** | Inconsistent watering | Drip irrigation on timer. Water deeply 1.5"/week. Mulch to retain moisture. |
| **No flowers** | Summer heat stress | Afternoon shade cloth (30%). Consistent water. Avoid planting too deep. |
| **Powdery mildew on leaves** | Poor air circulation | Thin runners, remove dead leaves, don't overhead water |
| **Root rot** | Waterlogging | Ensure drainage. Don't plant too deep. Raised bed helps. |
| **Spider mites** | Hot, dry air | Hose plants with water mid-morning. Neem oil if severe. |
| **Birds eating ripe berries** | Feast-time predators | Bird netting over bed during peak harvest (May–June) |
| **Few runners producing** | Overcrowding | Thin to 1–2 per plant. Remove competition. |

## Integration with Existing Garden

### Suggested Location
- **Near:** Blueberry & Raspberry Bed (companion berry area)
- **South-facing:** Full sun, easy access from main pathways
- **Close to water:** For irrigation ease
- **Avoid:** Shade from trees, wet spots, near walnut trees

### Companion Planting
- **Borage:** Attracts pollinators (beneficial for strawberry pollination)
- **Thyme:** Repels worms, looks pretty as living mulch between plants
- **Calendula:** Attracts beneficial insects
- **Chamomile:** Nearby, attracts pollinators
- **Keep away:** Brassicas (different soil pH preference), fennel (allelopathic)

### Eggshell Use
Crush eggshells and work into bed mix before planting (calcium for healthy fruit, slug deterrent). Continue sprinkling crushed eggshells monthly around plants through growing season.
