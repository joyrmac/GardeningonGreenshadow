## Planting Order

1. **Structural plants first:** Edgeworthia, Hydrangea, Butterfly Bush, Lilac
2. **Perennials:** Coneflowers, Joe Pye Weed, Goldenrod, Yarrow, Milkweed
3. **River pathway:** Lay out before filling in surrounding squares
4. **Annuals and seeds:** Cosmos, Zinnias, Snapdragons, Bachelor Buttons, Nasturtiums
5. **Edge plants last:** Chamomile, Johnny Jump Ups, Creeping Phlox, Calendula

## Cautions

- **Milkweed needs cold stratification.** Refrigerate seeds in a damp paper towel for 30 days before planting, or direct sow in fall for spring germination
- **Butterfly Bush is invasive in some states.** Check local regulations. Deadhead spent blooms to prevent self-seeding
- **Edgeworthia is cold-hardy to Zone 7.** If gardening in Zone 5b (IL), this plant may not survive winter without heavy protection -- consider replacing with a Witch Hazel
- **Broadleaf Hydrangea blooms on OLD wood.** Never prune in spring or you'll lose all flowers. Only prune immediately after blooming (late summer)
- **The "river" pathway needs a weed barrier underneath.** Use landscape fabric under the gravel/stones, or you'll be weeding between rocks forever
- **Joe Pye Weed gets 5-7 ft tall.** Plant it where it won't shade out shorter plants. Stake in exposed/windy spots

---

## Quick Fixes

- **Hydrangea flowers turning green?** Normal at end of season. Not a problem
- **Butterfly Bush not blooming?** Needs full sun (6+ hours). If it's shaded by the hydrangea, cut hydrangea back
- **Coneflower leaves turning black at base?** Likely bacterial leaf spot from overhead watering. Water at soil level only. Remove affected leaves
- **Nasturtiums all leaves, no flowers?** Soil is too rich. They actually bloom better in poor soil. Stop fertilizing them
