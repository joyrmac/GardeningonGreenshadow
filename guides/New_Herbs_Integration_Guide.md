# New Herbs Integration Guide

> Quick reference for adding Astragalus, Fenugreek, and Nettle to your garden system

---

## Quick Answer: Where Does Each Herb Go?

### **ASTRAGALUS** → Potion Patch (Back/Middle Row)
- **Size:** 24-30" tall by year 2-3
- **When to plant:** Late March (as starts) or Feb (seed indoors)
- **Where exactly:** Potion Patch middle ring (between tall plants like Valerian + short plants like Calendula), or back section (behind Echinacea)
- **Spacing:** 12-18" apart from other plants
- **First harvest:** Year 3 (roots), but flowers feed pollinators year 1+
- **Your tea:** Steep dried root, mild slightly sweet flavor, building immunity over time

### **FENUGREEK** → Potion Patch (Front/Edge Zone)
- **Size:** 12-18" tall, bushy
- **When to plant:** Early April (direct sow, don't transplant)
- **Where exactly:** Front edges of Potion Patch (between Calendula + Chamomile), outer ring
- **Spacing:** 6-8" apart
- **First harvest:** July-August (seeds ready), can eat leaves fresh all summer
- **Your tea:** This IS your constant-brew. Seeds taste like maple + spice, nourishing daily drink
- **Self-seeding:** YES — let 10-20% mature, expect volunteers next spring

### **NETTLE** → Dedicated Pot (Near Kitchen)
- **Size:** Untamed spreader (why it MUST be potted)
- **When to plant:** Spring-summer (buy or start from seed)
- **Where exactly:** 5-gallon pot, placed near kitchen door, herb spiral area, or accessible path
- **Spacing:** In pot only (not ground)
- **First harvest:** May onward (pinch top 6")
- **Your tea:** Fresh or dried, unlimited supply, weekly harvest sustains you all year
- **Never in ground:** Serious spreader — pot only, always. Even in ground, it takes over
- **Optional year 2+:** Divide the root system, start second pot if you want more

## Detailed Integration: Each Herb in Context

### **ASTRAGALUS: The Perennial Investment**

**What you're getting:**
- A slow-building immune support partner
- Delicate flowers that attract pollinators
- A 10+ year plant that deepens every year
- Roots you can harvest year 3 onward

**Timeline:**
```
YEAR 1: Plant in spring, let establish, flowers attract bees
YEAR 2: Grows larger, more flowers, still no root harvest
YEAR 3+: Ready for gentle root harvesting (take some, leave some to expand)
```

**In the Potion Patch (recommended):**
```
CENTER RING (tall: Valerian, Mullein, Echinacea, Angelica)
  ↓ (12-18" spacing)
MIDDLE RING (medium: ASTRAGALUS ← here, Yarrow, Hyssop, St. John's Wort)
  ↓ (12-18" spacing)
FRONT ROW (short: Calendula, Chamomile, Borage, Fenugreek)
```

**In Potion Patch (alternative):**
```
BACK (behind Valerian)
  Mullein | Yarrow | Angelica | ASTRAGALUS ← here
```

**Growing notes:**
- Full sun, good drainage
- Minimal water after year 1
- Basically indestructible once established
- Flowers appear spring → summer, delicate lavender-ish
- Let it stay in place 10+ years (deep taproot)

**Harvest:**
- Year 3+: In fall, dig gently, harvest some roots
- Leave roots behind to expand → bigger harvest year 4
- Dry roots thoroughly before storage
- Make tea: simmer 1 tbsp dried root 10-15 mins, sip daily for immune support

**Ecological role:**
- Pollinator magnet (spring flowers)
- Nitrogen-fixing (feeds soil)
- Deep taproot breaks compaction
- Long-lived perennial foundation plant

**Witchy correspondence:**
- Protection, resilience, ancestral grounding
- "Ask your roots to go deeper"
- Long-term planning, slow intentional work

### **FENUGREEK: The Quick Abundance Grower**

**What you're getting:**
- Fast, reliable seed crop by late summer
- Tea supply literally from your garden (just dry + brew)
- Self-seeding backup (easier each year)
- Nitrogen for surrounding soil
- Easy to manage, annual, no takeover risk

**Timeline:**
```
EARLY APRIL: Direct sow seeds (don't transplant)
MAY-JUNE: Rapid growth, thin to 6-8" apart
JULY: Start harvesting fresh leaves for tea
LATE AUGUST: Seed pods mature, harvest, dry, thresh
YEAR 2: Self-sown volunteers appear (thin if too crowded)
```

**In Potion Patch (recommended front zone):**
```
FRONT/EDGES (short: Calendula, Chamomile, Borage, FENUGREEK ← here)
  Spacing: 6-8" apart in clusters
  Position: Edges around perimeter
```

**In the Potion Patch outer ring (alternative):**
```
OUTER RING (short plants): scattered among Calendula + Chamomile
  Same spacing, same idea
```

**Growing notes:**
- Direct sow (don't start indoors, roots hate transplant)
- Sun + moderate water
- Fast grower (4-6 weeks to bushy plant)
- Delicate white/pale yellow flowers appear May-June (bees love)
- Seed pods form after flowers (greenish, elongated)

**Harvest timing:**
- **Fresh leaves:** Anytime May-August (pick top leaves, plant keeps producing)
- **Seeds:** Wait until pods brown + dry on plant (late Aug), pick pods, thresh seeds indoors, dry 1-2 weeks
- **Yield:** One plant makes ~1 tbsp dried seeds (enough for multiple tea brews)

**Tea recipe:**
- 1 tsp Fenugreek seeds per cup hot water
- Steep 5-10 mins
- Tastes like maple + spice, warming, nourishing
- Your constant-brew, the one you'll make weekly

**Self-seeding:**
- Let 10-20% of plants fully mature + drop seeds
- Next year, volunteers appear (just thin if crowded)
- Year 2+: Basically plants itself, you just thin

**Ecological role:**
- Nitrogen-fixing legume (feeds surrounding soil)
- Pollinator flowers (May-June)
- Self-seeding represents abundance/multiplication
- Annual, no takeover risk

**Witchy correspondence:**
- Abundance, manifestation, golden energy
- Nourishment, grounding, self-sufficiency
- "Plant this and watch it multiply"
- Tea for manifestation work

### **NETTLE: The Pot-Bound Powerhouse**

**What you're getting:**
- Unlimited nutritional tea supply (fresh or dried)
- Mineral-rich greens (iron, calcium, silica — nourishing daily)
- Butterfly larvae host (Red Admiral, Mourning Cloak, Comma butterflies)
- Deep mineral miner (feeds surrounding soil when mulched around pot)
- Basically indestructible once potted
- Zero fertilizer needed

**Critical rule:**
```
🚨 NEVER IN GROUND 🚨
Nettle spreads aggressively if planted in open earth.
POT ONLY. Always. No exceptions.
```

**Timeline:**
```
SPRING: Buy start or grow from seed → pot in 5-gal container
MAY ONWARD: Begin harvesting weekly (pinch top 6")
YEAR 1: Establishing, getting full + bushy
YEAR 2: Root-bound, consider dividing or second pot
YEAR 3+: Mature system, unlimited harvests
```

**Pot specifics:**
- **Size:** 5-gallon minimum (12-14" diameter)
- **Soil:** Good potting soil + compost mix
- **Location:** Near kitchen (for easy access), herb spiral area, accessible path
- **Sun:** Full sun or afternoon shade OK
- **Water:** Keep moist (not waterlogged) — nettles love moisture
- **Depth:** Deep pot OK, encourages root development

**Harvest:**
- **Fresh:** Pinch top 6" (includes tender leaves) — weekly, year-round
- **Dried:** Harvest in summer when potent, hang-dry, store in jars
- **Amount:** One pot produces enough for daily tea indefinitely

**Tea recipe:**
- Fresh: Handful of leaves, steep in hot water 5-10 mins
- Dried: 1 tbsp dried leaves, steep 5-10 mins
- Tastes green + mineral, nourishing, non-addictive
- Drink freely (not medicinal strong, nutritive — food as medicine)

**Container management:**
- Water when soil surface is dry (weekly, more in heat)
- Minimal fertilizer (nettle is nutrient miner, pulls from soil)
- Drain hole essential (root rot prevention)
- Repot every 2-3 years if pot-bound + want expansion

**Dividing (optional year 2+):**
- Nettle spreads via root runners underground
- If pot is very full, carefully pull apart root sections
- Repot sections into new 5-gal pots
- Give one to friend, expand your nettle empire
- Or just keep the main pot, it's plenty

**Placement ideas:**
- [ ] Near kitchen door (grab fresh leaves while cooking)
- [ ] Herb spiral area (integrated with cooking herbs)
- [ ] Outdoor path edge (accessible, visible)
- [ ] Potting bench area (organized)
- [ ] Any spot with sun + water access

**Ecological role:**
- Host plant for butterfly larvae (Red Admiral, Mourning Cloak, Comma butterflies need nettle)
- Deep roots mine minerals from soil (feed surrounding beds when liquid-fertilized around pot)
- Supports beneficial insects
- Encourages pollinators to your garden area

**Witchy correspondence:**
- Protection, fierce softness, wild power
- Women's medicine, grounding
- "Nettle teaches us to be fierce + soft"
- Resilience, boundaries, deep roots
- Protection spell herb

## FAQ: New Herbs Edition

**Q: Can I grow Nettle in ground instead of pot?**
A: No. Nettle spreads aggressively underground via runners. In 2 years it takes over entire beds. Pot only, always.

**Q: Fenugreek smells weird — is that normal?**
A: Yes, Fenugreek has a distinctive maple-curry smell. It's part of the charm. Tea tastes better than it smells.

**Q: When can I harvest Astragalus root?**
A: Year 3+. Before that, let it establish deep roots. You harvest in fall, dig gently, take some roots, replant what's left to expand.

**Q: Will Astragalus really take 3 years?**
A: Yes. It's a slow builder. But roots from year 3 onward are what you wanted — strong, established, potent.

**Q: Can I start Nettle from seed instead of buying a start?**
A: Yes, but it's slow. Buy a start (often sold as "edible nettle" or even "weed"), pot it, begin harvesting in 1-2 months instead of 6+ months.

**Q: What if my Fenugreek doesn't self-seed well?**
A: Just resow next spring. It's not finicky. But if you let even a few plants mature + drop seeds, you'll have volunteers.

**Q: Nettle flowers — do they matter?**
A: No, nettle flowers are tiny + green. You harvest leaves before flowering (pinch top 6"), so flowers rarely appear on harvested plants. Not a priority.

**Q: Can these three grow near each other?**
A: Yes! Astragalus + Fenugreek grow well together in the Potion Patch. Nettle pot can be anywhere. All three are companions in witchy herbology.
