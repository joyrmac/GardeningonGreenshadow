## Smudge Bundle Recipes

### How to Make a Smudge Stick
1. Harvest stems 8-12 inches long in the **morning after dew dries**
2. Bundle 5-10 stems together, about thumb-thickness
3. Wrap tightly with **cotton twine** (not synthetic — it melts) starting at the base, spiral up, then back down
4. Hang upside down in a **dry, dark, warm spot** for 2-3 weeks
5. Should feel papery-dry and crackle slightly when ready
6. Light the tip, blow out the flame, let it smolder

### Recipe 1: "Garden Sage Cleanse" (closest to traditional smudge)
**Best for:** Cleansing a room, moving into a new space, after arguments
- 5-6 stems **Garden Sage**
- 2-3 stems **Rosemary**
- 1-2 stems **Thyme**
- Wrap with cotton twine

### Recipe 2: "Lavender Calm" (evening / sleep / anxiety)
**Best for:** Before bed, after a hard day, calming a space
- 4-5 stems **Lavender** (any variety — Spike has strongest scent)
- 2-3 stems **Chamomile** (tuck flower heads in)
- 1-2 stems **Lemon Balm**
- Wrap with cotton twine

### Recipe 3: "Bee Balm Spirit" (warm, spicy, ceremonial)
**Best for:** Meditation, ritual, honoring ancestors, full moon
- 4-5 stems **Bee Balm** (Wild Scarlet)
- 2-3 stems **Garden Sage**
- 1-2 stems **Hyssop**
- A few **Yarrow** flower heads tucked in
- Wrap with cotton twine

### Recipe 4: "Wake Up" (morning energy / mental clarity)
**Best for:** Starting the day, creative work, clearing brain fog
- 3-4 stems **Rosemary**
- 2-3 stems **Peppermint or Spearmint**
- 1-2 stems **Basil**
- 1 stem **Thyme**
- Wrap with cotton twine

### Recipe 5: "Joy Bundle" (happiness, lifting spirits)
**Best for:** Celebrations, gratitude practice, welcoming guests
- 3-4 stems **Lavender** (Munstead or Hidcote)
- 2-3 stems **Lemon Balm**
- 2-3 **Borage** flower stalks
- 1-2 stems **Catnip**
- Wrap with cotton twine

### Recipe 6: "Woodland Resin" (cedar/juniper substitute)
**Best for:** Deep cleansing, grounding, when you want that woodsy-piney scent
- 4-5 small **Cryptomeria/Blue Spruce** branch tips (dried)
- 2-3 stems **Rosemary**
- 1-2 stems **Thyme**
- Wrap with cotton twine — burn outdoors, it'll smoke more

## When to Harvest for Burning

Herbs are most potent (highest essential oil content) when harvested at the right time:

| Herb | Best Harvest Time | What to Cut | Peak Month |
|------|-------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Lavender** | Just before flowers fully open | Cut entire flower stalk + 2-3" stem | June–July |
| **Rosemary** | Anytime once established; strongest in summer | 6-8" stem tips | June–Aug |
| **Garden Sage** | Before it flowers | Stems with leaves | May–June |
| **Bee Balm** | When flowers are at peak color | Stems with flowers + leaves | July–Aug |
| **Chamomile** | When flowers are fully open, petals flat | Flower heads only (pinch off) | June–Aug |
| **Thyme** | Just before flowering | Woody stem tips | June–July |
| **Hyssop** | When flowering | Entire flowering stems | July–Aug |
| **Lemon Balm** | Before flowering (most lemony) | Leaf clusters, top 6" | May–June, again in Aug |
| **Mints** | Just before flowering | Top 6-8" of stems | June–July |
| **Basil** | Before flowering; pinch flowers to keep harvesting | Top leaf clusters | July–Sept |
| **Anise** | When seeds turn brown | Cut seed heads into paper bag | Sept–Oct |
| **Yarrow** | Full bloom | Flower heads + top stems | June–Aug |
| **Borage** | When flowers are open | Flower heads + top leaves | June–Sept |
| **Calendula** | Full bloom | Flower heads | May–frost |
| **Marigold** | Full bloom, slightly past peak | Flower heads | July–frost |
| **Goldenrod** | Full bloom | Flower stalks | Sept–Oct |
| **Cryptomeria** | During regular pruning | Branch tips, 6-8" | Anytime |

## Drying Methods

### Method 1: Hanging Bundles (best for stems — sage, rosemary, lavender, bee balm)
- Bundle 5-8 stems with rubber band
- Hang upside down in warm, dark, dry spot (garage, closet, covered porch)
- 1-3 weeks depending on humidity
- Done when stems snap, don't bend

### Method 2: Screen/Rack Drying (best for flowers and loose leaves)
- Spread single layer on window screen or cooling rack
- Place in warm, dry area with airflow
- Turn every few days
- 1-2 weeks
- Best for: chamomile, calendula, marigold, borage, loose mint leaves

### Method 3: Paper Bag Method (best for seeds)
- Cut seed heads into paper bag
- Hang bag in warm spot or set on shelf
- Seeds fall to bottom as they dry
- Best for: anise, poppy, dill (for fragrance, not food)

### Method 4: Oven (emergency/quick dry — sacrifices some fragrance)
- Spread on baking sheet
- 170°F with door cracked
- Check every 30 min
- 2-4 hours
- Use only if you need something dried TODAY

## Storage

- Store dried herbs in **mason jars** or **paper bags** (not plastic — traps moisture)
- Label with herb name + date
- Keep in cool, dark, dry place
- **Shelf life for burning:** 1-2 years (fragrance weakens over time)
- **Best by:** Use within 6 months for strongest scent

## Safety Notes

- Always burn in a **well-ventilated area** or outdoors
- Use a **heat-safe dish** — ceramic bowl with sand, abalone shell, cast iron
- Never leave smoldering herbs unattended
- Keep water nearby to fully extinguish
- Charcoal discs get extremely hot — use tongs, not fingers
- If you have asthma or respiratory issues, burn in open air only
- Cats are sensitive to smoke — keep them out of the room while burning (ironic given the catnip)
