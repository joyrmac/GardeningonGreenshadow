# April 2026 Garden Guide
## Strawberry Planting, Tender Crops, Bloomveil Annuals

> **Theme:** All-in for spring planting! Strawberries go in. Tender crops transplant.
> **Weather:** Frost danger April 1-6. SAFE planting after April 15 (soil 60F+).
> **Milestones:** Last frost April 6. Safe plant date April 15.

---

## Overview: What's Happening

**The Big Picture:**
- **🍓 STRAWBERRY PLANTING (Apr 15-20)** — Bed prep + 15-21 plants in ground
- **Tender crop planting** (after Apr 15) — Tomatoes, peppers, annuals when frost risk gone
- **Bloomveil annual planting** — All the zinnias, cosmos, dahlias after Apr 15
- **Gourd Gate construction** (if not done in March)
- **Wildflower broadcasting** — Native perennials in Wayward Meadow
- **Direct sowing continues** — Sunflowers, corn, late crops

**Daily commitment:** 5-30 min weekdays, half-days weekends. Heavy work mid-month (strawberry bed).

## Week-by-Week Tasks

### Week 1: April 1-12 — Strawberry Prep + Early Planting

**Monday-Friday (Daily 5 min):**
- [ ] Check all surviving indoor trays (melons, pumpkins, etc.)
- [ ] Monitor weather (last frost is April 6 — don't transplant before!)
- [ ] Check germinated seedlings (carrots, peas, etc. from Mar 9)

**Early April (April 5-10) — Strawberry Bed Preparation**

- [ ] **🍓 Receive transplants** (should arrive by now if ordered Mar 31)
- [ ] **🍓 Confirm bed location** (recheck: full sun, well-draining, near water)
- [ ] **🍓 Buy soil amendments**
  - [ ] Compost (3-4 cu ft bulk)
  - [ ] Peat moss (2-3 cu ft)
  - [ ] Perlite (1 cu ft bag)
  - [ ] Straw mulch (1-2 bales)
  - [ ] Drip irrigation kit (optional)

- [ ] **🍓 Prep soil** (2-3 hours)
  - Clear site: remove grass, weeds, rocks
  - Spread compost + peat + perlite on top (6-8" total layer)
  - Till or dig in to 8-10" depth
  - Break up clumps, remove rocks
  - Rake smooth, level
  - Water gently to settle

**Saturday, April 12 (HALF DAY) — Last-Minute Prep**

- [ ] Harden off any remaining indoor starts (last chance before frost pass!)
- [ ] Finish Wildwood bed layouts (if not done in March)
- [ ] Check Bloomveil bed prep (ready for annuals after Apr 15?)
- [ ] Mark strawberry planting zones with string

---

### Week 2: April 13-20 — STRAWBERRY PLANTING + Frost Danger Passes

**Monday-Friday (April 13-17 — 5-10 min):**
- [ ] Last frost date is April 6! But wait until April 15 to be safe
- [ ] Monitor temps (can go below 40F some nights — don't plant yet)
- [ ] Water strawberry bed (keep soil moist before planting)

**🍓 STRAWBERRY: PLANTING DAY (April 15-20 — 2-3 hours)**

*Choose a day when soil is moist but not soggy, temps 50F+*

- [ ] Mark 3 rows with string (spacing: 2-3 ft apart)
- [ ] Dig holes 12-18" apart in each row
- [ ] **Plant 15-21 strawberry transplants**
  - [ ] Albion (6-8 plants)
  - [ ] Chandler (2-3 plants)
  - [ ] San Andreas (4-6 plants)
  - [ ] Seascape (3-4 plants)
- [ ] **Critical:** Set crown exactly at soil level!
  - Not buried (will rot)
  - Not exposed (roots dry out)
  - Exactly at surface!
- [ ] Firm soil gently around each crown
- [ ] **Water immediately after each plant** (don't wait until end)
- [ ] Mulch with 2-3" straw (not touching crown)
- [ ] Set up drip irrigation or hand-watering plan

**Weekend (April 19-20) — Tender Crop Planting Begins**

*Now frost danger is officially past (April 6 was average last frost)*

- [ ] **Tomato transplants** (if hardened off and soil is 60F+)
  - Stake or cage at planting time (don't wait!)
  - Space 24-36" apart
  - Water deeply

- [ ] **Pepper transplants** (if ready)
  - Pinch first flower clusters (focus on roots, not fruit year 1)
  - Space 18-24" apart

- [ ] **Eggplant transplants** (if ready)

- [ ] **Cantaloupe, watermelon, cucumber** transplants (if soil 60F+)
  - Or direct sow if preferred

- [ ] **Start Bloomveil annual planting** (see detail section below)
  - Zinnias, cosmos, snapdragons, dahlias, coneflowers, etc.
  - Plant by color zone
  - Stake/support as needed

---

### Week 3: April 21-30 — Continued Planting + Gourd Gate

**Monday-Friday (April 21-25):**
- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Daily watering** (1" per week total)
- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Watch for wilting** (first week is critical)
- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Weed pass** (remove competitors)
- [ ] Check all transplants (tomatoes, peppers, annuals)
- [ ] Water everything deeply (no rain = manual watering)

**Saturday, April 26 (HALF DAY) — Finish Planting + Infrastructure**

- [ ] **Finish Bloomveil annual planting** (if not done Apr 19-20)
  - All 5 zones should have plants by now
  - Mark zones if not done

- [ ] **Gourd Gate construction** (if not done in March)
  - Build cattle panel trellises
  - Assemble raised beds
  - Prep for May planting

- [ ] **Wildflower/native perennial broadcasting**
  - Wayward Meadow: American Meadows 30-plant mix
  - Spread seed, rake lightly, water

- [ ] **Sunflower direct sowing** (late April window)
  - 2" deep, 12" apart
  - Choose varieties (tall, dwarf, colors)

- [ ] **Corn circle direct sowing** (if buckwheat turned under)

**Sunday, April 27-30 — Ongoing Care**

- [ ] **🍓 STRAWBERRY: Check daily**
  - Water if dry
  - Remove any wilting leaves
  - Watch for slugs, pests
  - Should be settling in nicely by now

- [ ] **Bloomveil deadheading** (some early bloomers)
  - Remove spent flowers
  - Keep plants focused

- [ ] **Garden walk-through**
  - Note what's thriving, what needs attention
  - Plan May priorities

## Detailed Tasks

### Bloomveil Annual Planting (Mid-Late April — 2-3 hours)

**By Zone (See Bloomveil.md for full plant lists):**

**Zone 1: Sunrise Cloud** (Peach, coral, soft gold, warm pink)
- Cosmos 'Apricotta' (3 plants)
- Zinnia 'Oklahoma Pink' (3 plants)
- Zinnia 'Candy Cane Stripe' (2 plants)
- Lizzy Dwarf Gladiolus (3 plants)
- Chamomile (Roman) (4 plants, border)
- Lemon Balm potted (1)
- Calendula 'Pink Surprise' (2)

**Zone 2: Berry Patch** (Raspberry, fuchsia, deep pink, magenta)
- Zinnia 'Benary's Giant Coral' (2 tall, back)
- Snapdragon 'Appleblossom' (3 tall, back)
- Zinnia 'Macarenia' (3 mid, middle)
- Cosmos 'Candy Stripe' (2 mid-tall)
- Calendula 'Pink Surprise' (3 front)
- Parsley potted (1 corner)

**Zone 3: Spice Drift** (Orange, red, deep sunset, amber)
- Zinnia 'Redman Super Cactus' (3 tall, back)
- Cosmos 'Mandarin Orange' (3 mid)
- Snapdragons deep tones (3 tall, back)
- Calendula 'Sunset Mix' (3 front, wavy drift)
- Marigold 'Amber Glow' (3 front edge)
- Basil or Sage (2 corners)

**Zone 4: Moonlit Meadow** (White, cream, silver, soft blush)
- Cosmos white (3 tall, back)
- Chamomile (Roman) (4 front border)
- Zinnia white (3 mid)
- Borage (2 mid)
- Snapdragons white/blush (3 tall, back)
- Yarrow (white) (2)
- Lavender (2)

**Zone 5: Twilight Hollow** (Purple, deep blue, lilac, violet)
- Joe Pye Weed (2 tall, back)
- Coneflower 'Pink Double Delight' (3 mid-tall)
- Poppy varieties (3 mid)
- Cosmos 'Sea Shells' (2 tall, back)
- Snapdragon lavender/purple (3 tall, back)
- Tweedia 'Heavenly Blue' (1 vining, trellis)
- Lavender 'Hidcote Blue' (2 front)
- Hyssop (2 tall)

**Planting Steps:**
1. Mark zone positions with stakes
2. Dig holes sized for root balls
3. Plant back to front (tall first, short last)
4. Firm soil gently
5. Water deeply immediately
6. Mulch 2-3" around plants (1" from stems)
7. Stake tall flowers if needed
8. Place crystals for each zone (see Witchy Garden Styling)

**Spacing Guide:**
- Tall flowers (back): 12-18" apart
- Mid-height: 12" apart
- Short/edging: 6-9" apart
