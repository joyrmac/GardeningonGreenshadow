## Detailed Tasks

### Strawberry Care (All Month)

**Watering (Critical!)**
- Target: 1-1.5" per week
- In heat waves: 1.5-2" per week
- Method: Drip irrigation best, hand-water at soil level (avoid wetting leaves)
- Time: Early morning (6-8 am) to prevent mildew

**Weeding (2x weekly)**
- Pull anything competing: grass, weeds, runner volunteers not planted
- Get roots out completely
- Don't let weeds compete for water/nutrients

**Runner Management**
- By May: runners appear from base of plants
- Keep 1-2 strongest runners per plant
- Remove extras (they drain energy)
- If propagating for next year: let 5-6 rooted plantlets establish in pots

**Pest Management**
- **Slugs:** Eggshell rings help. Hand-pick at night. Beer traps. Remove dense mulch if severe.
- **Spider mites:** Hose plants with water mid-morning (kills them). Neem oil if severe.
- **Rot:** Remove any rotting berries immediately (don't let disease spread)
- **Ants:** Usually not a problem, but remove if nesting in bed

**Fertilizing (When flowers appear)**
- Once flowers visible: fertilize weekly
- Use 10-10-10 balanced or strawberry-specific formula
- Follow package directions (usually dilute spray or granular scatter)

### Bloomveil Maintenance (All Month)

**Watering**
- 1-1.5" per week minimum
- In heat spikes (80F+): 1.5-2" per week
- Deep watering 1-2x weekly beats frequent shallow watering
- Morning is best (prevents mildew)

**Deadheading (Critical!)**
- Remove spent blooms immediately
- This forces plant to make more flowers (continuous bloom)
- Leave a few flowers in late season for seeds if desired

**Staking/Support**
- Tall flowers (Joe Pye, tall cosmos, snapdragons) may need stakes in wind
- Install now if wind damage occurring
- Soft ties prevent stem damage

**Fertilizing**
- Optional but beneficial: weekly balanced fertilizer (10-10-10)
- Or monthly if low-maintenance approach

**Mulch**
- Keep 2-3" around all plants
- Keep 1" away from stems (prevents rot)
- Top-up monthly as it settles/decomposes

### Butterfly Puddler Maintenance

**Water Refilling**
- Every 2-3 days in cool weather
- Daily in heat waves (water evaporates fast)
- Should stay moist to saturation at sand layer

**Mosquito Check**
- If you see tiny worm-like lines in water: mosquito larvae
- Drain water, scrub vessel, refill with fresh sand/water
- Change water weekly in peak heat

**Monitoring**
- Watch for first butterfly visitors (should start appearing late May)
- Take photos of visitors + surrounding flowers
