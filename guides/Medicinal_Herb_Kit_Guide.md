## Spread Risk Cheat Sheet

**MUST BE CONTAINED (pot, buried container, or root barrier):**
- Mugwort (will take over everything)
- Stinging Nettle (spreads underground + stings you)
- Mint / Peppermint (you already know)
- Catnip (already planted, already spreading)
- Lemon Balm (aggressive seeder)
- Motherwort (self-seeds prolifically — not a runner, but babies everywhere)
- Wild Licorice (underground runners)

**WATCH CLOSELY (self-seeds but manageable):**
- St. John's Wort, Feverfew, Horehound, Valerian, Chicory, Yarrow, Mullein, Borage

**WELL-BEHAVED:**
- Lavender, Rosemary, Sage, Echinacea, Marshmallow, Skullcap, Elderberry, Ashwagandha, Holy Basil, Angelica

## Motherwort Deep Dive — Since You Love It

### Growing
- **When to start:** Direct sow outdoors after last frost (mid-April) OR start indoors now
- **Germination:** 10-21 days. Needs light to germinate — press seeds onto soil surface, don't cover
- **Spacing:** 12-18" apart
- **Sun:** Full sun to part shade (afternoon shade is fine in NC heat)
- **Soil:** Average, well-drained. Not fussy.
- **Water:** Average — drought tolerant once established
- **Year 1:** Leafy growth, may not flower
- **Year 2+:** Flowers June-August, 3-5 ft tall, self-seeds

### Harvesting for Tincture
- **When:** Harvest when in FULL BLOOM (usually June-July, year 2+)
- **What:** Cut top 1/3 of flowering stems (leaves + flowers + stem)
- **Time of day:** Morning after dew dries, before peak heat
- **Tip:** Don't harvest more than 1/3 of any single plant

### Making Motherwort Tincture
**Fresh Plant Tincture (Folk Method — simplest):**
1. Harvest flowering tops on a dry morning
2. Chop coarsely — fill a clean mason jar 2/3 full (pack loosely, not tight)
3. Pour **100 proof vodka** (50% alcohol) over the herb to the very top
4. Stir/poke with chopstick to remove air bubbles
5. Cap tightly, label with date and herb name
6. Store in cool dark place, shake daily for 6 weeks
7. Strain through cheesecloth, squeeze out every drop
8. Bottle in amber glass dropper bottles
9. Label: "Motherwort Tincture — [date] — 1:2 fresh, 50% alcohol"

**Dose:** 30-60 drops (1-2 dropperfuls) in a little water, up to 3x daily. You already know what works for you from the store-bought version.

**Yield:** One quart jar of fresh herb + vodka = roughly 12-16 oz of tincture = several months' supply.

**From 50 seeds:** If even half germinate and survive, 25 plants will give you more motherwort than you can use. You'll be making tincture for friends.

### Containment
Motherwort is in the mint family but doesn't spread by runners like mint does. It self-seeds AGGRESSIVELY though. Options:
- **Deadhead** spent flowers before seeds drop (most control)
- **Grow in a contained bed** with a mulch border
- **Let it naturalize** in one area and just mow around it
- In The Gloam, a buried 5-gallon pot works well for the first year while you see how it behaves

## Priority Planting Order — Start NOW, Phase the Rest

### Phase 1: Start Indoors THIS WEEK (March 8-15)
- **Ashwagandha** — needs long warm season, start now
- **Holy Basil (Tulsi)** — start with your regular basil
- **Cayenne Pepper** — start with your other peppers
- **Toothache Plant** — frost tender, start indoors

### Phase 2: Direct Sow After Last Frost (Mid-April)
- **Motherwort** — press onto soil surface, don't cover
- **Feverfew** — surface sow
- **Fenugreek** — direct sow
- **Calendula** — direct sow (extra, for apothecary garden)
- **Chamomile** — scatter seed on prepared soil

### Phase 3: Direct Sow Late April–May
- **Mullein** — scatter on disturbed/bare soil. It LOVES poor soil.
- **St. John's Wort** — surface sow, needs light
- **Skullcap** — direct sow after frost
- **Horehound** — direct sow, full sun
- **Chicory** — direct sow
- **Burdock** — direct sow (taproot — don't transplant)
- **Marshmallow** — direct sow or start indoors
- **Angelica** — prefers to be direct sown. Give it part shade.
- **Hollyhock** — direct sow

### Phase 4: Contained Planting (May, Once Beds Are Ready)
- **Mugwort** — buried pot ONLY
- **Stinging Nettle** — buried pot, back corner, root barrier
- **Wild Licorice** — buried pot

### Phase 5: Plant as Shrub (Anytime Spring)
- **Elderberry** — pick its permanent spot carefully, it gets BIG (6-12 ft)
- Sow seeds in pots first, transplant when 6-12" tall
- OR: buy a live elderberry plant for faster results (seeds take 2-3 years to fruit)

### Phase 6: Already Growing / Just Add to Existing Plans
- Bee Balm, Echinacea, Yarrow, Valerian — can go in Wayward Meadow or Gloam anytime
- Dandelion, Plantain — just stop pulling them from the areas you want them

### Skip for 2026 (Store Seeds)
- Extra catnip, mint, peppermint, lemon balm, hyssop, anise, lavender, rosemary, sage, dill, marigold — you already have these covered
